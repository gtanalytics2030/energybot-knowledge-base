## CHUNK 15 — Rate HTP-IV (HT Night Hours Supply)

**DOCUMENT:** Tariff Schedule for FY 2026-27 — AspenPark Infra Vadodara Private Limited (AIVPL)
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Tariff Schedule
**HIERARCHY:** Part II — High Tension and Extra High Tension Tariffs — Rate HTP-IV
**PROVISION:** Rate HTP-IV, Clauses 14.1 to 14.8.2 and Notes 1–7
**TITLE:** Rate HTP-IV — HT Night Hours Supply (10:00 PM to 6:00 AM, 100 kVA and above)
**SUMMARY:** Rate HTP-IV is an optional concessional tariff for HT consumers (100 kVA and above) who use electricity exclusively during night hours from 10:00 PM to 6:00 AM. Demand charges are one-third of HTP-I demand charges. Energy charges are a flat 225 paise/unit. Up to 15% of contracted demand and up to 10% of total units consumed during the billing period may be used beyond night hours. Violation of the 15% demand condition results in billing at full HTP-I demand rates for that month; violation of the 10% energy condition results in billing all energy at HTP-I energy rates. Consumers may switch between HTP-I and HTP-IV up to four times per calendar year on 15 days' advance written notice.
**KEY TOPICS:** HTP-IV, night hours tariff, concessional tariff, night shift industry, demand charges, energy charges, 10 PM to 6 AM, deviation conditions, HTP-I fallback billing, switching option, 4 times per year, advance notice, power factor penalty, power factor rebate, HV/EHV rebate

**TEXT:**

**14. RATE: HTP-IV**

This tariff shall be applicable for supply of electricity to HT consumers opting to use electricity exclusively during night hours from 10.00 PM to 06.00 AM next day and contracted for regular power supply of 100 kVA and above.

**14.1. DEMAND CHARGES:**

| Charge Head | Rate |
|---|---|
| 1/3rd of the Fixed Charges specified in Rate HTP-I above | (1/3rd of HTP-I demand charges as per para 11.1) |

**14.2. ENERGY CHARGES:**

| Charge Head | Rate |
|---|---|
| For all units consumed during the month | 225 Paise per unit |

**14.3. Billing Demand**
**14.4. Minimum Bill**
**14.5. Maximum demand and its measurement** — Same as HTP-I Tariff
**14.6. Contract Demand** — Same as HTP-I
**14.7. Rebate for supply at HV and EHV** — Same as HTP-I

**14.8. POWER FACTOR ADJUSTMENT CHARGES:**

14.8.1. Penalty for poor Power Factor:
(a) The power factor adjustment charges shall be levied at the rate of 1% on the total amount of electricity bills for the month under the head "Energy Charges", arrived at using tariff as per para 14.2 of this schedule, for every 1% drop or part thereof in the average power factor during the month below 90% up to 85%.
(b) In addition to the above clause, for every 1% drop or part thereof in average power factor during the month below 85% at the rate of 2% on the total amount of electricity bill for that month under the head "Energy Charges", arrived at using tariff as per para 14.2 of this schedule, will be charged.

14.8.2. Power Factor Rebate:
If the power factor of the consumer's installation in any month is above 95%, the consumer will be entitled to a rebate at the rate of 0.5% (half percent) in excess of 95% power factor on the total amount of electricity bill for that month under the head "Energy Charges", arrived at using tariff as per para 14.2 of this schedule, for every 1% rise or part thereof in the average power factor during the month above 95%.

**NOTE:**
1. 15% of the contracted demand can be availed beyond the night hours prescribed as per para 14 above.
2. 10% of total units consumed during the billing period can be availed beyond the night hours prescribed as per para 14 above.
3. In case the consumer failed to observe condition no. 1 above during any of the billing month, then demand charge during the relevant billing month shall be billed as per HTP-I category demand charge rates given in para 11.1 of this schedule.
4. In case the consumer failed to observe condition no. 2 above during any of the billing month, then entire energy consumption during the relevant billing month shall be billed as per HTP-I category energy charge rates given in para 11.2 of this schedule.
5. In case the consumer failed to observe above condition no. 1 and 2 both during any of the billing month, then demand charge and entire energy consumption during the relevant billing month shall be billed as per HTP-I category demand charge and energy charge rates given in para 11.1 and 11.2 respectively, of this schedule.
6. This tariff shall be applicable if the consumer so opts to be charged in place of HTP-I tariff by using electricity exclusively during night hours as above.
7. This option can be exercised to shift from HTP-I tariff category to HTP-IV tariff or from HTP-IV tariff category to HTP-I tariff four times in a calendar year by giving not less than 15 days' advance notice in writing before commencement of billing period.

**TITLE:** ⭐ Power Factor Adjustment Charges — Standard Provisions Applicable to All HT/EHT Categories
**SUMMARY:** This anchor chunk consolidates the uniform power factor penalty and rebate framework applicable across all HT and EHT tariff categories (HTP-I through Railway Traction and HT EV). The penalty structure is: 1% of energy charges per 1% drop below 90% power factor (down to 85%), and 2% per 1% drop below 85%. The rebate is 0.5% per 1% rise above 95% power factor. In each case the penalty/rebate is calculated on the monthly energy charge amount under the respective tariff's energy charge provision. General Clause 14 makes clear that payment of a power factor penalty does not exempt the consumer from taking steps to improve their power factor.
**KEY TOPICS:** Power factor, power factor penalty, power factor adjustment charge, power factor rebate, 90%, 85%, 95%, energy charges, HT consumers, EHT consumers, PFAC, poor power factor, reactive power

**TEXT:**

### ⭐ POWER FACTOR ADJUSTMENT — STANDARD FRAMEWORK (HTP-I through HT EV)

The following framework applies uniformly across all HT/EHT tariff categories. References to the specific energy charge paragraph (e.g., para 11.2, 12.2, 13.2, etc.) refer to the energy charge provision of the applicable rate category.

**PENALTY FOR POOR POWER FACTOR:**

| Power Factor Band | Penalty Rate |
|---|---|
| Below 90% up to 85% | 1% on total monthly energy charges for every 1% drop (or part thereof) below 90% |
| Below 85% | Additional 2% on total monthly energy charges for every 1% drop (or part thereof) below 85% |

**POWER FACTOR REBATE:**

| Power Factor Level | Rebate Rate |
|---|---|
| Above 95% | 0.5% on total monthly energy charges for every 1% rise (or part thereof) above 95% |

**Calculation base:** The total amount of electricity bills for the month under the head "Energy Charges" arrived at using the tariff as per the energy charge provision of the respective rate category.

**Important:** As per General Clause 14, payment of power factor penalty does not exempt the consumer from taking steps to improve the power factor to levels specified under the Electricity Act, 2003 regulations. The licensee is entitled to take any other action deemed necessary and authorised under the Act.

**Applicable to:** Rate HTP-I (para 11.6), Rate HTP-II (para 12.10), Rate HTP-III (para 13.9), Rate HTP-IV (para 14.8), Rate HTP-V (para 15.8), Rate Railway Traction (para 16.8). Also applicable by cross-reference to Rate HT EV.

5. Bank Guarantee to be maintained valid at all times; renewed at least 1 month before expiry; revised if contracted/sanctioned load is increased during the year.
