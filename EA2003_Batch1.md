## CHUNK EA-00: Preamble and Enactment

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Act of Parliament (Central)
**HIERARCHY:** Preamble
**PROVISION:** Preamble — The Electricity Act, 2003 [No. 36 of 2003]
**TITLE:** Long Title and Preamble
**SUMMARY:** The Electricity Act, 2003 is a central legislation enacted to consolidate laws relating to generation, transmission, distribution, trading and use of electricity. Its primary objectives include developing the electricity industry, promoting competition, protecting consumer interests, rationalizing tariffs, ensuring transparent subsidy policies, promoting efficient and environmentally benign policies, and establishing the Central Electricity Authority, Regulatory Commissions and an Appellate Tribunal for Electricity.
**KEY TOPICS:** Electricity Act 2003, generation, transmission, distribution, trading, consumer protection, tariff rationalization, subsidy transparency, Central Electricity Authority, Regulatory Commissions, Appellate Tribunal for Electricity
**TEXT:**
An Act to consolidate the laws relating to generation, transmission, distribution, trading and use of electricity and generally for taking measures conducive to development of electricity industry, promoting competition therein, protecting interest of consumers and supply of electricity to all areas, rationalization of electricity tariff, ensuring transparent policies regarding subsidies, promotion of efficient and environmentally benign policies, constitution of Central Electricity Authority, Regulatory Commissions and establishment of Appellate Tribunal and for matters connected therewith or incidental thereto.

Be it enacted by Parliament in the Fifty-fourth Year of the Republic of India as follows.

The Act received Presidential assent on 26th May, 2003 and was published on 2nd June, 2003 (Jyaistha 12, 1925 Saka).
**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 110 (Appellate Tribunal), Section 70 (Central Electricity Authority), Section 76 (Central Commission), Section 82 (State Commission)

(1) This Act may be called the Electricity Act, 2003.
(2) It extends to the whole of India except the State of Jammu and Kashmir.
(3) It shall come into force on such date as the Central Government may, by notification, appoint:

## CHUNK EA-03: Section 3 — National Electricity Policy and Plan

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Act of Parliament (Central)
**HIERARCHY:** Part II — National Electricity Policy and Plan
**PROVISION:** Section 3
**TITLE:** National Electricity Policy and Plan
**SUMMARY:** Section 3 requires the Central Government to prepare and periodically revise the National Electricity Policy and tariff policy in consultation with State Governments and the Authority. The Central Electricity Authority is mandated to prepare a National Electricity Plan once every five years consistent with the National Electricity Policy, publish a draft for public comment, and notify the plan after Central Government approval.
**KEY TOPICS:** National Electricity Policy, tariff policy, National Electricity Plan, Central Electricity Authority, Central Government, State Government, renewable energy, hydro, coal, natural gas, nuclear, five-year plan
**TEXT:**
Section 3. (National Electricity Policy and Plan) —

(1) The Central Government shall, from time to time, prepare the National Electricity Policy and tariff policy, in consultation with the State Governments and the Authority for development of the power system based on optimal utilisation of resources such as coal, natural gas, nuclear substances or materials, hydro and renewable sources of energy.

(2) The Central Government shall publish National Electricity Policy and tariff policy from time to time.

(3) The Central Government may, from time to time, in consultation with the State Governments and the Authority, review or revise, the National Electricity Policy and tariff policy referred to in sub-section (1).

(4) The Authority shall prepare a National Electricity Plan in accordance with the National Electricity Policy and notify such plan once in five years:

Provided that the Authority while preparing the National Electricity Plan shall publish the draft National Electricity Plan and invite suggestions and objections thereon from licensees, generating companies and the public within such time as may be prescribed:

Provided further that the Authority shall —
(a) notify the plan after obtaining the approval of the Central Government;
(b) revise the plan incorporating therein the directions, if any, given by the Central Government while granting approval under clause (a).

(5) The Authority may review or revise the National Electricity Plan in accordance with the National Electricity Policy.
**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 70 (Authority), Section 61 (tariff policy), Section 73 (functions of Authority)

## CHUNK EA-04: Sections 4, 5 and 6 — National Policies on Rural Areas

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Act of Parliament (Central)
**HIERARCHY:** Part II — National Electricity Policy and Plan
**PROVISION:** Sections 4, 5, 6
**TITLE:** National Policy on stand-alone systems, rural electrification, and joint responsibility
**SUMMARY:** These three sections together address rural electrification policy. **TEXT:**
Section 4. (National Policy on stand alone systems for rural areas and non-conventional energy systems):

The Central Government shall, after consultation with the State Governments, prepare and notify a national policy, permitting stand alone systems (including those based on renewable sources of energy and other non-conventional sources of energy) for rural areas.

Section 5. (National policy on electrification and local distribution in rural areas):

The Central Government shall also formulate a national policy, in consultation with the State Governments and the State Commissions, for rural electrification and for bulk purchase of power and management of local distribution in rural areas through Panchayat Institutions, users' associations, co-operative societies, non-Governmental organisations or franchisees.

Section 6. (Joint responsibility of State Government and Central Government in rural electrification.) [Substituted by Act 26 of 2007, Sec. 2, w.e.f. 15th June 2007]:

## CHUNK EA-08: Section 8 — Hydro-electric Generation

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Act of Parliament (Central)
**HIERARCHY:** Part III — Generation of Electricity
**PROVISION:** Section 8
**TITLE:** Hydro-electric generation
**SUMMARY:** Section 8 requires any generating company intending to set up a hydro-generating station (with capital expenditure exceeding a notified threshold) to obtain the concurrence of the Central Electricity Authority. The Authority must evaluate whether the proposed river works affect optimal development of the river for power, drinking water, irrigation, navigation and flood control, and whether the scheme meets dam design and safety norms.
**KEY TOPICS:** hydro-electric generation, hydro generating station, Central Electricity Authority concurrence, river works, dam safety, capital expenditure threshold, multi-purpose scheme
**TEXT:**
Section 8. (Hydro-electric generation) —

(1) Notwithstanding anything contained in section 7, any generating company intending to set-up a hydro-generating station shall prepare and submit to the Authority for its concurrence, a scheme estimated to involve a capital expenditure exceeding such sum, as may be fixed by the Central Government, from time to time, by notification.

(2) The Authority shall, before concurring in any scheme submitted to it under sub-section (1) have particular regard to, whether or not in its opinion —
(a) the proposed river-works will prejudice the prospects for the best ultimate development of the river or its tributaries for power generation, consistent with the requirements of drinking water, irrigation, navigation, flood-control, or other public purposes, and for this purpose the Authority shall satisfy itself, after consultation with the State Government, the Central Government, or such other agencies as it may deem appropriate, that an adequate study has been made of the optimum location of dams and other river-works;
(b) the proposed scheme meets the norms regarding dam design and safety.

(3) Where a multi-purpose scheme for the development of any river in any region is in operation, the State Government and the generating company shall co-ordinate their activities with the activities of the person responsible for such scheme in so far as they are inter-related.
**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 7 (licence exemption for generation), Section 73 (functions of Authority)

## CHUNK EA-09: Section 9 — Captive Generation

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Act of Parliament (Central)
**HIERARCHY:** Part III — Generation of Electricity
**PROVISION:** Section 9
**TITLE:** Captive generation
**SUMMARY:** Section 9 grants any person the right to construct, maintain and operate a captive generating plant and dedicated transmission lines without a licence. Supply from a captive plant through the grid is regulated like a generating company. A second proviso (inserted by Act 26 of 2007) exempts captive plants from needing a licence to supply electricity to any licensee or consumer subject to State Commission regulations. Captive plant owners have a statutory right of open access for carrying electricity to their own use, subject to availability of transmission capacity determined by CTU/STU, with disputes settled by the Appropriate Commission.
**KEY TOPICS:** captive generating plant, dedicated transmission lines, open access, captive generation, licence exemption, Central Transmission Utility, State Transmission Utility, Appropriate Commission, section 42
**TEXT:**
Section 9. (Captive generation):

(1) Notwithstanding anything contained in this Act, a person may construct, maintain or operate a captive generating plant and dedicated transmission lines:

Provided that the supply of electricity from the captive generating plant through the grid shall be regulated in the same manner as the generating station of a generating company.

Provided further that no licence shall be required under this Act for supply of electricity generated from a captive generating plant to any licencee in accordance with the provisions of this Act and the rules and regulations made thereunder and to any consumer subject to the regulations made under sub-section (2) of section 42. [Inserted by Act 26 of 2007, Sec. 3, w.e.f. 15th June 2007]

(2) Every person, who has constructed a captive generating plant and maintains and operates such plant, shall have the right to open access for the purposes of carrying electricity from his captive generating plant to the destination of his use:

Provided that such open access shall be subject to availability of adequate transmission facility and such availability of transmission facility shall be determined by the Central Transmission Utility or the State Transmission Utility, as the case may be:

Provided further that any dispute regarding the availability of transmission facility shall be adjudicated upon by the Appropriate Commission.
**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 42(2) (open access regulations), Section 38 (Central Transmission Utility), Section 39 (State Transmission Utility), Section 76/82 (Appropriate Commission)

## CHUNK EA-10: Section 10 — Duties of Generating Companies

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Act of Parliament (Central)
**HIERARCHY:** Part III — Generation of Electricity
**PROVISION:** Section 10
**TITLE:** Duties of generating companies
**SUMMARY:** Section 10 prescribes the duties of generating companies: to establish, operate and maintain generating stations, tie-lines, sub-stations and dedicated transmission lines as per the Act and regulations. A generating company may supply electricity to any licensee and, subject to open access regulations under section 42(2), also to consumers. Every generating company must submit technical details to the Appropriate Commission and the Authority, and coordinate with CTU/STU for electricity transmission.
**KEY TOPICS:** generating company, duties, generating stations, transmission, supply to licensees, supply to consumers, open access, Appropriate Commission, Central Transmission Utility, State Transmission Utility
**TEXT:**
Section 10. (Duties of generating companies) —

(1) Subject to the provisions of this Act, the duties of a generating company shall be to establish, operate and maintain generating stations, tie-lines, sub-stations and dedicated transmission lines connected therewith in accordance with the provisions of this Act or the rules or regulations made thereunder.

(2) A generating company may supply electricity to any licensee in accordance with this Act and the rules and regulations made thereunder and may, subject to the regulations made under sub-section (2) of section 42, supply electricity to any consumer.

(3) Every generating company shall —
(a) submit technical details regarding its generating stations to the Appropriate Commission and the Authority;
(b) co-ordinate with the Central Transmission Utility or the State Transmission Utility, as the case may be, for transmission of the electricity generated by it.
**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 42(2) (open access regulations), Section 38 (Central Transmission Utility), Section 39 (State Transmission Utility), Section 76/82 (Appropriate Commission), Section 70 (Authority)

## CHUNK EA-11: Section 11 — Directions to Generating Companies in Extraordinary Circumstances

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Act of Parliament (Central)
**HIERARCHY:** Part III — Generation of Electricity
**PROVISION:** Section 11
**TITLE:** Directions to generating companies
**SUMMARY:** Section 11 empowers the Appropriate Government to direct a generating company to operate and maintain its generating station in accordance with Government directions during extraordinary circumstances (defined as threats to State security, public order, natural calamity, or other circumstances in public interest). The Appropriate Commission may offset the adverse financial impact of such directions on the generating company.
**KEY TOPICS:** generating company, extraordinary circumstances, government directions, security of State, natural calamity, public order, public interest, financial impact, Appropriate Commission
**TEXT:**
Section 11. (Directions to generating companies) —

(1) Appropriate Government may specify that a generating company shall, in extraordinary circumstances operate and maintain any generating station in accordance with the directions of that Government.

Explanation. — For the purposes of this section, the expression "extraordinary circumstances" means circumstances arising out of threat to security of the State, public order or a natural calamity or such other circumstances arising in the public interest.

(2) The Appropriate Commission may offset the adverse financial impact of the directions referred to in sub-section (1) on any generating company in such manner as it considers appropriate.
**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 76/82 (Appropriate Commission)
