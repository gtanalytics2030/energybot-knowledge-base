## CHUNK EA-126: Section 126 — Assessment for Unauthorised Use of Electricity

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Act of Parliament (Central)
**HIERARCHY:** Part XII — Investigation and Enforcement
**PROVISION:** Section 126
**TITLE:** Assessment for unauthorised use of electricity
**SUMMARY:** Section 126 empowers the assessing officer to provisionally assess electricity charges payable by any person found to be engaging in unauthorised use of electricity (UUE), based on inspection of premises, equipment, gadgets or records. The provisional order is served on the person in occupation/possession. The person may file objections; the assessing officer must pass a final assessment order within 30 days. A person accepting provisional assessment must deposit the amount within 7 days. If the period of UUE cannot be ascertained, it is limited to 12 months immediately preceding the inspection. The assessment is made at twice the applicable tariff rate. "Unauthorised use of electricity" includes use through artificial means; through unauthorised means; through a tampered meter; for a purpose other than authorised; or for premises/areas other than those for which supply was authorised.
**KEY TOPICS:** unauthorised use of electricity, UUE, assessment, provisional assessment, assessing officer, 30-day final order, 12-month limitation period, twice the tariff rate, tampered meter, artificial means, inspection, objections
**TEXT:**
Section 126. (Assessment) —

(1) If on an inspection of any place or premises or after inspection of the equipments, gadgets, machines, devices found connected or used, or after inspection of records maintained by any person, the assessing officer comes to the conclusion that such person is indulging in unauthorized use of electricity, he shall provisionally assess to the best of his judgement the electricity charges payable by such person or by any other person benefited by such use.

(2) The order of provisional assessment shall be served upon the person in occupation or possession or in charge of the place or premises in such manner as may be prescribed.

(3) The person, on whom an order has been served under sub-section (2) shall be entitled to file objections, if any, against the provisional assessment before the assessing officer, who shall, after affording a reasonable opportunity of hearing to such person, pass a final order of assessment within thirty days from the date of service of such order of provisional assessment of the electricity charges payable by such person. [Substituted by Act 26 of 2007, Sec. 11, w.e.f. 15th June 2007]

(4) Any person served with the order of provisional assessment, may, accept such assessment and deposit the assessed amount with the licensee within seven days of service of such provisional assessment order upon him.

(5) If the assessing officer reaches to the conclusion that unauthorised use of electricity has taken place, the assessment shall be made for the entire period during which such unauthorized use of electricity has taken place and if, however, the period during which such unauthorised use of electricity has taken place cannot be ascertained, such period shall be limited to a period of twelve months immediately preceding the date of inspection. [Substituted by Act 26 of 2007, Sec. 11, w.e.f. 15th June 2007]

(6) The assessment under this section shall be made at a rate equal to twice the tariff rates applicable for the relevant category of services specified in sub-section (5). [Amended by Act 26 of 2007 — "twice" substituted for "one-and-half times"]

Explanation. — For the purposes of this section —
(a) "assessing officer" means an officer of a State Government or Board or licensee, as the case may be, designated as such by the State Government;
(b) "unauthorised use of electricity" means the usage of electricity —
(i) by any artificial means; or
(ii) by a means not authorised by the concerned person or authority or licensee; or
(iii) through a tampered meter; or
(iv) for the purpose other than for which the usage of electricity was authorised; or [Substituted by Act 26 of 2007]
(v) for the premises or areas other than those for which the supply of electricity was authorized. [Substituted by Act 26 of 2007]
**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 127 (appeal), Section 135 (theft of electricity), Section 62 (tariff), Section 151 (cognizance of offences)

## CHUNK EA-127: Section 127 — Appeal Against Assessment Order

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Act of Parliament (Central)
**HIERARCHY:** Part XII — Investigation and Enforcement
**PROVISION:** Section 127
**TITLE:** Appeal to Appellate Authority against assessment order
**SUMMARY:** Section 127 allows any person aggrieved by a final assessment order under section 126 to appeal within 30 days to the Appellate Authority prescribed by the State Commission. No appeal is entertainable unless the appellant has deposited half of the assessed amount (amended from one-third by Act 26 of 2007). The Appellate Authority disposes of the appeal after hearing both parties and the order is final. No appeal lies against orders made with consent of both parties. Default in payment of assessed amount after 30 days from the assessment order attracts compound interest at 16% per annum, compounded every 6 months.
**KEY TOPICS:** appeal against assessment, Appellate Authority, 30-day appeal period, half deposit of assessed amount, final order, consent order, 16% compound interest, 6-monthly compounding, State Commission
**TEXT:**
Section 127. (Appeal to Appellate Authority) —

(1) Any person aggrieved by the final order made under section 126 may, within thirty days of the said order, prefer an appeal in such form, verified in such manner and be accompanied by such fee as may be specified by the State Commission, to an appellate authority as may be prescribed.

(2) No appeal against an order of assessment under sub-section (1) shall be entertained unless an amount equal to half of the assessed amount is deposited in cash or by way of bank draft with the licensee and documentary evidence of such deposit has been enclosed along with the appeal. [Substituted by Act 26 of 2007, Sec. 12 — "half" for "one third"]

(3) The appellate authority referred to in sub-section (1) shall dispose of the appeal after hearing the parties and pass appropriate order and send copy of the order to the assessing officer and the appellant.

(4) The order of the appellate authority referred to in sub-section (1) passed under sub-section (3) shall be final.

(5) No appeal shall lie to the appellate authority referred to in sub-section (1) against the final order made with the consent of the parties.

(6) When a person defaults in making payment of assessed amount, he, in addition to the assessed amount shall be liable to pay, on the expiry of thirty days from the date of order of assessment, an amount of interest at the rate of sixteen per cent, per annum compounded every six months.
**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 126 (assessment), Section 111 (Appellate Tribunal — separate from this Appellate Authority)

## CHUNK EA-135: Section 135 — Theft of Electricity

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Act of Parliament (Central)
**HIERARCHY:** Part XIV — Offences and Penalties
**PROVISION:** Section 135
**TITLE:** Theft of Electricity
**SUMMARY:** Section 135 (as substituted by Act 26 of 2007) penalises dishonest abstraction, consumption or use of electricity by: tapping or connecting to overhead/underground/underwater lines without authorisation; tampering with meters or installing current-reversing transformers/loop connections; damaging/destroying electric meters or apparatus; using electricity through a tampered meter; or using electricity for a purpose other than authorised. Punishment is imprisonment up to 3 years or fine or both. Minimum fines apply: for load up to 10 KW — at least 3× financial gain (6× for second/subsequent conviction); for load above 10 KW — minimum fine is 3× financial gain (second/subsequent conviction: imprisonment 6 months–5 years plus fine of 6× financial gain). Second/subsequent conviction where load exceeds 10 KW results in prohibition from electricity supply for 3 months to 2 years from any source. If artificial means exist for abstraction, it is presumed (until contrary is proved) that abstraction was dishonestly caused by the consumer. A licensee may immediately disconnect supply on detection of theft; must lodge a police complaint within 24 hours; and must restore supply within 48 hours of deposit/payment of assessed amount. An authorised officer may enter, search, seize equipment and books of account; domestic premises cannot be searched between sunset and sunrise except in presence of an adult male member.
**KEY TOPICS:** theft of electricity, tampering meter, tapping lines, current-reversing transformer, loop connection, minimum fine, 3× financial gain, 6× financial gain, imprisonment, 10 KW threshold, prohibited from supply, immediate disconnection, police complaint 24 hours, restoration 48 hours, search and seizure, domestic premises restriction
**TEXT:**
Section 135. (Theft of Electricity) — [Substituted by Act 26 of 2007, Sec. 13, w.e.f. 15th June 2007]

(1) Whoever, dishonestly —
(a) taps, makes or causes to be made any connection with overhead, underground or under water lines or cables, or service wires, or service facilities of a licensee or supplier as the case may be; or
(b) tampers a meter, installs or uses a tampered meter, current reversing transformer, loop connection or any other device or method which interferes with accurate or proper registration, calibration or metering of electric current or otherwise results in a manner whereby electricity is stolen or wasted; or
(c) damages or destroys an electric meter, apparatus, equipment, or wire or causes or allows any of them to be so damaged or destroyed as to interfere with the proper or accurate metering of electricity,
(d) uses electricity through a tampered meter; or
(e) uses electricity for the purpose other than for which the usage of electricity was authorised,

so as to abstract or consume or use electricity shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years or with fine or with both:

Provided that in a case where the load abstracted, consumed, or used or attempted abstraction or attempted consumption or attempted use —
(i) does not exceed 10 kilowatt, the fine imposed on first conviction shall not be less than three times the financial gain on account of such theft of electricity and in the event of second or subsequent conviction the fine imposed shall not be less than six times the financial gain on account of such theft of electricity;
(ii) exceeds 10 kilowatt, the fine imposed on first conviction shall not be less than three times the financial gain on account of such theft of electricity and in the event of second or subsequent conviction, the sentence shall be imprisonment for a term not less than six months, but which may extend to five years and with fine not less than six times the financial gain on account of such theft of electricity:

Provided further that in the event of second and subsequent conviction of a person where the load abstracted, consumed, or used or attempted abstraction or attempted consumption or attempted use exceeds 10 kilowatt, such person shall also be debarred from getting any supply of electricity for a period which shall not be less than three months but may extend to two years and shall also be debarred from getting supply of electricity for that period from any other source or generating station:

Provided also that if it is proved that any artificial means or means not authorized by the Board or licensee or supplier, as the case may be, exist for the abstraction, consumption or use of electricity by the consumer, it shall be presumed, until the contrary is proved, that any abstraction, consumption or use of electricity has been dishonestly caused by such consumer.

(1A) Without prejudice to the provisions of this Act, the licensee or supplier, as the case may be, may, upon detection of such theft of electricity, immediately disconnect the supply of electricity:

Provided that only such officer of the licensee or supplier, as authorized for the purpose by the Appropriate Commission or any other officer of the licensee or supplier of the rank higher than the rank so authorised shall disconnect the supply line of electricity:

Provided further that such officer of the licensee or supplier shall lodge a complaint in writing relating to the commission of such offence in police station having jurisdiction within twenty four hours from the time of such disconnection:

Provided also that the licensee or supplier, on deposit or payment of the assessed amount or electricity charges in accordance with the provisions of this Act, shall, without prejudice to the obligation to lodge the complaint, restore the supply line of electricity within forty-eight hours of such deposit or payment.

(2) Any officer of the licensee or supplier authorised in this behalf by the State Government may —
(a) enter, inspect, break open and search any place or premises in which he has reason to believe that electricity has been or is being used unauthorisedly;
(b) search, seize and remove all such devices, instruments, wires and any other facilitator or article which has been, or is being, used for unauthorized use of electricity;
(c) examine or seize any books of account or documents which in his opinion shall be useful for or relevant to, any proceedings in respect of the offence under sub-section (1) and allow the person from whose custody such books of account or documents are seized to make copies thereof or take extracts therefrom in his presence.

(3) The occupant of the place of search or any person on his behalf shall remain present during the search and a list of all things seized in the course of such search shall be prepared and delivered to such occupant or person who shall sign the list:

Provided that no inspection, search and seizure of any domestic places or domestic premises shall be carried out between sunset and sunrise except in the presence of an adult male member occupying such premises.

(4) The provisions of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, relating to search and seizure shall apply, as far as may be, to searches and seizure under this Act.
**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 126 (assessment for UUE), Section 127 (appeal), Section 136 (theft of materials), Section 151 (cognizance), Section 152 (compounding), Section 153 (Special Courts)

## CHUNK EA-136-143: Sections 136–143 — Other Offences and Penalties

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Act of Parliament (Central)
**HIERARCHY:** Part XIV — Offences and Penalties
**PROVISION:** Sections 136, 137, 138, 140, 141, 142, 143
**TITLE:** Theft of electric materials; Receiving stolen property; Interference with meters; Intentional injury to works; Extinguishing public lamps; Non-compliance of Commission directions; Adjudicating officer
**SUMMARY:** Section 136 punishes dishonest cutting/removing/transferring electric lines, materials or meters (up to 3 years imprisonment or fine; second/subsequent offence: 6 months–5 years and minimum Rs. 10,000 fine). Section 137 punishes receiving stolen electric lines/materials (up to 3 years or fine). Section 138 penalises interference with meters or works of a licensee. Section 140 penalises intentional injury to electric works. Section 141 penalises extinguishing public lamps. Section 142 empowers the Commission to impose a penalty (up to Rs. 1 lakh for non-company, up to Rs. 1 lakh per day for continuing default) for non-compliance of Commission directions. Section 143 provides for adjudicating officers to adjudge penalties for contraventions of specified provisions.
**KEY TOPICS:** theft of electric materials, stolen property, interference with meters, intentional injury, public lamps, non-compliance of Commission directions, Rs. 1 lakh penalty, adjudicating officer, continuing default penalty
**TEXT:**
Section 136. (Theft of electric lines and materials) —

(1) Whoever, dishonestly —
(a) cuts or removes or takes away or transfers any electric line, material or meter from a tower, pole, any other installation or place without the consent of the licensee or the owner; or
(b) stores, possesses or otherwise keeps in his premises, custody or control, any electric line, material or meter without the consent of the owner; or
(c) loads, carries, or moves from one place to another any electric line, material or meter without the consent of its owner,
is said to have committed an offence of theft of electric lines and materials, and shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years or with fine or with both.

(2) If a person, having been convicted of an offence punishable under sub-section (1) is again guilty of an offence punishable under that sub-section, he shall be punishable for the second or subsequent offence for a term of imprisonment which shall not be less than six months but which may extend to five years and shall also be liable to fine which shall not be less than ten thousand rupees.

Section 137. (Punishment for receiving stolen property):

Whoever, dishonestly receives any stolen electric line or material knowing or having reasons to believe the same to be stolen property, shall be punishable with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to three years or with fine or with both.

Section 138. (Interference with meters or works of licensee):

(1) Whoever —
(a) removes, alters, defaces, damages or destroys any meter, or in any way alters the index of any meter, or prevents any meter from duly registering; or
(b) alters, in any way, the condition of any wires or apparatus belonging to any licensee; or
(c) unauthorisedly breaks or unfastens any seal placed upon any meter or other apparatus, shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years, or with fine, or with both.

Section 140. (Penalty for intentionally injuring works):

Whoever, with intent to cut off the supply of electricity, intentionally injures any works or part thereof, shall be punishable with fine not exceeding ten thousand rupees.

Section 141. (Extinguishing public lamps):

Whoever wilfully extinguishes any public lamp shall be punishable with a fine which may extend to two thousand rupees.

Section 142. (Punishment for non-compliance of directions by Appropriate Commission):

Subject to the provisions of this Act, if any person fails to comply with any order or direction given under this Act by the Appropriate Commission within such time as specified by the Appropriate Commission, he shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to one year or with fine which may extend to one lakh rupees, or with both in respect of each offence, and if the failure is a continuing one, with a further fine which may extend to six thousand rupees for every day after the first during which the failure continues.

Section 143. (Power to adjudicate) —

(1) For the purpose of adjudging the penalty referred to in section 142, the Appropriate Commission shall appoint any officer not below the rank of a Secretary to the Government as the adjudicating officer for holding an inquiry in the prescribed manner after giving any person concerned a reasonable opportunity of being heard for the purpose of imposing any penalty.
**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 135 (theft of electricity), Section 151 (cognizance), Section 152 (compounding), Section 126 (assessment), Section 111 (Appellate Tribunal)

## CHUNK EA-149-158: Sections 149–158 — Offences by Companies, Abetment, Cognizance, Compounding, Special Courts

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Act of Parliament (Central)
**HIERARCHY:** Part XIV–Part XV — Offences and Penalties; Special Courts
**PROVISION:** Sections 149, 150, 151, 151A, 151B, 152, 153, 154, 155, 156, 157, 158
**TITLE:** Corporate liability; Abetment; Cognizance; Compounding; Special Courts; Arbitration
**SUMMARY:** Section 149 holds every person in charge of a company guilty when the company commits an offence, unless they prove absence of knowledge or due diligence. Directors/officers knowingly conniving are separately liable. Section 150 penalises abetment of any offence under the Act; officers/employees of a licensee/Board who connive at theft are punishable with up to 3 years or fine; electrical contractor/supervisor licences may be cancelled for abetment. Sections 151, 151A, 151B provide that offences under sections 135–140 and 150 are cognizable and non-bailable, police may investigate under Chapter XII CrPC, and cognizance may be on written complaint of the Government/Commission/licensee or on police report. Section 152 provides for compounding of theft offences by payment of specified amounts (Industrial: Rs. 20,000/KW or KVA; Commercial: Rs. 10,000; Agricultural: Rs. 2,000; Others: Rs. 4,000), with compounding allowed only once per person. Section 153 allows State Governments to constitute Special Courts for offences under sections 135–140 and 150. Section 158 provides for arbitration of any matter directed to be determined by arbitration under the Act through the Appropriate Commission's nominee per the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996.
**KEY TOPICS:** offences by companies, corporate liability, director liability, abetment, theft connivance, cognizable offence, non-bailable offence, police investigation, compounding of offences, Industrial Rs. 20000/KW, Commercial Rs. 10000/KW, Agricultural Rs. 2000/KW, Special Courts, speedy trial, arbitration
**TEXT:**
Section 149. (Offences by companies) —

(1) Where an offence under this Act has been committed by a company, every person who at the time the offence was committed was in charge of and was responsible to the company for the conduct of the business of the company, as well as the company shall be deemed to be guilty of having committed the offence and shall be liable to be proceeded against and punished accordingly:
Provided that nothing contained in this sub-section shall render any such person liable to any punishment if he proves that the offence was committed without his knowledge or that he had exercised all due diligence to prevent the commission of such offence.

(2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (1), where an offence under this Act has been committed by a company and it is proved that the offence has been committed with the consent or connivance of or is attributable to any neglect on the part of any director, manager, secretary or other officer of the company, such director, manager, secretary or other officer shall also be deemed to be guilty of having committed such offence and shall be liable to be proceeded against and punished accordingly.

Explanation. — "company" means a body corporate and includes a firm or other association of individuals; "director", in relation to a firm, means a partner in the firm.

Section 150. (Abetment) —

(1) Whoever abets an offence punishable under this Act, shall, notwithstanding anything contained in the Indian Penal Code, be punished with the punishment provided for the offence.

(2) Without prejudice to any penalty or fine which may be imposed or prosecution proceeding which may be initiated under this Act or any other law for the time being in force, if any officer or other employee of the Board or the licensee enters into or acquiesces in any agreement to do, abstains from doing, permits, conceals or connives at any act or thing whereby any theft of electricity is committed, he shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years, or with fine, or with both.

(3) The licence or certificate of competency or permit or such other authorisation issued under the rules made or deemed to have been made under this Act to any person who acting as an electrical contractor, supervisor or worker abets the commission of an offence punishable under sub-section (1) of section 135, sub-section (1) of section 136, section 137, or section 138, on his conviction for such abetment, may also be cancelled by the licensing authority. [Inserted by Act 26 of 2007, Sec. 14]

Section 151. (Cognizance of offences):

No court shall take cognizance of an offence punishable under this Act except upon a complaint in writing made by Appropriate Government or Appropriate Commission or any of their officer authorized by them or a Chief Electrical Inspector or an Electrical Inspector or licensee or the generating company, as the case may be, for this purpose.

Provided that the court may also take cognizance of an offence punishable under this Act upon a report of a police officer filed under section 173 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. [Inserted by Act 26 of 2007, Sec. 15]

Provided further that a special court constituted under section 153 shall be competent to take cognizance of an offence without the accused being committed to it for trial.

Section 151A. For the purposes of investigation of an offence punishable under this Act, the police officer shall have all the powers as provided in Chapter XII of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. [Inserted by Act 26 of 2007, Sec. 16]

Section 151B. Notwithstanding anything contained in the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, an offence punishable under sections 135 to 140 or section 150 shall be cognizable and non-bailable. [Inserted by Act 26 of 2007, Sec. 16]

Section 152. (Compounding of offences) —

(1) Notwithstanding anything contained in the Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, the Appropriate Government or any officer authorized by it may accept from any consumer or person who committed or who is reasonably suspected of having committed an offence of theft of electricity punishable under this Act, a sum of money by way of compounding of the offence as specified in the Table below:

TABLE — Compounding rates per Kilowatt (KW)/HP for LT supply and per KVA of contracted demand for HT:

1. Industrial Service — Twenty thousand rupees per KW/HP or KVA;
2. Commercial Service — Ten thousand rupees per KW/HP or KVA;
3. Agricultural Service — Two thousand rupees per KW/HP or KVA;
4. Other Services — Four thousand rupees per KW/HP or KVA.

Provided that the Appropriate Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, amend the rates specified in the Table above.

(2) On payment of the sum of money in accordance with sub-section (1), any person in custody in connection with that offence shall be set at liberty and no proceedings shall be instituted or continued against such consumer or person in any criminal court.

(3) The acceptance of the sum of money for compounding an offence shall be deemed to amount to an acquittal within the meaning of section 300 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973.

(4) The Compounding of an offence under sub-section (1) shall be allowed only once for any person or consumer.

Section 153. (Constitution of Special Courts):

(1) The State Government may, for the purposes of providing speedy trial of offences referred to in sections 135 to 140 and section 150, by notification in the Official Gazette, constitute as many Special Courts as may be necessary for such area or areas as may be specified in the notification.

Section 158. (Arbitration):

Where any matter is, by or under this Act, directed to be determined by arbitration, the matter shall, unless it is otherwise expressly provided in the licence of a licensee, be determined by such person or persons as the Appropriate Commission may nominate in that behalf on the application of either party; but in all other respects the arbitration shall be subject to the provisions of the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996.
**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 135 (theft), Section 136 (materials theft), Section 137 (receiving stolen), Section 138 (interference with meters), Section 111 (Appellate Tribunal), Section 126 (assessment)

## CHUNK EA-131-134: Sections 131–134 — Reorganisation of State Electricity Boards

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Act of Parliament (Central)
**HIERARCHY:** Part XIII — Reorganisation of Board
**PROVISION:** Sections 131, 132, 133, 134
**TITLE:** Vesting of Board property in State Government; Transfer scheme; Use of proceeds; Officers and employees; Compensation
**SUMMARY:** Section 131 provides that with effect from the effective date of a transfer scheme published by the State Government, all property, rights and liabilities of the State Electricity Board (SEB) vest in the State Government and are then re-vested in Government companies as State Transmission Utility, generating company, transmission licensee, or distribution licensee. Transfer to non-wholly-State-owned entities must be at fair value. The scheme is binding on all persons including third parties. Section 132 deals with use of proceeds from sale or transfer of Board assets for specified purposes including debt repayment. Section 133 addresses transfer of officers and employees of the Board to successor entities on same terms and conditions or otherwise as agreed. Section 134 deals with payment of compensation or damages on transfer.
**KEY TOPICS:** State Electricity Board reorganisation, transfer scheme, vesting of property, STU, generating company, distribution licensee, SEB unbundling, effective date, transfer to employees, compensation, fair value transfer
**TEXT:**
Section 131. (Vesting of property of Board in State Government) —

(1) With effect from the date on which a transfer scheme, prepared by the State Government to give effect to the objects and purposes of this Act, is published or such further date as may be stipulated by the State Government (the "effective date"), any property, interest in property, rights and liabilities which immediately before the effective date belonged to the State Electricity Board shall vest in the State Government on such terms as may be agreed between the State Government and the Board.

(2) Any property, interest in property, rights and liabilities vested in the State Government under sub-section (1) shall be re-vested by the State Government in a Government company or in a company or companies, in accordance with the transfer scheme so published along with such other property, interest in property, rights and liabilities of the State Government as may be stipulated in such scheme, on such terms and conditions as may be agreed between the State Government and such company or companies being State Transmission Utility or generating company or transmission licensee or distribution licensee, as the case may be:

Provided that the transfer value of any assets transferred hereunder shall be determined, as far as may be, based on the revenue potential of such assets at such terms and conditions as may be agreed between the State Government and the successor entities.

(3) Notwithstanding anything contained in this section, where —
(a) the transfer scheme involves the transfer of any property or rights to any person or undertaking not wholly owned by the State Government, the scheme shall give effect to the transfer only for fair value to be paid by the transferee to the State Government;
(b) a transaction of any description is effected in pursuance of a transfer scheme, it shall be binding on all persons including third parties and even if such persons or third parties have not consented to it.

Section 132. (Use of proceeds of sale or transfer of Board, etc.):

The proceeds of the sale or transfer of any Board property, interest in property or rights and liabilities shall be applied for meeting, inter alia, the liability of the Board towards the financial institutions, banks, or any other lender.

Section 133. (Provisions relating to officers and employees) —

(1) The State Government may, in relation to the reorganisation of the Board —
(a) provide for the transfer of officers and employees of the Board to any Government company or company or companies with benefits not less favourable to such officers and employees than those enjoyed by them immediately before such transfer;
(b) provide for the continuity of the terms and conditions of service of such officers and employees with the successor entities;
(c) provide for the conversion of any part of the outstanding borrowings of the Board into equity or preference capital of any of the successor entities.

Section 134. (Payment of compensation or damages on transfer):

Any property or rights transferred to any company or companies in pursuance of the transfer scheme under section 131 shall be subject to such compensation as the State Government may determine based on revenue potential of such assets or as may be mutually agreed between the parties.
**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 39 (STU), Section 172 (transitional provisions), Section 14 (deemed licensees), Section 185 (repeal and saving)
