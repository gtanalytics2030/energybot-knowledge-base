## CHUNK EA-159-172: Sections 159–172 — Works, Safety, Inspectors, Miscellaneous, Transitional

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Act of Parliament (Central)
**HIERARCHY:** Parts XVII–XVIII — Other Provisions; Miscellaneous
**PROVISION:** Sections 159, 160, 161, 162, 163, 164, 165, 166, 167, 168, 169, 170, 171, 172
**TITLE:** Protection of infrastructure; Safety; Accidents; Electrical Inspectors; Entry into premises; Coordination Forum; Good faith protection; Public servants; Recovery of penalties; Service of notices; Transitional provisions
**SUMMARY:** Section 159 prohibits injuring railways, highways, airports, canals, docks or piers in the course of electricity operations. Section 160 requires all operators to take reasonable precautions not to injuriously affect telegraphic/telephonic lines. Section 161 requires immediate notice to the Electrical Inspector on accidents causing or likely to cause loss of human/animal life, and the Government may order inquiry by an Electrical Inspector. Section 162 empowers the Appropriate Government to appoint Chief Electrical Inspectors and Electrical Inspectors; appeals from Inspector decisions lie to the Appropriate Government or Commission. Section 163 gives licensees the right to enter premises at reasonable times for inspection, testing, repair or removal of supply lines and meters, after notice; refusal to allow entry permits licensee to cut off supply. Section 166 requires the Central Government to establish a Coordination Forum (including CERC, CEA, generating companies, and transmission licensees) and a Forum of Regulators (all SERC Chairpersons with CERC Chairperson as head). State Governments are also required to constitute State Coordination Forums and district committees for electrification review. Section 168 protects good faith actions by Government, Tribunal, Commission officers, and assessing officers from suit/prosecution. Section 169 declares Commission and Tribunal members/officers as public servants. Section 170 allows recovery of unpaid penalties as arrears of land revenue. Section 171 prescribes service of notices by registered post or such prescribed means. Section 172 provides transitional provisions: SEB is deemed STU and licensee for 1 year; existing licences/approvals continue for 1 year; SEB undertakings to be transferred per Part XIII; State Government may suspend Act's applicability for up to 6 months.
**KEY TOPICS:** infrastructure protection, railway highway safety, accident notice, Electrical Inspector, entry into premises, Coordination Forum, Forum of Regulators, good faith protection, public servants, penalty recovery, service of notices, transitional provisions, SEB transitional period, 1-year transition, deemed STU
**TEXT:**
Section 159. (Protection of railways, highways, airports and canals):

No person shall, in the generation, transmission, distribution, supply or use of electricity, in any way injure any railway, highway, airports, tramway, canal or water-way or any dock, wharf or pier vested in or controlled by a local authority, or obstruct or interfere with the traffic on any railway, airway, tramway, canal or water-way.

Section 161. (Notice of accidents and injuries) —

(1) If any accident occurs in connection with the generation, transmission, distribution, supply or use of electricity and the accident results or is likely to have resulted in loss of human or animal life or in any injury to a human being or an animal, such person shall give notice of the occurrence and of any such loss or injury actually caused by the accident, in such form and within such time as may be prescribed, to the Electrical Inspector or such other person as aforesaid and to such other authorities as the Appropriate Government may direct.

(2) The Appropriate Government may, if it thinks fit, require any Electrical Inspector, or any other person appointed by it in this behalf, to inquire and report — (a) as to the cause of any accident affecting the safety of the public; or (b) as to the manner in which provisions of this Act or any licence, so far as those provisions affect the safety of any person, have been complied with.

Section 162. (Appointment of Chief Electrical Inspector and Electrical Inspector):

(1) The Appropriate Government may, by notification, appoint duly qualified persons to be Chief Electrical Inspector or Electrical Inspectors, who shall exercise the powers and perform the functions under this Act and exercise such other powers and perform such other functions as may be prescribed within such areas or in respect of such class of works and electric installations.

(2) In the absence of express provision to the contrary in this Act, or any rule made thereunder, an appeal shall lie from the decision of a Chief Electrical Inspector or an Electrical Inspector to the Appropriate Government or, if the Appropriate Government so directs, to an Appropriate Commission.

Section 163. (Power for licensee to enter premises):

(1) A licensee or any person duly authorised by a licence may, at any reasonable time and on informing the occupier of his intention, enter any premises to which electricity is, or has been, supplied by him for the purpose of — (a) inspecting, testing, repairing or altering the electric supply-lines, meters, fittings, works and apparatus for the supply of electricity belonging to the licensee; or (b) ascertaining the amount of electricity supplied or the electrical quantity contained in the supply; or (c) removing, where a supply of electricity is no longer required, any electric supply-lines, meters, fittings, works or apparatus belonging to the licensee.

(3) Where a consumer refuses to allow a licensee or any person authorised to enter his premises in pursuance of the provisions of sub-section (1) or sub-section (2), or when such licensee or person has so entered, refuses to allow him to perform any act which he is authorised to perform, the licensee may, after the expiry of twenty-four hours from the service of a notice in writing on the consumer, cut off the supply to the consumer for so long as such refusal or failure continues, but for no longer.

Section 166. (Coordination Forum) —

(1) The Central Government shall constitute a coordination forum consisting of the Chairperson of the Central Commission and Members thereof, the Chairperson of the Authority, representatives of generating companies and transmission licensees engaged in inter-State transmission of electricity for smooth and coordinated development of the power system in the country.

(2) The Central Government shall also constitute a forum of regulators consisting of the Chairperson of the Central Commission and Chairpersons of the State Commissions.

(3) The Chairperson of the Central Commission shall be the Chairperson of the Forum of regulators referred to in sub-section (2).

(4) The State Government shall constitute a Coordination Forum consisting of the Chairperson of the State Commission and Members thereof, representatives of the generating companies, transmission licensee and distribution licensees engaged in generation, transmission and distribution of electricity in that State for smooth and coordinated development of the power system in the State.

(5) There shall be a committee in each district to be constituted by the Appropriate Government — (a) to coordinate and review the extension of electrification in each district; (b) to review the quality of power supply and consumer satisfaction; (c) to promote energy efficiency and its conservation.

Section 168. (Protection of action taken in good faith):

No suit, prosecution or other proceeding shall lie against the Appropriate Government or Appellate Tribunal or the Appropriate Commission or any officer of Appropriate Government, or any Member, Officer or other employees of the Appellate Tribunal or any Members, officer or other employees of the Appropriate Commission or the assessing officer or any public servant for anything done or in good faith purporting to be done under this Act or the rules or regulations made thereunder.

Section 170. (Recovery of penalty payable under this Act):

Any penalty payable by a person under this Act, if not paid, may be recovered as if it were an arrear of land revenue.

Section 172. (Transitional provisions):

Notwithstanding anything to the contrary contained in this Act —
(a) a State Electricity Board constituted under the repealed laws shall be deemed to be the State Transmission Utility and a licensee under the provisions of this Act for a period of one year from the appointed date or such earlier date as the State Government may notify, and shall perform the duties and functions of the State Transmission Utility and a licensee in accordance with the provisions of this Act and rules and regulations made thereunder:
Provided that the State Government may, by notification, authorise the State Electricity Board to continue to function as the State Transmission Utility or a licensee for such further period beyond the said period of one year as may be mutually decided by the Central Government and the State Government;
(b) all licences, authorisations approvals, clearances and permissions granted under the provisions of the repealed laws may, for a period not exceeding one year from the appointed date or such earlier period as may be notified by the Appropriate Government, continue to operate as if the repealed laws were in force;
(d) the State Government may, by notification, declare that any or all the provisions contained in this Act, shall not apply in that State for such period, not exceeding six months from the appointed date.
**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 39 (STU), Section 14 (deemed licensee), Section 131 (Part XIII transfer scheme), Section 185 (repealed laws), Section 42 (consumer grievances), Section 86 (SERC functions)

## CHUNK EA-173-185: Sections 173–185 — Overriding Effect, Rule-Making Powers, Repeal and Saving

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Act of Parliament (Central)
**HIERARCHY:** Part XVIII — Miscellaneous
**PROVISION:** Sections 173, 174, 175, 176, 177, 178, 179, 180, 181, 182, 183, 184, 185
**TITLE:** Inconsistency; Overriding effect; Rule-making; Regulation-making; Repeal and saving
**SUMMARY:** Section 173 preserves the Consumer Protection Act 1986, Atomic Energy Act 1962 and Railways Act 1989 over inconsistent provisions of this Act. Section 174 gives this Act overriding effect over all other laws except as per section 173. Section 175 provides this Act is in addition to and not in derogation of other laws. Section 176 grants the Central Government broad rule-making powers covering National Electricity Plan timelines, licence conditions (capital adequacy), fees, NLDC constitution, works procedures, CEA membership, Commission terms of service, Appellate Tribunal pay, inquiry procedures, service of notices, and other prescribed matters. Section 177 grants the CEA regulation-making power for meters, connectivity, safety, grid standards, and other technical matters. Section 178 grants CERC regulation-making power covering tariff, open access, trading margins, Grid Code, quality standards, and other matters. Section 179 requires Central Government rules and CEA/CERC regulations to be laid before both Houses of Parliament for 30 days (with modification/annulment power). Section 180 grants State Governments rule-making powers for similar matters at the State level. Section 181 grants SERCs broad regulation-making powers covering licence applications, conditions, wheeling charges, open access, cross-subsidy reduction, electricity supply code, tariff determination methodology, standards of performance, and all other matters specified in the Act. Section 182 requires State Government rules and SERC regulations to be laid before the State Legislature. Section 183 empowers the Central Government to remove difficulties within 2 years of commencement. Section 184 excludes Defence/Atomic Energy/similar Central Government Ministries/Departments from the Act's application. Section 185 repeals the Indian Electricity Act 1910, Electricity (Supply) Act 1948 and Electricity Regulatory Commissions Act 1998 with savings for existing instruments, Indian Electricity Rules 1956 (until section 53 regulations are made), and State Government directives.
**KEY TOPICS:** overriding effect, Consumer Protection Act, rule-making power, Central Government rules, CEA regulations, CERC regulations, SERC regulations, laid before Parliament, State Legislature, difficulty removal, Defence exemption, repeal, Indian Electricity Act 1910, Electricity Supply Act 1948, ERCA 1998, Indian Electricity Rules 1956, saving clause
**TEXT:**
Section 173. (Inconsistency in laws):

Nothing contained in this Act or any rule or regulation made thereunder or any instrument having effect by virtue of this Act, rule or regulation shall have effect in so far as it is inconsistent with any other provisions of the Consumer Protection Act, 1986 or the Atomic Energy Act, 1962 or the Railways Act, 1989.

Section 174. (Act to have overriding effect):

Save as otherwise provided in section 173, the provisions of this Act shall have effect notwithstanding anything inconsistent therewith contained in any other law for the time being in force or in any instrument having effect by virtue of any law other than this Act.

Section 175. (Provisions of this Act to be in addition to and not in derogation of other laws):

The provisions of this Act are in addition to and not in derogation of any other law for the time being in force.

Section 177. (Powers of Authority to make regulations): The Authority may, by notification, make regulations consistent with this Act and the rules generally, covering meters, electricity supply code, safety requirements, grid standards, technical standards for construction and operation of electrical plants and electric lines, and other matters.

Section 178. (Powers of Central Commission to make regulations): The Central Commission may, by notification, make regulations consistent with this Act and the rules, covering: interconnection requirements, trading licence conditions, tariff determination methodology, Grid Code, open access charges (inter-State), trading margins, quality standards and enforcement, and all other matters specified under this Act for CERC regulation.

Section 179. (Rules and regulations to be laid before Parliament):

Every rule made by the Central Government, every regulation made by the Authority, and every regulation made by the Central Commission shall be laid, as soon as may be after it is made, before each House of the Parliament, while it is in session, for a total period of thirty days which may be comprised in one session or in two or more successive sessions, and if, before the expiry of the session immediately following, both Houses agree in making any modification in the rule or regulation or agree that the rule or regulation should not be made, the rule or regulation shall thereafter have effect only in such modified form or be of no effect.

Section 180. (Powers of State Governments to make rules): The State Government may, by notification, make rules for carrying out the provisions of this Act, covering licence fees, works, Commission terms of service, oath of office, SERC Fund application, accounts, reports, budget, assessment procedures, inquiry procedures, notices, and other prescribed matters.

Every rule made by the State Government and every regulation made by the State Commission shall be laid, as soon as may be after it is made, before each House of the State Legislature or where the Legislature consists of one House, before that House.

Section 183. (Power to remove difficulties):

(1) If any difficulty arises in giving effect to the provisions of this Act, the Central Government may, by order published, make such provisions not inconsistent with the provisions of this Act, as may appear to be necessary for removing the difficulty:
Provided that no order shall be made under this section after the expiry of two years from the date of commencement of this Act.

Section 184. (Provisions of the Act not to apply in certain cases):

The provisions of this Act shall not apply to the Ministry or Department of the Central Government dealing with Defence, Atomic Energy or such other similar Ministries or Departments or undertakings or Boards or institutions under the control of such Ministries or Departments as may be notified by the Central Government.

Section 185. (Repeal and saving) —

(1) Save as otherwise provided in this Act, the Indian Electricity Act, 1910, the Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948 and the Electricity Regulatory Commissions Act, 1998 are hereby repealed.

(2) Notwithstanding such repeal —
(a) anything done or any action taken or purported to have been done or taken including any rule, notification, inspection, order or notice made or issued or any appointment, confirmation or declaration made or any licence, permission, authorisation or exemption granted or any document or instrument executed or any direction given under the repealed laws shall, in so far as it is not inconsistent with the provisions of this Act, be deemed to have been done or taken under the corresponding provisions of this Act;
(b) the provisions contained in sections 12 to 18 of the Indian Electricity Act, 1910 and rules made thereunder shall have effect until the rules under sections 67 to 69 of this Act are made;
(c) the Indian Electricity Rules, 1956 made under section 37 of the Indian Electricity Act, 1910 as it stood before such repeal shall continue to be in force till the regulations under section 53 of this Act are made;
(d) all rules made under sub-section (1) of section 69 of the Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948 shall continue to have effect until such rules are rescinded or modified.

(3) The provisions of the enactments specified in the Schedule, not inconsistent with the provisions of this Act, shall apply to the States in which such enactments are applicable.

(4) The Central Government may, as and when considered necessary, by notification, amend the Schedule.
**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 42 (SERC open access regulations), Section 61 (tariff regulations), Section 86 (SERC functions), Section 53 (safety — Indian Electricity Rules 1956), Section 172 (transitional provisions)

## ⭐ ANCHOR CHUNK EA-A02: Open Access — Comprehensive Reference

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Synthesized Reference Chunk — Act of Parliament (Central)
**HIERARCHY:** Cross-sectional Reference
**PROVISION:** Sections 9(2), 38, 39, 40, 42, 49, 62(1)(c)
**TITLE:** Open Access — Who can seek it, charges payable, timeline, exemptions
**SUMMARY:** Open access under the Electricity Act, 2003 is the statutory right to non-discriminatory use of transmission or distribution systems. This anchor chunk consolidates all open access provisions for quick reference by RAG retrieval.
**KEY TOPICS:** open access, wheeling charges, cross-subsidy surcharge, additional surcharge, captive plant exemption, 1 MW threshold, 5-year timeline, non-discriminatory access, State Commission, CTU, STU, open access regulations
**TEXT:**

**Definition (Section 2(47)):**
Open access means the non-discriminatory provision for the use of transmission lines or distribution system or associated facilities with such lines or system by any licensee or consumer or a person engaged in generation in accordance with the regulations specified by the Appropriate Commission.

**Who has open access rights:**

1. **Captive generating plant owners (Section 9(2)):** Every person who has constructed and operates a captive generating plant has a statutory right of open access to carry electricity from the captive plant to the destination of his own use. Subject to transmission facility availability (determined by CTU/STU). No surcharge for captive plant open access carrying electricity to own use.

2. **Consumers above 1 MW (Section 42(2) proviso — inserted by Act 57 of 2003):** State Commission must, not later than 5 years from commencement of the Electricity (Amendment) Act, 2003, provide open access to all consumers requiring supply above 1 MW maximum power at any time.

3. **Any consumer permitted by State Commission (Section 42(3)–(4)):** Where the State Commission permits a consumer or class of consumers to receive supply from a generating company or licensee other than the area distribution licensee, the distribution licensee must provide wheeling as a common carrier.

**Charges for open access:**

| Charge | Payable to | Applicable When |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| Wheeling charges | Transmission/distribution licensee | Always for use of infrastructure |
| Cross-subsidy surcharge | Distribution licensee | On payment per Section 42(2); utilised to meet current cross-subsidy; progressively reduced; NOT leviable for captive plant to own use |
| Additional surcharge | Distribution licensee | Where consumer gets supply from other than area licensee, to meet distribution licensee's fixed costs (Section 42(4)) |
| Inter-State transmission charges | CTU/transmission licensee | For use of CTU/inter-State transmission system |
| Intra-State transmission charges | STU/transmission licensee | For use of STU/intra-State transmission system |

**Non-discriminatory access obligations:**
- CTU (Section 38(2)(d)): Must provide non-discriminatory open access to its inter-State transmission system.
- STU (Section 39(2)(d)): Must provide non-discriminatory open access to its intra-State transmission system.
- Transmission licensees (Section 40(c)): Same duty as STU.
- Distribution licensees (Section 42(3)): Duties of a common carrier providing non-discriminatory open access.

**Bilateral agreements (Section 49):** Consumers granted open access may enter agreements for electricity supply on agreed terms including tariff, notwithstanding the tariff determination provisions of Section 62(1)(d) (retail tariff).

**Dispute resolution:** Disputes on transmission facility availability — Appropriate Commission (Section 9(2)); differences on intervening transmission facilities — Appropriate Commission (Section 35).

**Tariff for wheeling:** Determined by the Appropriate Commission under Section 62(1)(c).
**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 2(47), Section 9(2), Section 38(2)(d), Section 39(2)(d), Section 40(c), Section 42(2)–(4), Section 49, Section 62(1)(c)
