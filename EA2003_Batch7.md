## CHUNK EA-51-54: Sections 51–54 — Distribution Licensee Other Businesses, Electricity Traders, Safety and Control of Electricity Use

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Central Legislation
**HIERARCHY:** Part VI — Distribution > Sections 51–54
**PROVISION:** Sections 51, 52, 53, 54
**TITLE:** Other Businesses of Distribution Licensees; Electricity Traders; Safety Provisions; Control of Transmission and Use

**SUMMARY:**
Section 51 permits distribution licensees to engage in other businesses for optimum asset utilisation, with revenue sharing toward wheeling charge reduction and mandatory separate accounts. Section 52 empowers the Appropriate Commission to specify eligibility criteria for electricity traders (technical, capital adequacy, credit worthiness) and their duties. Section 53 authorises the Central Electricity Authority (CEA) to specify safety measures for protection of the public and persons engaged in the electricity sector. Section 54 restricts transmission or use of electricity above 250 watts and 100 volts in streets, public assembly places, factories, or mines to authorised persons only (CTU, STU or licensees), with 7 days' prior notice to the Electrical Inspector and District Magistrate.

**KEY TOPICS:**
- Distribution licensee diversification; other businesses; asset utilisation
- Wheeling charge reduction from ancillary revenue
- Separate accounts; ring-fencing distribution business
- Electricity trader eligibility; capital adequacy; credit worthiness
- Electricity trader duties; supply and trading
- CEA safety specifications; public protection; personal injury
- Control of high-voltage electricity use; 250 watt/100 volt threshold
- Streets; factories; mines; public assembly places
- Prior notice to Electrical Inspector; 7-day notice period

**TEXT:**

**Section 51 — Other businesses of distribution licensees**

A distribution licensee may, with prior intimation to the Appropriate Commission, engage in any other business for optimum utilisation of its assets:

Provided that a proportion of the revenues derived from such business shall, as may be specified by the concerned State Commission, be utilised for reducing its charges for wheeling:

Provided further that the distribution licensee shall maintain separate accounts for each such business undertaking to ensure that distribution business neither subsidises in any way such business undertaking nor encumbers its distribution assets in any way to support such business.

Provided also that nothing contained in this section shall apply to a local authority engaged, before the commencement of this Act, in the business of distribution of electricity.

---

**Section 52 — Provisions with respect to electricity traders**

(1) Without prejudice to the provisions contained in clause (c) of section 12, the Appropriate Commission may specify the technical requirement, capital adequacy requirement and credit worthiness for being an electricity trader.

(2) Every electricity trader shall discharge such duties, in relation to supply and trading in electricity, as may be specified by the Appropriate Commission.

---

**Section 53 — Provisions relating to safety and electricity supply**

The Authority may in consultation with the State Government, specify suitable measures for —

(a) protecting the public (including the persons engaged in the generation, transmission or distribution or trading) from dangers arising from the generation, transmission or distribution or trading of electricity, or use of electricity supplied or installation, maintenance or use of any electric line or electrical plant;

(b) eliminating or reducing the risks of personal injury to any person, or damage to property of any person or interference with use of such property;

(c) prohibiting the supply or transmission of electricity except by means of a system which conforms to the specifications as may be specified;

(d) giving notice in the specified form to the Appropriate Commission and the Electrical Inspector of accidents and of failures of supply of electricity.

---

**Section 54 — Control of transmission and use of electricity**

(1) Save as otherwise exempted under this Act, no person other than the Central Transmission Utility or a State Transmission Utility, or a licensee shall transmit or use electricity at a rate exceeding two hundred and fifty watts and one hundred volts —

(a) in any street, or

(b) in any place —
  (i) in which one hundred or more persons are ordinarily likely to be assembled; or
  (ii) which is a factory within the meaning of the Factories Act, 1948 or a mine within the meaning of the Mines Act, 1952; or
  (iii) to which the State Government, by general or special order, declares the provisions of this sub-section to apply,

without giving, before the commencement of transmission or use of electricity, not less than seven days' notice in writing of his intention to the Electrical Inspector and to the District Magistrate or, in a Presidency town, the Commissioner of Police.

(2) The Appropriate Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, direct that sub-section (1) shall, with such modifications as may be specified in the notification, apply to the use of electricity in any area specified in the notification.

**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 12 (licence requirement); Section 57 (standards of performance); Factories Act 1948; Mines Act 1952

## CHUNK EA-58-60: Sections 58–60 — Different Performance Standards, Performance Information, Market Domination

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Central Legislation
**HIERARCHY:** Part VI — Distribution > Sections 58–60
**PROVISION:** Sections 58, 59, 60
**TITLE:** Different Standards of Performance; Information on Performance Levels; Market Domination

**SUMMARY:**
Section 58 allows the Appropriate Commission to specify different standards of performance for different classes of licensees. Section 59 requires every licensee to periodically furnish to the Commission information on performance levels achieved and compensation paid, which the Commission must publish at least annually. Section 60 empowers the Appropriate Commission to issue directions against anti-competitive conduct — agreements, abuse of dominant position, or combinations by licensees or generating companies that adversely affect competition in the electricity industry.

**KEY TOPICS:**
- Differentiated performance standards by class of licensee
- Licensee performance reporting obligations
- Annual publication of performance data by Commission
- Compensation paid under Section 57 — reporting
- Market domination; anti-competitive conduct
- Abuse of dominant position; adverse effect on competition
- Combinations; agreements; electricity industry competition
- Commission's power to issue directions for competition compliance

**TEXT:**

**Section 58 — Different standards of performance by licensee**

The Appropriate Commission may specify different standards under sub-section (1) of section 57 for a class or classes of licensee.

---

**Section 59 — Information with respect to levels of performance**

(1) Every licensee shall, within the period specified by the Appropriate Commission, furnish to the Commission the following information, namely —

(a) the level of performance achieved under sub-section (1) of section 57;

(b) the number of cases in which compensation was made under sub-section (2) of section 57 and the aggregate amount of the compensation.

(2) The Appropriate Commission shall at least once in every year arrange for the publication, in such form and manner as it considers appropriate, of such of the information furnished to it under sub-section (1).

---

**Section 60 — Market domination**

The Appropriate Commission may issue such directions as it considers appropriate to a licensee or a generating company if such licensee or generating company enters into any agreement or abuses its dominant position or enters into a combination which is likely to cause or causes an adverse effect on competition in electricity industry.

**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 57 (standards of performance and compensation); Section 86 (SERC functions)

## CHUNK EA-67-69: Sections 67–69 — Works in Streets, Overhead Lines, Notice to Telegraph Authority

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Central Legislation
**HIERARCHY:** Part VIII — Works > Sections 67–69
**PROVISION:** Sections 67, 68, 69
**TITLE:** Provisions as to Opening Up of Streets, Railways etc.; Overhead Lines; Notice to Telegraph Authority

**SUMMARY:**
Section 67 authorises licensees to carry out civil works in streets and public infrastructure (opening up soil, breaking up pavements, altering pipes, laying electric lines) within their licence area, subject to rules made by the Appropriate Government including restoration obligations, compensation for damage, and dispute resolution through a designated authority. Section 68 requires prior approval of the Appropriate Government before installing overhead lines, with exceptions for low-voltage (≤11 kV) single-consumer lines and within-premises lines; approval may impose conditions and can be revoked. Section 69 requires a licensee to give advance notice (submit a proposal or 10-day written notice) before laying electric lines within 10 metres of any telegraph line.

**KEY TOPICS:**
- Licensee civil works; opening streets; breaking pavement; laying electric lines
- Restoration of streets after works; compensation for damage
- Overhead line installation; prior approval of Appropriate Government
- Overhead line exemptions: 11 kV single consumer; within premises
- Conditions on overhead line approval; revocation of approval
- Telegraph authority; 10-metre proximity notice
- Prior proposal for new installations; 30-day decision period
- 10-day notice for repairs and amendments near telegraph lines

**TEXT:**

**Section 67 — Provisions as to opening up of streets, railways, etc.**

(1) A licensee may, from time to time but subject always to the terms and conditions of his licence, within his area of supply or transmission or when permitted by the terms of his licence to lay down or place electric supply lines without that area, carry out works such as —

(a) to open and break up the soil and pavement of any street, railway or tramway;
(b) to open and break up any sewer, drain or tunnel in or under any street, railway or tramway;
(c) to alter the position of any line or works or pipes, other than a main sewer pipe;
(d) to lay down and place electric lines, electrical plant and other works;
(e) to repair, alter or remove the same;
(f) to do all other acts necessary for transmission or supply of electricity.

(2) The Appropriate Government may, by rules made by it in this behalf, specify —

(a) the cases and circumstances in which the consent in writing of the owner or occupier of any street, railway or tramway, or any authority owning or having control of such street, railway or tramway, is required before any work referred to in sub-section (1) is carried out;

(b) the manner in which, and the conditions subject to which, a licensee shall carry out any works in any street, railway or tramway;

(c) compensation payable to the owner or occupier of any premises or street, railway or tramway, for any damage caused by carrying out any works under this section;

(d) the manner of disposal of any dispute arising between a licensee and such owner, occupier or authority.

---

**Section 68 — Provisions relating to Overhead lines**

(1) An overhead line shall, with prior approval of the Appropriate Government, be installed or kept installed above ground in accordance with the provisions of sub-section (2).

(2) The provisions contained in sub-section (1) shall not apply —

(a) in relation to an electric line which has a nominal voltage not exceeding 11 kilovolts and is used or intended to be used for supplying to a single consumer;

(b) in relation to so much of an electric line as is or will be within premises in the occupation or control of the person responsible for its installation; or

(c) in such other cases, as may be prescribed.

(3) The Appropriate Government shall, while granting approval under sub-section (1), impose such conditions (including conditions as to the ownership and operation of the line) as appear to it to be necessary.

(4) The Appropriate Government may vary or revoke the approval at any time after the end of such period as may be stipulated in the approval granted by it.

---

**Section 69 — Notice to telegraph authority**

(1) A licensee shall, before laying down or placing, within ten meters of any telegraph line, electric line, electrical plant or other works, not being either service lines, or electric lines or electrical plant, for the repair, renewal or amendment of existing works of which the character or position is not to be altered —

(a) submit a proposal in case of a new installation to an authority to be designated by the Central Government and such authority shall take a decision on the proposal within thirty days;

(b) give not less than ten days' notice in writing to the telegraph authority in case of repair, renewal or amendment of existing works, specifying —

  (i) the course of the works or alterations proposed;
  (ii) the manner in which the works are to be utilised;
  (iii) the amount and nature of the electricity to be transmitted;
  (iv) the extent to, and the manner in which (if at all), earth returns are to be used.

(2) Where the telegraph authority, within thirty days in case of new installation and ten days in case of repair, renewal or amendment after receipt of such proposal or notice, as the case may be, objects to the proposed works or alterations on the ground that they will interfere with any telegraph line, the licensee shall comply with requirements so specified by such authority so far as may be reasonably practicable and shall pay to the telegraph authority the reasonable expenses of taking all such precautions as may be necessary to prevent any interference with such telegraph line.

**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 53 (safety provisions); Section 162 (entry into premises)

## CHUNK EA-128-130: Sections 128–130 — Investigation by Commission, Orders for Compliance, Procedure for Directions

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Central Legislation
**HIERARCHY:** Part XI — Disputes > Sections 128–130
**PROVISION:** Sections 128, 129, 130
**TITLE:** Investigation of Licensee/Generating Company Affairs; Orders for Securing Compliance; Procedure Before Issuing Directions

**SUMMARY:**
Section 128 empowers the Appropriate Commission to direct an Investigating Authority to examine affairs of any licensee or generating company that fails to comply with licence conditions or the Act. The Investigating Authority may employ auditors and must be given access to books of account; findings are reported to the Commission. Section 129 allows the Commission to issue compliance directions where a licensee is contravening or likely to contravene licence conditions or Act provisions, with regard to loss or damage to affected persons. Section 130 prescribes the mandatory pre-direction procedure: serve notice, publish notice, and consider objections from affected parties before issuing directions under Section 129.

**KEY TOPICS:**
- Investigation of licensees and generating companies
- Investigating Authority; appointment; powers
- Access to books of account; auditor assistance
- Non-compliance with licence conditions; Act provisions
- Orders for securing compliance; likely contravention
- Loss and damage consideration before directions
- Pre-direction procedure: notice, publication, objections
- Natural justice; hearing before Commission directions
- Appropriate Commission enforcement powers

**TEXT:**

**Section 128 — Investigation of certain matters**

(1) The Appropriate Commission may, on being satisfied that a licensee has failed to comply with any of the conditions of licence or a generating company or a licensee has failed to comply with any of the provisions of this Act or rules or regulations made thereunder, at any time, by order in writing, direct any person (hereafter in this section referred to as "Investigating Authority") specified in the order to investigate the affairs of any generating company or licensee and to report to that Commission on any investigation made by such Investigating Authority:

Provided that the Investigating Authority may, wherever necessary, employ any auditor or any other person for the purpose of assisting him in any investigation under this section.

(2) Notwithstanding anything to the contrary contained in section 235 of the Companies Act, 1956, the Investigating Authority may, at any time, and shall, on being directed so to do by the Appropriate Commission, cause an inspection to be made, by one or more of his officers, of any licensee or generating company and his books of account; and the Investigating Authority shall supply to the Appropriate Commission a copy of the report on any inspection made under this sub-section.

(3) The licensee or the generating company whose affairs are investigated under this section shall be bound to produce before the Investigating Authority all such books of account, registers and other documents in his custody or power and to furnish it with such statements and information, as the Investigating Authority may require, and shall, in all other respects, co-operate with the Investigating Authority in the conduct of the investigation.

(4) The Investigating Authority shall, for the purposes of sub-sections (2) and (3), have the same powers as are vested in a civil court under the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, while trying a suit, in respect of the following matters, namely —
(a) summoning and enforcing the attendance of persons and examining them on oath;
(b) requiring the discovery and production of documents;
(c) receiving evidence on affidavits;
(d) issuing commissions for the examination of witnesses or documents.

---

**Section 129 — Orders for securing compliance**

(1) Where the Appropriate Commission, on the basis of material in its possession, is satisfied that a licensee is contravening, or is likely to contravene, any of the conditions mentioned in his licence or conditions for grant of exemption or the licensee or the generating company has contravened or is likely to contravene any of the provisions of this Act, it shall, by an order, give such directions as may be necessary for the purpose of securing compliance with that condition or provision.

(2) While giving direction under sub-section (1), the Appropriate Commission shall have due regard to the extent to which any person is likely to sustain loss or damage due to such contravention.

---

**Section 130 — Procedure for issuing directions by Appropriate Commission**

The Appropriate Commission, before issuing any direction under section 129, shall —

(a) serve notice in the manner as may be specified to the concerned licensee or the generating company;

(b) publish the notice in the manner as may be specified for the purpose of bringing the matters to the attention of persons, likely to be affected, or affected;

(c) consider suggestions and objections from the concerned licensee or generating company and the persons, likely to be affected, or affected.

**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 12 (licence); Section 14 (grant of licence); Section 16–19 (licence conditions and revocation); Section 129 (compliance directions); Companies Act 1956 s.235; Code of Civil Procedure 1908

## CHUNK EA-144-148: Sections 144–148 — Adjudication Factors, Jurisdiction Bar, Non-Compliance Penalty, Cumulative Liability, Government Works

**DOCUMENT:** The Electricity Act, 2003
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Central Legislation
**HIERARCHY:** Part XII — Offences and Penalties > Sections 144–148
**PROVISION:** Sections 144, 145, 146, 147, 148
**TITLE:** Factors for Adjudicating Officer; Bar on Civil Courts; Punishment for Non-Compliance; Penalties Cumulative; Government Works

**SUMMARY:**
Section 144 directs the adjudicating officer to consider disproportionate gain and repetitive nature of default when fixing penalty quantum under Sections 29, 33 and 43. Section 145 bars civil courts from jurisdiction over matters within the competence of the assessing officer (Section 126), appellate authority (Section 127), or adjudicating officer — no injunctions may be granted. Section 146 prescribes punishment for non-compliance with any Act order or direction: imprisonment up to 3 months or fine up to Rs. 1 lakh or both, plus Rs. 5,000 per day continuing fine after first conviction (with a savings provision excluding Section 121 directions). Section 147 clarifies that Act penalties are cumulative — in addition to compensation liability and licence revocation, not in derogation. Section 148 extends Act penal provisions to electricity supplied by or works belonging to the Appropriate Government.

**KEY TOPICS:**
- Adjudicating officer: factors for penalty — disproportionate gain; repetitive default
- Penalty under Sections 29, 33, 43
- Bar on civil court jurisdiction — assessing officer; appellate authority; adjudicating officer
- No injunction against Act proceedings
- Non-compliance with orders or directions: 3 months imprisonment; Rs. 1 lakh fine
- Continuing contravention: Rs. 5,000 per day additional fine
- Section 121 directions excluded from Section 146
- Penalties cumulative — compensation liability; licence revocation preserved
- Government works subject to penal provisions of the Act

**TEXT:**

**Section 144 — Factors to be taken into account by adjudicating officer**

While adjudicating the quantum of penalty under section 29 or section 33 or section 43, the adjudicating officer shall have due regard to the following factors, namely —

(a) the amount of disproportionate gain or unfair advantage, wherever quantifiable, made as a result of the default;
(b) the repetitive nature of the default.

---

**Section 145 — Civil courts not to have jurisdiction**

No civil court shall have jurisdiction to entertain any suit or proceeding in respect of any matter which an assessing officer referred to in section 126 or an appellate authority referred to in section 127 or the adjudicating officer appointed under this Act is empowered by or under this Act to determine and no injunction shall be granted by any court or other authority in respect of any action taken or to be taken in pursuance of any power conferred by or under this Act.

---

**Section 146 — Punishment for non-compliance of orders or directions**

Whoever, fails to comply with any order or direction given under this Act, within such time as may be specified in the said order or direction or contravenes or attempts or abets the contravention of any of the provisions of this Act or any rules or regulations made thereunder, shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three months or with fine, which may extend to one lakh rupees, or with both in respect of each offence and in the case of a continuing failure, with an additional fine which may extend to five thousand rupees for every day during which the failure continues after conviction of the first such offence:

Provided that nothing contained in this section shall apply to the orders, instructions or directions issued under section 121.

---

**Section 147 — Penalties not to affect other liabilities**

The penalties imposed under this Act shall be in addition to, and not in derogation of, any liability in respect of payment of compensation or, in the case of a licensee, the revocation of his licence which the offender may have incurred.

---

**Section 148 — Penalty where works belong to Government**

The provisions of this Act shall, so far as they are applicable, be deemed to apply also when the acts made punishable thereunder are committed in the case of electricity supplied by or of works belonging to the Appropriate Government.

**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** Section 29 (RLDC non-compliance penalty); Section 33 (SLDC non-compliance penalty); Section 43 (duty to supply penalty); Section 121 (National Electricity Policy directions — excluded from Section 146); Section 126 (UUE assessment); Section 127 (appeal against assessment); Section 136–143 (other offences)
