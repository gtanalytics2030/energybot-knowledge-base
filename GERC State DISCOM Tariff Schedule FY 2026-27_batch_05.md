## CHUNK 15 — Rate HTP-IV (HT Night Hours Supply)

**DOCUMENT:** Annexure: Tariff Schedule of Four State Owned DISCOMs (DGVCL, MGVCL, PGVCL & UGVCL) — FY 2026-27
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Tariff Schedule
**HIERARCHY:** Part II — High Tension and Extra High Tension Tariffs — Rate HTP-IV
**PROVISION:** Rate HTP-IV, Clauses 14.1 to 14.8.2 and Notes 1–7
**TITLE:** Rate HTP-IV — HT Night Hours Supply (10:00 PM to 6:00 AM, 100 kVA and above) — DGVCL, MGVCL, PGVCL, UGVCL
**ISSUING AUTHORITY:** Gujarat Electricity Regulatory Commission (GERC)
**EFFECTIVE DATE:** 1st April 2026
**SUMMARY:** Rate HTP-IV is an optional concessional tariff for HT consumers (100 kVA and above) using electricity exclusively during night hours from 10:00 PM to 6:00 AM. Demand charges are one-third of HTP-I demand charges. Energy charges are a flat 225 paise/unit. Up to 15% of contracted demand and up to 10% of total units may be consumed beyond night hours without penalty. Violation of the 15% demand condition triggers billing at full HTP-I demand rates for that month; violation of the 10% energy condition triggers billing of all energy at HTP-I energy rates. Consumers may switch between HTP-I and HTP-IV up to four times per calendar year on 15 days' advance written notice. Power factor penalty and rebate apply.
**KEY TOPICS:** HTP-IV, night hours tariff, concessional tariff, night shift industry, demand charges, energy charges, 10 PM to 6 AM, deviation conditions, HTP-I fallback billing, switching option, four times per year, advance notice, power factor penalty, power factor rebate, HV/EHV rebate

**TEXT:**

**14. RATE: HTP-IV**

This tariff shall be applicable for supply of electricity to HT consumers opting to use electricity exclusively during night hours from 10:00 PM to 06:00 AM next day and contracted for regular power supply of 100 kVA and above.

**14.1. DEMAND CHARGES:**

| Charge Head | Rate |
|---|---|
| 1/3rd of the Fixed Charges specified in Rate HTP-I above | (1/3rd of HTP-I demand charges as per para 11.1) |

**14.2. ENERGY CHARGES:**

| Charge Head | Rate |
|---|---|
| For all units consumed during the month | 225 Paise per unit |

**14.3. Billing Demand**
**14.4. Minimum Bill**
**14.5. Maximum demand and its measurement** — Same as HTP-I Tariff
**14.6. Contract Demand** — Same as HTP-I
**14.7. Rebate for supply at HV and EHV** — Same as HTP-I

**14.8. POWER FACTOR ADJUSTMENT CHARGES:**

14.8.1. Penalty for poor Power Factor:
(a) The power factor adjustment charges shall be levied at the rate of 1% on the total amount of electricity bills for the month under the head "Energy Charges", arrived at using tariff as per para 14.2 of this schedule, for every 1% drop or part thereof in the average power factor during the month below 90% up to 85%.
(b) In addition to the above clause, for every 1% drop or part thereof in average power factor during the month below 85% at the rate of 2% on the total amount of electricity bill for that month under the head "Energy Charges", arrived at using tariff as per para 14.2 of this schedule, will be charged.

14.8.2. Power Factor Rebate:
If the power factor of the consumer's installation in any month is above 95%, the consumer will be entitled to a rebate at the rate of 0.5% (half percent) in excess of 95% power factor on the total amount of electricity bill for that month under the head "Energy Charges", arrived at using tariff as per para 14.2 of this schedule, for every 1% rise or part thereof in the average power factor during the month above 95%.

**NOTE:**
1. 15% of the contracted demand can be availed beyond the night hours prescribed as per para 14 above.
2. 10% of total units consumed during the billing period can be availed beyond the night hours prescribed as per para 14 above.
3. In case the consumer failed to observe condition no. 1 above during any of the billing month, then demand charge during the relevant billing month shall be billed as per HTP-I category demand charge rates given in para 11.1 of this schedule.
4. In case the consumer failed to observe condition no. 2 above during any of the billing month, then entire energy consumption during the relevant billing month shall be billed as per HTP-I category energy charge rates given in para 11.2 of this schedule.
5. In case the consumer failed to observe above condition no. 1 and 2 both during any of the billing month, then demand charge and entire energy consumption during the relevant billing month shall be billed as per HTP-I category demand charge and energy charge rates given in para 11.1 and 11.2 respectively, of this schedule.
6. This tariff shall be applicable if the consumer so opts to be charged in place of HTP-I tariff by using electricity exclusively during night hours as above.
7. This option can be exercised to shift from HTP-I tariff category to HTP-IV tariff or from HTP-IV tariff category to HTP-I tariff four times in a calendar year by giving not less than 15 days' advance notice in writing before commencement of billing period.

**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:**

- Para 11.2 (HTP-I energy charges — fallback on violation)
- Rate HTP-I (switching option)

## CHUNK 17 — Rate Railway Traction

**DOCUMENT:** Annexure: Tariff Schedule of Four State Owned DISCOMs (DGVCL, MGVCL, PGVCL & UGVCL) — FY 2026-27
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Tariff Schedule
**HIERARCHY:** Part II — High Tension and Extra High Tension Tariffs — Rate Railway Traction
**PROVISION:** Rate Railway Traction, Clauses 16.1 to 16.8.2
**TITLE:** Rate Railway Traction — Power Supply at 132 kV/66 kV — DGVCL, MGVCL, PGVCL, UGVCL
**ISSUING AUTHORITY:** Gujarat Electricity Regulatory Commission (GERC)
**EFFECTIVE DATE:** 1st April 2026
**SUMMARY:** The Railway Traction tariff applies for power supply to railway traction at 132 kV/66 kV. Demand charges are Rs. 180/kVA/month for billing demand up to contract demand and Rs. 425/kVA/month for excess. Energy charges are 500 paise/unit. Special provisions: excess demand due to load transfer at preceding/succeeding supply points or DISCOM maintenance is charged at normal rate. Bunching of trains qualifies for normal demand charges only if the railway provides convincing documentary proof; otherwise excess demand charges apply. Power factor penalty, rebate, billing demand, minimum bill, maximum demand, contract demand, and HV/EHV rebate are same as HTP-I.
**KEY TOPICS:** Railway traction, 132 kV, 66 kV, demand charges, energy charges, load transfer, bunching of trains, excess demand, documentary proof, DISCOM, power factor penalty, power factor rebate

**TEXT:**

**16. RATE: RAILWAY TRACTION**

This tariff is applicable for power supply to Railway Traction at 132 kV/66 kV.

**16.1. DEMAND CHARGES:**

| Charge Head | Rate |
|---|---|
| (a) For billing demand up to the contract demand | Rs. 180 per kVA per month |
| (b) For billing demand in excess of contract demand | Rs. 425 per kVA per month |

**NOTE:** In case of the load transfer for traction supply due to non-availability of power supply at preceding or succeeding point of supply or maintenance at DISCOM's level, excess demand over the contract demand shall be charged at normal rate at appropriate point of supply.

Normal Demand Charges will also apply in case of bunching of trains. However, DISCOMs shall charge excess demand charges while raising the bills and Railways have to give convincing details and documentary proof of bunching of trains if they want to be charged at the normal demand charges. **16.8. POWER FACTOR ADJUSTMENT CHARGES:**
