## CHUNK 21
**TITLE:** System of Supply – Voltage Category by Load Size (Section 3, Clause 3.1–3.2)
**SOURCE:** Pages 16–17, Section 3
**CLAUSE:** 3.1, 3.2

**Clause 3.1 — Frequency & Voltage Standards:**
The licensee must supply power within the frequency band specified in:
- CERC (Indian Electricity Grid Code) Regulations, 2010
- GERC (Gujarat Electricity Grid Code) Regulations, 2013

At the point of commencement of supply, the supplier shall NOT allow voltage to vary
beyond the limit specified by the Central Electricity Authority (CEA) — unless the consumer
gives written consent OR the Commission gives prior sanction.

**Clause 3.2 — Rated AC Supply Voltage by Load:**

| Connected / Contracted Load | System of Supply |
|---|---|
| Up to 6 kW connected load (motive power load other than irrigation pump ≤ 2 HP) | 230V — Single Phase |
| Above 6 kW up to 100 kVA/kW contracted demand; OR up to 150 kVA/kW if opted by applicant/consumer | 400V — Three Phase |
| Contract Demand > 100/150 kVA/kW and up to 4000 kVA/kW. For existing 22kV consumers, limit extended up to 8000 kVA/kW (with undertaking to revert to 4000 kVA/kW limit if system changes to 11kV) | 11kV and 22kV — Three Phase |
| Contract Demand exceeding 4000 kVA/kW | 33kV and above — Three Phase |

**Consumer Options:**
- A consumer MAY opt for a HIGHER voltage profile even if their contracted load is below
  the threshold for that profile.
- Existing consumers at lower voltage who cross the threshold may be offered a commercially
  viable option to move to the next higher voltage. The offer considers:
  (a) Likely reduction in T&D losses
  (b) Load reduction on licensee's transformers
- Existing consumers at lower voltage MAY continue at that voltage; higher limits apply
  only when load increases.

**KEYWORDS:** supply voltage, 230V single phase, 400V three phase, 11kV, 22kV, 33kV,
connected load, contracted demand, 6kW, 100 kVA, 4000 kVA, voltage threshold, system of
supply, motive power, irrigation pump, higher voltage option

## CHUNK 23
**TITLE:** Power Factor – Consumer Obligations, Capacitor Requirements & Disconnection
**SOURCE:** Pages 17–18, Section 3
**CLAUSE:** 3.4, 3.5, 3.6, 3.7, 3.8

**Clause 3.4 — Mandatory Power Factor Requirement:**
- All consumers with THREE-PHASE supply must maintain average power factor ≥ 90% (0.90).
- The licensee shall NOT commence supply to any applicant requiring motive power load of
  2 kW or more unless the installation has adequate power factor corrective equipment
  (shunt capacitor).

**Clause 3.5 — How to Get a Shunt Capacitor:**
Option A (Consumer procures):
- Consumer procures own shunt capacitor (ISI standard, adequate rating) at own cost.
- Must be tested by the Distribution Licensee before installation (testing fee applicable).

Option B (Licensee installs):
- Consumer may request the licensee to install the shunt capacitor.
- Subject to payment and availability with the licensee.
- Licensee may provide guidance on suitable capacitor rating and approved suppliers.

**Clause 3.6 — Minimum Charges Still Apply:**
If supply is discontinued due to non-provision of capacitor, the consumer is STILL liable for
minimum charges — provided the licensee was ready to supply and had served notice.

**Clause 3.7 — Ownership & Maintenance of Capacitor:**
- Capacitor installed by licensee at consumer's cost becomes the CONSUMER's property.
- Consumer is responsible for maintenance.
- If damaged or missing: consumer replaces at own cost (or licensee replaces at consumer's cost
  if the adequate rating is available).
- This also applies when total motive load reaches 2 kW or more after load enhancement.

**Clause 3.8 — Disconnection for Low Power Factor:**
If an existing three-phase installation (motive power ≥ 2 kW) is found to have average
power factor below 90%, the licensee may:
- Refuse supply, OR
- Disconnect supply
...AFTER giving 10 days' notice to rectify the capacitor.

**KEYWORDS:** power factor, 90%, shunt capacitor, motive power, 2kW, three phase, ISI standard,
capacitor ownership, minimum charges, disconnection notice, 10 days notice, power factor
corrective equipment, capacitor testing fee

## CHUNK 25
**TITLE:** New Connection – Licensee's Obligation to Supply & Extend Distribution System
**SOURCE:** Pages 18–19, Section 4
**CLAUSE:** 4.1, 4.2, 4.3

**Clause 4.1 — Licensee's Obligation to Supply:**
The licensee MUST give electricity supply to any owner or occupier of premises in its area
of supply, within timelines specified in GERC (Standard of Performance) Regulations 2005,
PROVIDED:
1. Supply is technically feasible
2. Applicant follows the procedure in this Code
3. Applicant agrees to pay costs as per GERC (Miscellaneous Charges) Regulations 2005

**Clause 4.2:**
The system of supply and voltage level depends on consumer category and load, as per
Clauses 3.2 and 3.3 of this Code.

**Clause 4.3 — Licensee's Obligation to Extend Distribution System:**
- The licensee is responsible for upgrading, extending, and strengthening its distribution
  system to meet demand in its area of supply.
- Cost of:
  (a) Extension of distribution mains
  (b) Extension/upgradation of system up to the point of supply
  (c) Extension of service connection from distribution mains to point of supply
  ...shall be levied as per GERC (Miscellaneous Charges) Regulations 2005.

**KEYWORDS:** licensee obligation to supply, new connection obligation, technically feasible,
distribution system extension, Standard of Performance, Miscellaneous Charges Regulations,
area of supply, supply voltage, point of supply cost

## CHUNK 27
**TITLE:** New Connection – Purchase of Existing Property & No-Dues Certificate Rules
**SOURCE:** Pages 19–20, Section 4
**CLAUSE:** 4.6, 4.7

**Clause 4.6 — Buying a Property with Disconnected Connection:**
- The new owner must verify that the previous owner paid all dues and obtained a
  "No-Dues Certificate" from the licensee.
- If the previous owner did NOT get a No-Dues Certificate before transfer of ownership,
  the NEW owner may approach the licensee with previous connection details to get it.
- The licensee MUST within 1 month of receiving such request either:
  (a) Intimate the outstanding dues in writing, OR
  (b) Issue a No-Dues Certificate

**Clause 4.7 — Licensee's Failure to Respond in 1 Month:**
- If the licensee fails to intimate dues OR issue No-Dues Certificate within 1 month:
  → New connection to the premises CANNOT be denied on grounds of previous consumer's dues
  → The licensee must recover its dues from the previous consumer through legal means

**KEYWORDS:** no dues certificate, existing property purchase, previous owner dues, new owner
connection, 1 month timeline, outstanding dues, property transfer, disconnected connection,
previous consumer, legal recovery

**Clause 4.8 — Demolition & Reconstruction:**
When an ENTIRE premises is demolished and reconstructed:
- Existing consumer installation must be SURRENDERED
- Meter and service line must be REMOVED
- Agreement must be TERMINATED

- New connection required for reconstructed building (after clearing all old dues)

**Clause 4.9 — Which Application Form to Use:**
- LT (Low Tension) new connection → Annexure I (Form A-1)
- HT/EHT (High/Extra High Tension) new connection → Annexure II (Form A-2)

**Clause 4.10 — How to Get Application Forms:**
- Available FREE at licensee's local office
- Available FREE for download on licensee's website
- Legible photocopies of blank forms are acceptable
- Licensee must display office addresses and phone numbers for form submission on website
- Assistance available at local office for filling the form

**Clause 4.11 — Online Submission Channels:**
The licensee must provide:
- Online submission via website/web portal
- Mobile application for online submission
- Centralized 24/7 toll-free call centre (within 6 months of Amendment notification)
- Customer Relation Manager (CRM) system for unified view of all service requests,
  with SMS/email alerts for: receipt of application, completion, status changes, and
  auto-escalation if services not provided in time
- Door-step services for senior citizens

**KEYWORDS:** demolition reconstruction, security deposit, terminate agreement, Form A-1,

## CHUNK 29
**TITLE:** New Connection – Documents Required: Simplified Rule for ≤10kW & Identity Proof
**SOURCE:** Pages 21–22, Section 4
**CLAUSE:** 4.12, 4.13, 4.14, 4.15

**Clause 4.12 — Standard Document Requirements:**
Application form must be accompanied by:
- Photograph of applicant
- Identity proof
- Proof of ownership or legal occupancy of premises
- Proof of current address
- Other documents as per Clauses 4.15–4.20 for specific categories

**Special Simplified Rule for Connections up to 10 kW:**
Only TWO mandatory documents required:
1. Identity proof (as per Clause 4.15)
2. Proof of ownership/occupancy (as per Clause 4.16) OR any other address proof not
   already submitted as identity proof (if ownership/occupancy proof is unavailable)

**Clause 4.13 — Registration Fee:**
Registration-cum-processing fee is payable as per GERC (Miscellaneous Charges)
Regulations 2005.

**Clause 4.14 — HP Conversion Formula:**
- Equivalent HP = kW ÷ 0.746
- kW = kVA × 0.90 (for all equipment EXCEPT welding equipment)
- For welding equipment (rated in kVA): 1 kVA = 1 HP; fractions rounded up to next ½ HP

**Clause 4.15 — Acceptable Identity Proof (Individuals):**
Any ONE of the following:
1. Electoral identity card (Voter ID)
2. Passport
3. Driving license
4. Ration card
5. Photo identity card issued by Government agency
6. PAN card
7. Aadhar card

For companies/trusts/educational institutions/government departments:
- Form signed by competent authority (Branch Manager, Principal, Executive Engineer, etc.)
- Along with relevant resolution/authority letter of the institution

**KEYWORDS:** identity proof, documents required, 10kW simplified, two documents, voter ID,

**SOURCE:** Pages 23–24, Section 4

**Clause 4.21 — Processing Hard Copy Applications:**
- Licensee verifies application form and enclosed documents on receipt.
- If DEFICIENT: Written note issued on the spot specifying shortcomings.
- If COMPLETE: Licensee collects registration charges, uploads on website, generates
  acknowledgement with registration number, and intimates applicant immediately.

**Processing Online Applications:**
- If DEFICIENT: Licensee intimates applicant by email within 2 working days specifying
  deficiencies.
- If COMPLETE (or deficiencies removed): Licensee acknowledges receipt of online payment,
  uploads application on website, generates acknowledgement with registration number,
  and intimates applicant immediately.

**Clause 4.22 — Application Register / Database:**
- Licensee maintains permanent record of all applications in an Application Register/Database.
- Each application gets a permanent serial number in the order it was uploaded on the website.
- Separate registers/databases may be maintained for different consumer categories.
- Register must be updated with stage-wise disposal status of each application.

**Clause 4.23 — First Come, First Served:**
- Applications are processed on "first come, first served" basis within each tariff category.
- Waiting list maintained area-wise; displayed on licensee's website or office notice board,
  updated DAILY.

**Clause 4.24 — When is Application FORM deemed received:**
On the date of GENERATION of acknowledgement with registration number.

**Clause 4.25 — When is APPLICATION deemed received:**
On the date of receipt of ALL applicable charges (including security deposit).

## CHUNK 32
**TITLE:** New Connection – Site Inspection Rules & Inspection Procedure
**SOURCE:** Pages 24–25, Section 4
**CLAUSE:** 4.26, 4.27, 4.28, 4.29

**Clause 4.26 — Inspection Scheduling:**
- At the time of receiving the application form, the licensee must fix an inspection date
  in mutual consultation with the applicant (under written acknowledgment).
- Inspection date must be within:
  - 7 working days — for Class-I cities and urban areas
  - 10 working days — for rural areas
- If applicant wants inspection on a non-working day (Sunday/gazetted holiday):
  Inspection fee of Rs. 500 is payable.

**Clause 4.27 — What the Licensee Does During Inspection:**
1. Fix the point of supply and location for meter/MCB installation (in consultation
   with consumer).
   - Service line must be at accessible location.
   - Meter must be fixed OUTSIDE or at the entry point of premises, protected from rain,
     accessible without entering/unlocking premises.
   - Licensee's apparatus/meter must NOT be fixed inside private quarters.
2. Record full address, nearby landmarks, and pole number from which connection is proposed.
3. Verify all application form particulars.
4. Obtain necessary way leaves and permissions for laying service lines.
   - If service line must cross other property (consumer has no public road frontage):
     Consumer obtains way leave at own expense and maintains it as long as supply continues.
   - If way leave is withdrawn: Supply may be cut off immediately.

**Clause 4.28 — Only One Connection Per Premises:**
- Licensee will NOT provide more than one connection per premises or for
  adjoining/contiguous premises of the same owner (unless separated by a public road or
  private premises).
- For a SECOND connection: Consumer must produce separate legal entity documents
  (separate Income Tax No./Sales Tax No., ration card, rent/lease agreement).

**Clause 4.29 — Compliance Before Connection:**
If at registration or inspection stage it appears the connection may not comply with applicable
rules/standards/codes, connection is provided ONLY after necessary compliance is secured.
Licensee must help consumer in securing compliance wherever practicable.

**KEYWORDS:** site inspection, 7 working days, 10 working days, Class-I cities, rural areas,

## CHUNK 33
**TITLE:** New Connection – Dues Clearance, LT Demand Note & HT/EHT Processing Timeline
**SOURCE:** Pages 25–26, Section 4
**CLAUSE:** 4.30, 4.31, 4.32

**Clause 4.30 — No New Connection with Outstanding Dues:**
Application for new connection, reconnection, load addition/reduction, name change, or
service line shifting SHALL NOT be entertained if:
- Any dues exist for that premises, OR
- Any dues exist for the applicant for any other connection in the licensee's jurisdiction

Exception: If earlier dues were recovered from the new applicant and the licensee later
recovers them from the previous consumer, the amount shall be REFUNDED to the new
applicant (after adjusting recovery expenses).

**Clause 4.31 — LT Supply: Demand Note Timeline:**
After compliance per Clause 4.29, for LT supply applications, the licensee must sanction
the load and issue a demand note within the timeline specified in GERC (Standard of
Performance) Regulations 2005.
Note: Time taken by applicant for compliance is EXCLUDED from this timeline.

**Clause 4.32 — HT/EHT Supply: Process & Timeline:**
Step 1: Licensee forwards application to Transmission Licensee within 12 working days
        of receipt.
Step 2: Transmission Licensee responds on feasibility within 25 working days of receipt.
Step 3: Licensee communicates Transmission Licensee's comments to applicant within
        3 days of receiving the response.
Step 4: If feasible, licensee issues demand note within timeline per Standard of
        Performance Regulations 2005.
Note: Time taken by applicant for compliance per Clause 4.29 is EXCLUDED from timeline.

**KEYWORDS:** outstanding dues, no connection with arrears, LT demand note timeline,
HT EHT supply process, Transmission Licensee feasibility, 12 working days, 25 working days,
3 days communication, Standard of Performance, dues clearance, previous consumer dues,
refund of recovered dues

## CHUNK 34
**TITLE:** New Connection – Demand Note Contents, Payment Rules & Refund of Excess Amount
**SOURCE:** Pages 26–28, Section 4
**CLAUSE:** 4.33, 4.34, 4.35, 4.36

**Clause 4.33 — What a Demand Note Must Contain:**
1. Details of works to be undertaken (including service line)
   - If estimate is issued on per-kW basis: Work details need not be listed.
2. Charges payable by applicant (as per GERC Miscellaneous Charges Regulations 2005)
   - Applicant may choose to do the works himself under licensee's supervision:
     → Only SUPERVISION charges payable to licensee (no full work charges)
     → Consumer procures own materials conforming to BIS/ISI standards
     → Consumer must complete work within Standard of Performance timelines
     → Failing which: licensee issues 60-day notice; after notice period, demand charges
       and minimum monthly charges become payable
3. Amount of security deposit (as per GERC Security Deposit Regulations 2005)

**Clause 4.34 — Payment Deadline for Demand Note:**
- Applicant must pay within 30 days of demand note issue.
- If not paid in 30 days: Application LAPSES — applicant informed in writing.
- Licensee's obligation to energise arises ONLY after receipt of FULL payment.
- Applicant may request additional time in writing (within the 30-day period); licensee
  may grant it.

**Clause 4.35 — Refund of Excess Amount:**
- Excess amount refunded within 30 days of energisation — by account payee cheque or
  electronic mode.
- If not refunded in 30 days: Licensee pays interest at Delayed Payment Surcharge rate
  (as per prevailing Tariff Order).
- Consumer gets item-wise detailed bill showing estimation vs actual expenditure.
- If applicant requests further info: Licensee must furnish or give reasons for non-furnishing
  within 10 days.
- Excess expenditure (above deposit): Applicant pays within 30 days of demand — else
  debited to next electricity bill.
- If estimate is per-kW basis: No adjustment required.

**Clause 4.36 — Distribution Transformer on Premises:**
If the licensee determines that a distribution transformer is needed within the applicant's
premises, the applicant must provide a suitable room or land portion free of cost for its
installation.

**KEYWORDS:** demand note contents, work details, supervision charges, consumer self-work,
BIS ISI materials, 30 days payment, application lapse, refund of excess, 30 days refund,
delayed payment surcharge, item-wise bill, security deposit, distribution transformer on

  installed in applicant's presence.

**Clause 4.44 — Order of Laying Service Lines:**
Service lines shall, wherever possible, be laid in the ORDER OF DEPOSIT RECEIPT DATE.

**TITLE:** Temporary Supply – Demand Note, Testing, Outstanding Dues & Refund After Disconnection
**SOURCE:** Pages 29–30, Section 4
**CLAUSE:** 4.50, 4.51, 4.52, 4.53, 4.54, 4.55, 4.56

**Clause 4.50 — Demand Note for Temporary Supply:**
If found feasible: Licensee sanctions load and issues demand note within 7 days of inspection
(per procedure in Clause 4.33). Both parties follow timelines in Clauses 4.34–4.43.

**Clause 4.51 — Testing (If Satisfactory):**
Applicant + Licensed Electrical Contractor must be present. If satisfactory: Licensee issues
written acknowledgment on the spot.

**Clause 4.52 — Testing (If NOT Satisfactory):**
Applicant informed on the spot in writing of faults. After rectification, applicant informs
licensee in writing. Licensee repeats procedure per Clause 4.40.

**Clause 4.53 — Re-Testing:**
- First test: FREE; subsequent tests: FEE CHARGED.
- If satisfactory on re-test: Written acknowledgment issued on the spot.

**Clause 4.54 — Releasing Temporary Connection:**
After payment of demand note charges AND satisfactory testing, temporary connection
released by the required date shown in the application form, or as per Standard of
Performance Regulations 2005 timelines.

**Clause 4.55 — No Temporary Connection If Dues Outstanding:**
If the applicant has outstanding dues ANYWHERE in the licensee's area: Temporary
connection not given until dues are cleared.

**Clause 4.56 — Refund After Temporary Supply Period Ends:**
After disconnection of temporary supply:
- Licensee prepares final bill based on ACTUAL consumption.
- From the deposit amount, the following are DEDUCTED:
  (a) Any unpaid dues
  (b) Material charges (meter, transformer, isolator, etc.) at 1% per month
  (c) Actual erection and dismantling charges — capped at 100% of material cost
- BALANCE is refunded.
- Refund must be made within 60 days of disconnection.
- If not refunded in 60 days: Licensee pays interest at late payment surcharge rate
  (as per prevailing Tariff Order).

## CHUNK 38
**TITLE:** Temporary Supply – Extension, Date Amendment & No Right to Permanent Connection
**SOURCE:** Page 30, Section 4
**CLAUSE:** 4.57, 4.58, 4.59

**Clause 4.57 — Extending the Period of Temporary Supply:**
Consumer must apply in writing at least 7 working days before expiry of temporary supply.
Licensee may grant extension and send a demand note for advance electricity charges for the
extension period within 7 working days of receiving the written request.

**Clause 4.58 — Amending the Start Date of Temporary Supply:**
Applicant may request a change in the start date to a date not later than 90 days from the
original sanction date. Application must be submitted:
- At least 4 days before the originally sanctioned start date — for Class-I cities and
  urban areas
- At least 8 days before the originally sanctioned start date — for rural areas

**Clause 4.59 — No Right to Permanent Connection:**
Grant of a temporary connection does NOT create any right in favour of the applicant
to claim a permanent connection. Permanent connections are governed by the Act and
Regulations independently.

**KEYWORDS:** temporary supply extension, 7 working days before expiry, 90 days date change,
4 days Class-I cities, 8 days rural areas, advance charges demand note, no right to permanent

## CHUNK 41 — CRITICAL COMPARISONS (Pages 16–30)
**TITLE:** Key Distinctions – Easily Confused Rules in Sections 3 & 4 (Pages 16–30)
**SOURCE:** Pages 16–30, Sections 3 & 4 (Synthesised Reference Chunk)
**CLAUSE:** Multiple

**1. Application Form received vs Application received:**
- Application FORM received = date of generation of acknowledgement with registration number
- APPLICATION received = date of receipt of ALL applicable charges + security deposit
- (Two different dates — triggers different timelines)

**2. Temporary Connection vs Permanent Connection:**
- Temporary: up to 12 months per grant; no right to claim permanent connection
- Permanent: governed by Act and regulations independently
- Temporary does NOT automatically convert to permanent

**3. First Test vs Re-Test Fees:**
- First test of consumer installation: FREE (no fee charged)
- Subsequent re-tests (due to faults found in first test): Fee charged
- This applies equally to both new connections and temporary connections

**4. Inspection in 7 days vs 10 days:**
- Class-I cities and urban areas: 7 working days
- Rural areas: 10 working days
- These are from the date of APPLICATION FORM receipt (not application receipt)

**5. LT Demand Note vs HT/EHT Demand Note:**
- LT: Licensee issues demand note directly after compliance
- HT/EHT: Must go to Transmission Licensee first (12 days → 25 days → 3 days → then
  demand note) — much longer process

**6. Consumer Self-Work vs Licensee Work:**
- If consumer does work: Only supervision charges payable to licensee; consumer buys
  own BIS/ISI materials
- If licensee does work: Full work cost in demand note

**7. Occupier vs Owner for Connection:**
- Owner: Standard documents only
- Occupier: Standard documents + NOC from owner (or indemnity bond if NOC unavailable)
- Long-term lease holders: Exempt from owner NOC requirement

**8. 30 days vs 60 days deadlines:**
- 30 days: Payment of demand note / Refund of excess amount after energisation
- 60 days: Consumer must avail supply after licensee's intimation of work completion /
  Refund of temporary connection deposit after disconnection

**KEYWORDS:** application form received date, application received date, temporary vs permanent,
first test free re-test fee, 7 days vs 10 days inspection, LT vs HT demand note process,
owner vs occupier, 30 days vs 60 days, consumer self-work, supervision charges, comparison
