## CHUNK 01
**TITLE:** GERC Supply Code – Identity, Legal Authority & Applicability
**SOURCE:** Page 1, Short Title, Scope, Extent & Commencement (Para 1–4)
**CLAUSE:** Notification No. 4 of 2015

The GERC Electricity Supply Code and Related Matters Regulations (Notification No. 4 of 2015)
is made by the Gujarat Electricity Regulatory Commission (GERC) under powers conferred by:
- Section 181(2)(x) read with Section 50 of the Electricity Act, 2003 (Act 36 of 2003)
- Section 42(1)(b) of the Gujarat Electricity Industry (Reorganisation and Regulation) Act, 2003
  (Gujarat Act 24 of 2003)

**Full Official Name:** Gujarat Electricity Regulatory Commission (Electricity Supply Code and
Related Matters) Regulations, 2015 — referred to as "the Supply Code."

**Purpose:** Defines obligations of both the licensee and consumers towards each other and
specifies practices for providing efficient, cost-effective, and consumer-friendly electricity service.

**Applicability — This Code applies to:**
1. All Distribution Licensees (including Deemed Licensees) and all consumers in the state of Gujarat.
   For Clause 4.32 specifically, it also applies to Transmission Licensees.
2. All persons exempted under Section 13 of the Electricity Act, 2003.
3. Cases of unauthorised supply, unauthorised use, diversion, abstraction, and theft of electricity.

**Commencement:** The Code came into force on the date of its publication in the Official Gazette
of the Government of Gujarat.

**Forms & Formats:** All forms and formats annexed to the Supply Code are for guidance only.
Licensees may amend these forms/formats with prior approval of the Commission; amended
versions must be posted on the respective licensee's website.

**KEYWORDS:** Supply Code applicability, Gujarat electricity, GERC, Distribution Licensee,
Deemed Licensee, Transmission Licensee, Section 13, unauthorised use, theft, gazette notification,
Notification No 4 of 2015, Electricity Act 2003

## CHUNK 03
**TITLE:** Supply Code Review Panel – Composition and Membership
**SOURCE:** Page 7, Section 1
**CLAUSE:** 1.1, 1.2, 1.3, 1.4, 1.5

**Chairperson:** Nominated by GERC from STU (State Transmission Utility) representatives.
Must be an STU officer not below the rank of Chief Engineer.

**Member-Secretary:** Nominated by GERC from Distribution Licensee representatives.
The Distribution Licensee whose representative is Member-Secretary must:
- Provide administrative and logistic support to the Review Panel
- Bear travel expenses of consumer organization members and any GERC-nominated members
- Render secretarial assistance to the Review Panel

**Members of the Review Panel (besides Chairperson):**
a) One member from each Distribution Licensee
b) One member representing all Generating Companies (nominated by GERC)
c) Chief Electrical Inspector, Government of Gujarat — ex-officio member
d) Four Industrial Consumer representatives (nominated by GERC):
   - Must be from registered industry bodies / chambers of commerce
   - Two representatives from LT Industrial Consumers
   - Two representatives from HT/EHT Consumers
e) Two members from registered Consumer bodies representing domestic and commercial consumers
   (nominated by GERC)
f) One member from registered bodies representing Agriculture Consumers (nominated by GERC)

**Additional Members:** GERC may add further members from any sector/profession as needed.

**Tenure:** Normally 2 years. Replacement member shall be from the same category for the
unexpired period. Existing members continue until their term expires or new members are appointed,
whichever is later.

**Procedure:** Review Panel decides its own procedure for transaction of business.

**KEYWORDS:** Review Panel, members, STU, Chief Engineer, Chief Electrical Inspector,
Distribution Licensee, Generating Companies, industrial consumers, agriculture consumers,
domestic consumers, Member-Secretary, tenure, GERC nomination

## CHUNK 04
**TITLE:** Supply Code Review Panel – Functions and Responsibilities
**SOURCE:** Pages 7–8, Section 1
**CLAUSE:** 1.6

**Functions of the Review Panel:**
a) Review of the Supply Code as and when necessary
b) Consider requests for review; make recommendations with reasons to the Commission
c) Issue guidelines on implementation of the Supply Code
d) Review causes of electrical accidents; recommend remedial measures based on Electrical
   Inspector reports under Section 161 of the Electricity Act, 2003 to avoid recurrence
e) Ensure consistency of proposed changes with other Codes, Laws, Acts, Rules and Regulations
   in force at that time
f) Undertake detailed studies on Supply Code matters; circulate findings and recommendations
   among Review Panel members and concerned entities
g) Hold regular meetings — minimum once every 6 months
h) Hold sub-committee meetings to discuss specific issues raised by any stakeholder group

**KEYWORDS:** Review Panel functions, Supply Code amendment, electrical accidents, Section 161,

## CHUNK 05
**TITLE:** Supply Code Review & Revision Process – How to Request an Amendment
**SOURCE:** Pages 8–9, Section 1
**CLAUSE:** 1.7, 1.8, 1.9, 1.10, 1.11

**How to Request an Amendment:**
- Send written request to Secretary of Review Panel WITH a copy to GERC
- If sent directly to GERC, it is forwarded to Review Panel Secretary
- Secretary circulates proposed changes to all panel members for written comments
- Chairperson may convene a meeting if needed
- Review Panel forwards recommendations to GERC for proposed amendments

**Post-Meeting Reporting (Member-Secretary must send to GERC):**
a) Outcome report of the review
b) Any proposed revision to the Supply Code
c) All written representations and objections from Users/Persons during review

**Record Keeping:**
- All changes incorporated in standard copy kept with Secretary of the Commission
- Standard copy includes chronological sheet of all changes + gazette notifications in sequence

**Availability of Updated Supply Code:**
- Updated version published on both GERC website AND all Licensees' websites
- All Distribution Licensees must maintain updated copies at their offices
- Copies must be available for sale at a reasonable cost

**KEYWORDS:** Supply Code amendment, revision process, how to amend, Secretary of review panel,
gazette notification, written request, updated Supply Code, website, reasonable cost

## CHUNK 06
**TITLE:** Supply Code Limitations, Confidentiality, Dispute Resolution & Related Regulations
**SOURCE:** Page 9, Section 1
**CLAUSE:** 1.12, 1.13, 1.14, 1.15, 1.16

**Limitations of the Supply Code (Clause 1.12):**
In unforeseen circumstances or operational contingencies, the Distribution Licensee must act
decisively to discharge its obligations. Users must provide reasonable cooperation and assistance.
The licensee must submit a report of such emergency actions to the Review Panel AND GERC
within 72 hours.

**Confidentiality (Clause 1.13):**
The Distribution Licensee SHALL NOT share/furnish any information about any consumer
UNLESS required by:
- Central Government departments under any law
- State Government departments under any law
- Any official agency under applicable law

**Dispute Resolution (Clause 1.14):**
Any dispute about Supply Code provisions between a User and Distribution Licensee must be
referred to GERC. GERC's decision is FINAL.

**Related Regulations (Clause 1.15) — Supply Code must be read together with:**
- GERC (Standard of Performance of Distribution Licensee) Regulations, 2005
- GERC (Licensee's Power to Recover Expenditure in Providing Supply and Miscellaneous
  Charges) Regulations, 2005
- GERC (Security Deposit) Regulations, 2005
- Other relevant regulations and codes notified by GERC

**Removing Implementation Difficulties (Clause 1.16):**
Distribution Licensee may apply to GERC for suitable orders to remove any difficulty that
arises in implementing these Regulations.

**KEYWORDS:** confidentiality, consumer data privacy, dispute resolution, GERC final authority,
emergency action, 72 hours report, Standard of Performance, Security Deposit, Miscellaneous

## CHUNK 08
**TITLE:** Definitions – Act, Agreement, Applicant, Application, Application Form
**SOURCE:** Pages 10–11, Section 2
**CLAUSE:** 2.3 items (1) to (5)

**(1) Act:**
Refers to the Electricity Act, 2003 AND Gujarat Electricity Industry (Reorganisation and
Regulation) Act, 2003 — read together and as amended from time to time.

**(2) Agreement:**
A formal contract entered into by the Distribution Licensee and the consumer for the supply
of electricity.

**(3) Applicant:**
An owner OR occupier of any land/premises who files an application form with a Distribution
Licensee for any of the following services:
- Supply of electricity (new connection)
- Increase or reduction in sanctioned load / contract demand
- Change in title or mutation of name
- Change in consumer category
- Disconnection or restoration of supply
- Termination of agreement
- Shifting of connection or other services
...in accordance with the Act and Supply Code rules/regulations.

**(4) Application:**
An application form COMPLETE in all respects in the format specified by the Commission,
along with documents AND showing payment of necessary charges and other compliances.
(Note: This is the FINAL stage — payment already made)

**(5) Application Form:**
An application form complete in all respects in the format specified by the Commission,
BEFORE payment of applicable charges.
(Note: This is the PRE-PAYMENT stage — form filled but charges not yet paid)

**KEY DIFFERENCE — Application vs Application Form:**
- Application Form = filled form, no payment yet
- Application = filled form + payment of charges made

## CHUNK 09
**TITLE:** Definitions – Area of Supply, Assessing Officer, Authorised Officer, Authorised Representative
**SOURCE:** Page 11, Section 2
**CLAUSE:** 2.3 items (6) to (9)

**(6) Area of Supply:**
The geographic area within which a Distribution Licensee is authorised by its License to
supply electricity. Each DISCOM (UGVCL, DGVCL, PGVCL, MGVCL, Torrent Power) has
a defined Area of Supply.

**(7) Assessing Officer:**
An officer designated by the State Government of Gujarat under Section 126 of the
Electricity Act, 2003. Responsible for assessing energy consumption in cases of
Unauthorised Use of Electricity (UUE). Issues provisional and final assessment orders.

**(8) Authorised Officer:**
An officer designated by the Commission or State Government under Section 135 of the
Electricity Act, 2003. Responsible for conducting physical inspections in cases of suspected
theft of electricity. Must carry and show a Photo Identity Card before entering any premises.

**Important distinction:**
- Assessing Officer (Section 126) → handles UUE (Unauthorised Use of Electricity) cases
- Authorised Officer (Section 135) → handles Theft of Electricity cases

**(9) Authorised Representative:**
All officers, staff, representatives, or persons discharging functions under the general or
specific authority of the concerned person or entity. Can act on behalf of a consumer or licensee.

**KEYWORDS:** area of supply, DISCOM area, Assessing Officer Section 126, Authorised Officer

## CHUNK 10
**TITLE:** Definitions – Billing Period, Calendar Year, Check Meter, Class-I Cities, Commission, Conductor
**SOURCE:** Pages 11–12, Section 2
**CLAUSE:** 2.3 items (10) to (15)

**(10) Billing Period:**
The nominal period between two consecutive meter reading dates.
- Minimum: 1 calendar month
- Maximum: 2 calendar months
- Can be monthly or bi-monthly as adopted by the Distribution Licensee

**(11) Calendar Year:**
Period from 1st January to 31st December of the same year.

**(12) Check Meter:**
A meter connected to the SAME core of CT (Current Transformer) and VT (Voltage Transformer)
as the main meter. Used ONLY for accounting and billing when the MAIN meter fails.
Important: Check meter readings are NOT used for routine billing.

**(13) Class-I Cities:**
Areas with a population exceeding 1 lakh (1,00,000) as per the latest Census of India.
This classification is important because it affects timelines for:
- New connection inspection: 7 working days (Class-I cities) vs 10 working days (rural areas)
- Other service timelines as per Standard of Performance Regulations

**(14) Commission:**
Refers to the Gujarat Electricity Regulatory Commission (GERC), constituted under the Act.

**(15) Conductor:**
Any wire, cable, bar, tube, rail, or plate used for conducting electrical energy, arranged to
be electrically connected to a system.

**KEYWORDS:** billing period, monthly billing, bi-monthly, check meter, main meter failure,

## CHUNK 11
**TITLE:** Definitions – Connected Load, Connection Point, Consumer Types, Consumer Installation
**SOURCE:** Pages 11–12, Section 2
**CLAUSE:** 2.3 items (16) to (19)

**(16) Connected Load (expressed in kW, kVA, or HP):**
Aggregate of manufacturer-rated capacities of ALL energy-consuming devices at the consumer's
premises that can be simultaneously operated.

**Conversion Formulas:**
- kVA → kW: multiply kVA × 0.9 (assuming Power Factor of 0.9)
- HP → kW: multiply HP × 0.746
- kW → HP: divide kW ÷ 0.746

**(17) Connection Point:**
The point at which the consumer's installation and/or apparatus is physically connected to
the Distribution Licensee's distribution system.

**(18) Consumer:**
Any person supplied electricity for their own use by a licensee, Government, or any person
in the business of supplying electricity under the Act. Includes persons whose premises are
connected for receiving electricity.

**Three Types of Consumer based on supply voltage:**
- LT Consumer (Low Tension): Supply at low or medium voltage (≤ 440V)
- HT Consumer (High Tension): Supply at high voltage (> 440V and < 66kV)
- EHT Consumer (Extra High Tension): Supply at extra high voltage (≥ 66kV)

**(19) Consumer Installation (Consumer's Installation):**
Any portable or stationary electrical unit — including wires, fittings, motors, and apparatus
— erected by or on behalf of the consumer at their premises, starting from the point of supply.
Also includes apparatus at the premises that is not yet connected but is intended to be connected.

**KEYWORDS:** connected load, kW kVA HP conversion, 0.746, 0.9, connection point, LT consumer,
HT consumer, EHT consumer, 440V, 66kV, consumer installation, low tension, high tension,
extra high tension, simultaneous use

## CHUNK 12
**TITLE:** Definitions – Contracted Demand, Cut-out, Date of Commencement, Demand Charge, Disconnection
**SOURCE:** Pages 12–13, Section 2
**CLAUSE:** 2.3 items (20) to (24)

**(20) Contracted Load / Sanctioned Load / Contracted Demand:**
The MAXIMUM demand (in kW, kVA, or HP) agreed to be supplied by the licensee, as
specified in the Agreement between licensee and consumer.
- kVA × 0.9 = kW (Power Factor assumed 0.9)
- HP × 0.746 = kW

Note: "Contracted Load", "Sanctioned Load", and "Contracted Demand" are used
interchangeably in the Supply Code and refer to the same thing.

**(21) Cut-out:**
Any appliance or device that AUTOMATICALLY interrupts electricity flow through a conductor
when current rises above a pre-determined level. Includes fusible devices (fuses).

**(22) Date of Commencement of Supply:**
The date on which the licensee energises the consumer's installation by connecting it to the
Distribution mains. This date is the starting point for the Agreement period (minimum 2 years).

**(23) Demand Charge for a Billing Period:**
A charge levied on the consumer based on the contracted/sanctioned/connected load OR
maximum demand (as applicable), calculated as per the procedure in the Tariff Order
approved by the Commission.

**(24) Disconnection:**
The physical separation OR remote disconnection of a User or Consumer from the distribution
system. (Remote disconnection was added via Third Amendment 2023 for smart meters.)

**KEYWORDS:** contracted load, sanctioned load, contracted demand, kW kVA HP, cut-out, fuse,
date of commencement, demand charge, disconnection, remote disconnection, smart meter,
Agreement period, Tariff Order, energisation

## CHUNK 13
**TITLE:** Definitions – Distribution System, Distribution Licensee, Distribution Mains, Earthing, Electric Line
**SOURCE:** Pages 12–13, Section 2
**CLAUSE:** 2.3 items (25) to (30)

**(25) Distribution Mains:**
The portion of any main supply line with which a service line is, or is intended to be,
immediately connected.

**(26) Distribution Licensee:**
A person granted a licence under Section 14 of the Electricity Act, 2003, authorising them
to operate and maintain a distribution system for supplying electricity to consumers in their
area of supply.
(Examples: UGVCL, DGVCL, PGVCL, MGVCL, Torrent Power)

**(27) Distribution System:**
The system of wires and associated facilities between:
- Delivery points on transmission lines or generating station connection, AND
- The point of connection to the consumer's installation.

Includes: overhead lines, underground cables, service lines, electrical plant, control
switchgear, and meters with design voltage of 33 kV and BELOW. Also includes higher
voltage systems specifically recognized by the Commission.

EXCLUDES: Any part of the Transmission System EXCEPT terminal equipment (metering
system, CT and PT) at the consumer end for EHV (66 kV and above) consumers.

**(28) Earthed:**
Connected to the general mass of earth in a manner that ensures immediate discharge of
energy without danger at all times.

**(29) Electric Line:**
Any line used for carrying electricity for any purpose. Includes:
(i) Any support structure — tower, pole, or other thing — from which such line is supported
(ii) Any apparatus connected to such line for carrying electricity

**(30) Electrical Inspector:**
A person appointed by the appropriate Government under Section 162(1) of the Electricity
Act, 2003. Includes the Chief Electrical Inspector.

**KEYWORDS:** distribution system, 33kV, distribution licensee, Section 14, distribution mains,
UGVCL DGVCL PGVCL MGVCL Torrent, earthing, electric line, pole tower, Electrical Inspector,

## CHUNK 14
**TITLE:** Definitions – Energy Charge, Voltage Levels, Financial Year, Feeder, GERC, Load Factor
**SOURCE:** Pages 13–14, Section 2
**CLAUSE:** 2.3 items (31) to (43)

**(31) Energy Charge:**
A charge levied based on the QUANTITY of electricity supplied (in kWh or kVAh), including
fuel surcharge and time-of-use charges as per the tariff schedule.

**(32) Extra High Voltage (EHV):**
Voltage equal to AND greater than 66 kV.

**(33) Financial Year:**
Period from 1st April of one year to 31st March of the next year.

**(34) Feeder:**
An LT, HT, or EHT line emanating from a sub-station, to which a distribution sub-station
or LT/HT/EHT consumers are connected.

**(35) Generating Companies:**
Any company, body corporate, association, or artificial juridical person that owns, operates,
or maintains a generating station.

**(36) GERC:**
Gujarat Electricity Regulatory Commission.

**(37) High Tension (HT) / High Voltage:**
Voltage greater than 440V AND less than 66kV.

**(38) Indian Standards (IS):**
Standards and specifications approved by the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS).

**(39) Independent Feeder:**
A feeder that supplies to a SINGLE consumer exclusively.

**(40) Interconnection:**
Same meaning as "Connection Point" as defined in Clause 2.3(17).

**(41) Licence:**
A licence granted under Section 14 of the Electricity Act, 2003.

**(42) Licensed Electrical Contractor (LEC):**
A contractor licensed under Clause 29 of the Central Electricity Authority (Measures relating
to Safety and Electric Supply) Regulations, 2010.

**(43) Load Factor:**
Ratio of actual units consumed in a given period to total units that would have been consumed
if load was maintained at contracted level throughout.

**Formula:** Load Factor (%) = (Actual units consumed × 100) ÷ (Contracted load in kW × No. of hours in the period)

## CHUNK 15
**TITLE:** Definitions – Low Tension, Main, Maximum Demand, Meter, Month, Occupier, Overhead Line
**SOURCE:** Pages 13–14, Section 2
**CLAUSE:** 2.3 items (44) to (50)

**(44) Low Tension (LT) / Low Voltage:**
Voltage NOT exceeding 440 Volts.

**(45) Main:**
Any electric supply line through which electricity is, or is intended to be, supplied.

**(46) Maximum Demand:**
The average kW or kVA supplied during the consecutive period of MAXIMUM use.
Recording intervals depend on the meter type:
- 30-minute interval meters
- 15-minute interval meters
- 3-minute interval meters
The meter must have MDI (Maximum Demand Indicator) feature to record this directly.

**(47) Meter:**
Equipment used for measuring, indicating, and recording electrical quantities such as:
- Energy in kWh or kVAh
- Maximum demand in kW or kVA
- Reactive energy in kVARh

Includes accessories: Current Transformer (CT), Voltage Transformer (VT), Potential
Transformer (PT), Capacitor Voltage Transformer (CVT) when used with the meter.

**(48) Month:**
Calendar month — from a specified date in one calendar month to the same date in the next
calendar month (approximately 30 days).

**(49) Occupier:**
The owner OR tenant OR any person in occupation of premises where energy is used or
proposed to be used.

**(50) Overhead Line:**
Any electric supply line placed above ground and in open air. EXCLUDES live rails of
traction systems.

## CHUNK 16
**TITLE:** Definitions – Person, Point of Supply, Power Factor, Premises, Rural Areas
**SOURCE:** Pages 14–15, Section 2
**CLAUSE:** 2.3 items (51) to (55)

**(51) Person:**
Any individual, company, body corporate, association, body of individuals (incorporated or
not), or artificial juridical person.

**(52) Point of Supply:**
Unless otherwise agreed, the Point of Supply is:
- **For LT Consumers:** The incoming terminal of the cut-out/MCB/ELCB installed by the
  consumer immediately after the meter at the consumer's premises
- **For HT/EHT Consumers (with on-premises substation):** Distribution box installed on
  the transformer centre/substation established on consumer's premises
- **For HT/EHT Consumers (general):** Control switchgears installed at the consumer's
  premises as per provisions of this Code

**(53) Power Factor:**
The cosine of the electrical angle between voltage and current vectors in an AC electrical
circuit. Required to be maintained at ≥ 0.90 (90%) for three-phase consumers.

**(54) Premises:**
Land, building, infrastructure, or part/combination thereof for which a separate meter or
metering arrangement has been made by the licensee.

**Special case — Agriculture Connection:**
"Premises" means the place of the SOURCE OF WATER for which the connection was given
or is intended to be given by the licensee.

**(55) Rural Areas:**
Areas covered within Gram Panchayats as defined in the Gujarat Gram Panchayat Act.
(This classification affects timelines: rural areas get 10 working days for inspection vs
7 working days for Class-I cities/urban areas.)

**KEYWORDS:** point of supply, LT point of supply, HT point of supply, MCB ELCB cut-out,
power factor cosine, 90% power factor, premises definition, agriculture premises water source,
rural areas, Gram Panchayat, person definition, 440V supply terminal

The most recent order issued by the Commission for a licensee specifying rates to be charged

**(62) Tariff Schedule:**
The most recent schedule of charges for electricity supply and services issued by the licensee

**(63) Theft:**

## CHUNK 18
**TITLE:** Definitions – Transmission Licensee, Transmission System, Urban Areas, User
**SOURCE:** Page 15, Section 2
**CLAUSE:** 2.3 items (64) to (67)

**(64) Transmission Licensee:**
A person granted a licence under Section 14 of the Electricity Act, 2003 authorising them
to establish or operate transmission lines.
(Example: Gujarat Energy Transmission Corporation — GETCO)

**(65) Transmission System:**
System of extra high voltage (EHV) electric lines operated at EHV (excluding generator
interconnection facilities), owned/operated by the Transmission Licensee for:
- Transmitting electricity from one power station to a sub-station
- Transmitting between power stations
- Transmitting between sub-stations
- Connecting to or from external interconnection equipment up to interconnection with the
  distribution system

Includes: plant, apparatus, and meters owned/used by transmission licensee.
EXCLUDES: Any part of any distribution system of the licensee.

**(66) Urban Areas:**
Areas covered by all Municipal Corporations and other Municipalities, including areas under:
- Urban Development Authorities
- Cantonment Authorities
- Industrial estates or townships

EXCLUDES areas covered under Class-I Cities (which are a separate classification).

Note: Urban Areas and Class-I Cities are DIFFERENT categories.
- Class-I Cities: Population > 1 lakh (defined under Census of India)
- Urban Areas: Municipal/cantonment areas, EXCLUDING Class-I Cities

**(67) User:**
Any person having an electrical interface with, or using, the distribution system of the
Distribution Licensee to whom this Code is applicable.

## CHUNK 20 — CRITICAL COMPARISONS
**TITLE:** Key Distinctions – Easily Confused Terms in the Supply Code
**SOURCE:** Pages 10–15, Section 2 (Synthesised Reference Chunk)
**CLAUSE:** 2.3 multiple items

This chunk directly answers common comparison questions your bot will receive:

**1. Application Form vs Application:**
- Application Form = form filled correctly in Commission-specified format, BEFORE payment
- Application = same form + payment of all applicable charges made
- Think of it as: Form → Application Form → (pay charges) → Application

**2. LT vs HT vs EHT Consumer:**
- LT: ≤ 440V
- HT: > 440V and < 66kV
- EHT: ≥ 66kV

**3. Connected Load vs Contracted/Sanctioned Load:**
- Connected Load = total installed capacity of ALL devices that CAN run simultaneously
- Contracted/Sanctioned Load = what the licensee has AGREED to supply (in the Agreement)
- Connected Load ≥ Contracted Load in normal use

**4. Assessing Officer vs Authorised Officer:**
- Assessing Officer (Section 126) = handles Unauthorised Use of Electricity (UUE) cases
- Authorised Officer (Section 135) = handles Theft of Electricity cases

**5. Class-I Cities vs Urban Areas:**
- Class-I Cities: Population > 1 lakh per Census of India
- Urban Areas: Municipal/cantonment areas — EXCLUDES Class-I Cities
- They are separate categories; Class-I Cities are NOT included in Urban Areas

**6. Tariff Order vs Tariff Schedule:**
- Tariff Order = GERC's order specifying the rates (the regulatory document)
- Tariff Schedule = the actual schedule of charges issued by the licensee based on Tariff Order

**7. Distribution System vs Transmission System:**
- Distribution System: ≤ 33kV (with some exceptions); connects sub-station to consumer
- Transmission System: EHV lines (generally > 66kV); connects power stations to sub-stations
- Distribution does NOT include Transmission except terminal metering equipment at consumer end for EHV consumers

**8. Financial Year vs Calendar Year:**
- Financial Year: 1 April to 31 March
- Calendar Year: 1 January to 31 December
