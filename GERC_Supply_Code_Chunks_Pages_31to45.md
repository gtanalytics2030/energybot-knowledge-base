- If connection is registered in APPLICANT's name: Identity proof (per Clause 4.15) only

## CHUNK 45
**TITLE:** Transfer of Connection – Change of Name Due to Ownership / Occupancy Change
**SOURCE:** Pages 32–33, Section 4
**CLAUSE:** 4.69, 4.70, 4.71

**Clause 4.69 — No Transfer Without Licensee's Written Consent:**
A consumer shall NOT assign, transfer, or part with the benefit of the Agreement with the
Distribution Licensee without prior WRITTEN CONSENT of the licensee.

**Clause 4.70 — When Can a Connection Be Transferred:**
A connection may be transferred in the name of another person:
- Upon DEATH of the consumer, OR
- Upon TRANSFER of ownership or occupancy of the premises
By filing the application form (Annexure I or II as applicable) for change of name.

IMPORTANT: Change of name does NOT entitle the applicant to demand shifting of the
connection from its present location.

**Clause 4.71 — Procedure for Change of Name (Ownership/Occupancy Change):**
1. Apply in format prescribed in Annexure I or II, along with:
   - Copy of latest bill (duly paid)
   - Proof of ownership/occupancy of property
   - All dues against the connection must be CLEARED before acceptance
   - NOC from registered consumer (if security deposit is to be transferred to applicant's name)
   - Any shortfall in security deposit is payable by the applicant

2. If NOC from registered consumer is NOT submitted:
   - Application accepted ONLY if fresh security deposit is paid by applicant
   - Original security deposit refunded to the original claimant when they make a claim

3. Change of name effected within time frame prescribed in GERC (Standard of Performance)
   Regulations 2005.

**KEYWORDS:** transfer of connection, change of name, ownership change, occupancy change,
written consent, dues cleared, NOC registered consumer, security deposit transfer, fresh
security deposit, change of name no shifting, Standard of Performance timeline

## CHUNK 46
**TITLE:** Transfer of Connection – Transfer to Legal Heir After Consumer's Death
**SOURCE:** Pages 33–34, Section 4
**CLAUSE:** 4.72

**Clause 4.72 — Procedure for Transfer to Legal Heir:**

**Step 1 — Apply with the following documents:**
- Application in format prescribed in Annexure I or II
- Copy of latest electricity bill (duly paid)
- ANY ONE proof of legal heirship:
  - Registered Will/deed
  - Succession Certificate or Legal Heir Certificate
  - Mutation in municipal/land records
  - Any other proof of legal heirship
- NOC from other legal heirs (if connection is being transferred in name of ONE of
  multiple legal heirs)

**Step 2 — Security Deposit:**
- Security deposit in the name of original (deceased) consumer is TRANSFERRED to the
  legal heir receiving the connection.
- Any SHORTFALL in security deposit (as per GERC Security Deposit Regulations 2005)
  is payable by the applicant.

**Step 3 — Change Effected:**
As per the time frame in GERC (Standard of Performance) Regulations 2005.

**Step 4 — Liability of Unpaid Dues:**
Any electricity charges or other sums unpaid by the deceased consumer OR erstwhile
owner/occupier shall be a CHARGE on the premises — recoverable by the licensee from:
- The legal representative/successors-in-law, OR
- New owner/occupier of the premises

**KEYWORDS:** legal heir transfer, death of consumer, succession certificate, registered will,
legal heir certificate, mutation records, NOC co-heirs, security deposit transfer, unpaid dues
deceased consumer, charge on premises, Annexure I II legal heir

**SOURCE:** Pages 34–35, Section 4

**Clause 4.76 — Erroneous Classification Found by Licensee:**
If a consumer has been classified in an INCORRECT category (or if contract demand
was incorrectly enhanced/reduced):
- Licensee may reclassify after issuing notice with MINIMUM 30-day notice period
  to execute a fresh Agreement.
- If consumer does not execute fresh Agreement within the notice period:
  → Licensee issues 21-day SHOW CAUSE NOTICE
  → After considering consumer's explanation (if any), licensee may disconnect supply.

**Applications for Change of Tariff Class (by licensee's initiative or consumer request):**
- Licensee must dispose of within MAXIMUM 7 DAYS of receiving the application
- OR communicate reasons for NOT changing within 7 days
- Any dispute: referred to Consumer Grievances Redressal Forum (CGRF)

**Clause 4.77 — Consumer Requesting Reclassification:**
1. Consumer applies in Annexure I or II format.
2. Processed per Clauses 4.64–4.66; inspection per Clauses 4.26–4.37.
3. Licensee notes meter reading at time of inspection.
4. If reclassification is VALID: Change effective from DATE OF INSPECTION;
   written acknowledgment sent to consumer.

**Clause 4.78 — Timeline for Disposal:**
- If valid: Change effective from date of inspection.
- If NOT valid: Consumer informed in writing with reasons within 10 DAYS from date
  of inspection.
- All tariff class change applications: disposed within maximum 7 DAYS of receipt.

**Clause 4.79 — Protection During Pending Reclassification:**
For the period the reclassification application is pending BEYOND the prescribed time limit,
the consumer shall NOT be liable for any action on grounds of Unauthorised Use of
Electricity (UUE).

## CHUNK 52
**TITLE:** Review of Contracted Load – Automatic Review by Licensee for HT/EHT/LT Consumers
**SOURCE:** Pages 38–39, Section 4
**CLAUSE:** 4.95, 4.96, 4.97

**Clause 4.95 — Review for HT, EHT & Demand-Based LT Connections:**
Trigger condition: Maximum demand exceeds contract demand by 5% or more for at
least FOUR TIMES during the last financial year.

Process:
1. Licensee issues 30-day notice to consumer to submit load enhancement application.
2. If no response by end of notice period: Licensee enhances contract demand to the
   AVERAGE of four highest MDI readings in last financial year.
3. Consumer pays all applicable regularisation charges.
4. Enhanced demand becomes revised contract demand on receipt of charges.

**Review for Non-Demand-Based LT Connections:**
Trigger: Connected load found in excess of Contracted/Sanctioned Load by:
- 25% or more — for RESIDENTIAL consumers
- 10% or more — for OTHER categories

Process:
1. Review conducted ONCE PER FINANCIAL YEAR.
2. Licensee issues 60-day notice to submit load enhancement application.
3. If no response: Licensee enhances load to the load found at time of inspection.
4. Consumer pays all applicable regularisation charges.
5. Enhanced load becomes revised Contracted/Sanctioned Load on receipt of charges.

**Special Provision for Un-metered Agriculture Connections:**
Trigger: Connected load (assessed by Accucheck meter) exceeds Contracted/Sanctioned
Load by 10% or more.

Process:
1. Licensee issues 30-day notice.
2. Consumer liable to pay regularisation charges.
3. If no response: Licensee initiates UUE proceedings per Clauses 7.31–7.44.

**Clause 4.96:**
For all the above reviews, procedures for application, inspection, demand note, payment,
and energisation follow the standard provisions in Clauses 4.21–4.32, 4.33–4.37, 4.82,
and 4.85.

**Clause 4.97:**
Consumer aggrieved may approach CGRF.

**KEYWORDS:** contracted load review, 5% excess MDI, four times financial year, 30-day notice
HT EHT, 60-day notice LT residential, 25% residential, 10% other categories, automatic
enhancement, MDI average, un-metered agriculture, Accucheck, UUE proceedings, demand
based LT, non-demand based LT, regularisation

## CHUNK 57
**TITLE:** Section 5 – AC Motor Installation Requirements & Starting Current Limits
**SOURCE:** Pages 43–45, Section 5
**CLAUSE:** 5.8, 5.9, 5.10, 5.11

**Clause 5.8 — Control Gear Requirements for Motors:**
Motors must have control gear with:
- No-volt release
- Overload release (triple pole fuses)
To keep maximum starting current within specified limits at all times.

**Maximum Starting Current Limits for LT/Medium Voltage Motors:**

| Motor Size | Maximum Current Demand Allowed |
|---|---|
| Up to and including 1 HP | 6 × full load current |
| Above 1 HP up to 10 HP | 3 × full load current |
| Above 10 HP up to 15 HP | 2 × full load current |
| Above 15 HP | 1.5 × full load current |

Non-compliance: Consumer liable to disconnection (likely disturbance to other consumers'
supply). Licensee may relax starting current limit based on location and working conditions.

**Additional rule:** Motors above 2 HP must be wound for 3-phase, 400V between phases.

**Clause 5.9 — Three-Phase Motor Circuit Protection:**
- Each 3-phase motor circuit: protected by an iron-clad TRIPLE POLE LINKED SWITCH
  (ganged operation).
- Wiring for motors: All three phase wires bunched in a SINGLE metallic or rigid PVC conduit,
  effectively earthed with wire throughout, connected to motor frame.
- TWO SEPARATE EARTH WIRES of adequate size from motor frame.
- Minimum earth wire size: 3.0 sq.mm (or 14 SWG).

**Clause 5.10 — Balanced Loading:**
All consumers using electricity for motive power must ensure BALANCED LOADING across
all three phases of the licensee's system at the incoming terminals of consumer's premises.

**Clause 5.11 — Advice for Other Apparatus:**
Consumers wanting to use apparatus OTHER THAN motors may seek advice from the
licensee on technical particulars and any special connection conditions BEFORE procurement.
Licensee must respond immediately (or as soon as practicable if more time needed to
gather information).

## CHUNK 61 — CRITICAL COMPARISONS (Pages 31–45)
**TITLE:** Key Distinctions – Easily Confused Rules in Pages 31–45
**SOURCE:** Pages 31–45, Sections 4 & 5 (Synthesised Reference Chunk)
**CLAUSE:** Multiple

**1. Change of Name (Ownership/Occupancy) vs Transfer to Legal Heir:**
- Ownership/occupancy change (4.71): All dues must be cleared; NOC from registered
  consumer needed for SD transfer; fresh SD if no NOC
- Legal heir transfer (4.72): NOC from co-heirs (if one heir is getting connection); unpaid
  dues of deceased consumer become a CHARGE on the premises — recoverable from the heir

**2. Online Deficiency Notice: New Connection vs Modification:**
- New connection (4.21): 2 WORKING DAYS to intimate deficiency
- Modification (4.64): 24 HOURS to intimate deficiency (tighter deadline)

**3. Reclassification: Effective Date:**
- Consumer-requested reclassification (4.77): Effective from DATE OF INSPECTION
- Licensee-initiated reclassification for erroneous category (4.76): Effective after fresh
  agreement is executed

**4. Deemed Grant: Load Enhancement vs Load Reduction:**
- Both have deemed grant after further 15 days following written notice
- Trigger: Load enhancement — if not decided within Standard of Performance period;
  Load reduction — if not decided within demand note issuance period

**5. HT vs EHT Load Reduction Demand Note Timeline:**
- LT: 10 days; HT: 15 days; EHT: 30 days
- (The higher the voltage, the more time the licensee gets)

**6. Load Enhancement Energisation: With vs Without Augmentation:**
- Up to 100 kVA/kW: 20 days WITHOUT augmentation / 60 days WITH augmentation
- This is the ONLY category with dual timeline depending on system augmentation need

**7. Consumer Self-Wiring vs Licensee Wiring:**
- Consumer wiring: Must be by Licensed Electrical Contractor; must follow BIS standards;
  Tested per CEA Clause 31; multi-storey (>15m) must also comply with CEA Clause 36
- Licensee equipment: Consumer provides space free of charge; all equipment remains
  licensee's PROPERTY

**8. ELCB Removal vs Non-installation of Capacitor:**
- ELCB removed after installation: 7 days notice then disconnect; reconnect only after
  ELCB reinstalled
- Capacitor not installed: 10 days notice then may disconnect; minimum charges STILL
  apply even if supply withheld (Clause 3.6)

**KEYWORDS:** change of name vs legal heir, 2 working days vs 24 hours, reclassification
effective date, deemed grant enhancement vs reduction, HT EHT demand note days, augmentation
20 vs 60 days, ELCB vs capacitor notice, consumer wiring vs licensee wiring, BIS standards,
CEA 36 multi-storey
