## CHUNK 65
**TITLE:** Section 5 (Concluded) – HT/EHT Switchgear, Licensee Property & Consumer Damage Liability
**SOURCE:** Page 46, Section 5
**CLAUSE:** 5.18, 5.19, 5.20, 5.21

**Clause 5.18 — HT/EHT Switchgear:**
High Voltage and Extra High Voltage consumers must use switchgears of adequate capacity
and suitable protective devices (per CEA Safety Regulations 2010) to protect the Distribution
Licensee's apparatus placed on the consumer's premises.

**Clause 5.19 — Licensee's Property — Strictly No Consumer Access:**
- Equipment for supply up to 33 kV: property of the Distribution Licensee.
- Air break switch for supply at 66 kV and above: property of the Distribution Licensee.
- These MUST NOT be operated, handled, or removed by anyone not employed by the
  Distribution Licensee.
- Seals, nameplates, and distinguishing numbers/marks on such property must NOT be
  interfered with, broken, removed, or erased — except by duly authorised licensee employees.

**Clause 5.20 — Emergency Permission for HT Consumer:**
The Distribution Licensee MAY grant written permission to an HT consumer (on application)
to operate the licensee's terminal switches, fuses, or circuit breakers for the purpose of
ISOLATING the consumer's own HT apparatus in an EMERGENCY — provided such
operation does NOT affect supply continuity to other consumers.

**Clause 5.21 — Consumer Liable for Damage to Licensee's Property:**
Every consumer must compensate the Distribution Licensee for any damage caused to the
licensee's mains, apparatus, instruments, or other property on the consumer's premises —
whether caused by the consumer, their servants, or persons employed by them.

**KEYWORDS:** HT EHT switchgear protective devices, licensee property, 33kV air break switch,
consumer not to operate, emergency permission HT, terminal switches isolation, consumer
damage liability, seals nameplates, CEA regulations, authorized employee only

## CHUNK 66
**TITLE:** Section 6 – Meter Requirement, Types of Meters & Consumer's Option to Procure
**SOURCE:** Pages 46–48, Section 6
**CLAUSE:** 6.1, 6.2, 6.3, 6.4, 6.5, 6.6, 6.7

**Clause 6.1 — No Supply Without a Meter:**
No installation shall be serviced without a meter.
ALL NEW CONNECTIONS must be serviced with SMART PRE-PAYMENT METER or
PRE-PAYMENT METER (added via 3rd Amendment 2023).
Exception: Licensee may seek Commission's approval (with justification) to deviate from
this requirement; Commission may allow deviation with recorded reasons.
All meters must conform to CEA (Installation & Operation of Meters) Regulations 2006.

**Clause 6.2 — MCB/CB Requirements:**
- LT loads: Linked Switch with fuse, MCB, ELCB, or Cut-out
- HT/EHT loads: Linked Switch with fuse or Circuit Breakers (CBs) per CEA Clause 35
All of appropriate rating and specification, installed by the consumer.

**Clause 6.3 — Consumer's Option for Meter Procurement:**
Option A — Consumer purchases meter themselves:
- From vendor with make/specification approved by the licensee
- Licensee publishes approved list on website; also lists where tested/sealed meters can be purchased
- After procurement: licensee tests, installs, and seals the meter

Option B — Licensee supplies meter:
- On payment of applicable charges
- Consumer indicates choice in the application form

**Clause 6.4 — Licensee May Upgrade / Install Remote Meters:**
Licensee may review installed meters when technology upgrades or site suitability changes.
May install remote metering devices or Maximum Demand (MD) meters with additional features.

**Clause 6.5 — Meter Types for New Connections:**
(1) LT Consumers:
   - 1-phase LT: Smart pre-payment meter or pre-payment meter.
     If exempted: Electronic meter.
   - 3-phase LT: Smart pre-payment meter or pre-payment meter.
     If exempted: Electronic meter with MDI (Maximum Demand Indicator).

(2) HT/EHT Consumers:
   - 3-phase Tri-vector meter with MDI.
   - Must have "Time of the Day" metering feature.
   - Must store at least 45 days of data.
   - Option to record peak hours MDI.
   - Must be Smart pre-payment or pre-payment meter.
     If exempted: Electronic meter with AMR (Automatic Meter Reading) facility.

(3) Where licensee faces problems in dues collection: Pre-paid meters may be installed.

**Clause 6.6 — Multiple Categories in Same Premises:**
If the licensee supplies different consumer categories within the same premises, SEPARATE
meters shall be installed for each category.

**Clause 6.7 — HT/EHT on Independent Feeder:**
If an HT/EHT consumer is served by an independent feeder exclusively for their use,
metering arrangement may be at the consumer's premises OR (if mutually agreed) at the
licensee's sub-station — in which case no meter is needed at consumer's premises.

**KEYWORDS:** smart prepayment meter, prepayment meter, no supply without meter, MCB ELCB
CB, consumer procures meter, licensee supplies meter, approved vendor list, LT meter type,
HT EHT tri-vector meter, MDI, time of day metering, 45 days storage, AMR, separate meter
multiple categories, independent feeder metering, Commission exemption meter

## CHUNK 67
**TITLE:** Section 6 – Supply & Installation of Meters, Meter Particulars Sheet & Check Meter
**SOURCE:** Pages 48–49, Section 6
**CLAUSE:** 6.8, 6.9, 6.10, 6.11, 6.12, 6.13, 6.14, 6.15, 6.16

**Clause 6.8 — Enclosure for Consumer-Procured Meters:**
If a consumer procures their own meter and related apparatus, they must provide a LOCKED
and WEATHERPROOF enclosure (design approved by licensee) to house the metering
equipment including CTs and PTs. If the licensee supplies the meter, the enclosure is
included in the estimate and provided by the licensee.

**Clause 6.9 — Meter Rent:**
- If meter is supplied by the licensee: Meter RENT charged as per GERC (Miscellaneous
  Charges) Regulations 2005.
- If consumer bears cost of meter: NO meter rent is charged.

**Clause 6.10 — Meter Space & Accessibility:**
Consumer must provide suitable and adequate space for meter installation, ensuring
it is ALWAYS accessible to the licensee or its representatives. For multi-storeyed buildings:
meter fixed on ground floor/rising mains with proper air ventilation and adequate illumination.

**Clause 6.11 — Who Installs the Meter:**
Initial installation or replacement of meter shall be done by the LICENSEE's authorized
person/representative in the presence of the consumer or their authorized representative.
- For initial installation: Advance notice given keeping overall connection release timeline in mind.
- For meter REPLACEMENT: Notice period of 7 DAYS.

**Clause 6.12 — Meter Particulars Sheet:**
Licensee must maintain a Meter Particulars Sheet (standardized format) for each meter recording
particulars at initial installation or replacement.
- Licensee retains one copy.
- Second copy signed by licensee's representative given to consumer under proper acknowledgment.
- Consumer/representative also signs the sheet.
- All subsequent faults, repairs, and replacements entered into the sheet by the licensee.

**Clause 6.13 — Sealing of Meters:**
Whenever a new meter is installed (new connection or replacement), meter and associated
equipment must be SEALED in the consumer's/representative's presence. Seals, nameplates,
and distinguishing marks must NOT be broken, erased, or altered by the consumer. Seal
treatment per CEA (Installation and Operation of Meters) Regulations 2006.

**Clause 6.14 — Consumer's Custody Obligation:**
Consumer is responsible for safe custody of meter(s), MCB/CB, etc. if installed at consumer's
premises. Consumer must promptly notify the licensee about any fault, accident, or problem.

**Clause 6.15 — Licensee's Maintenance Responsibility:**
It is the LICENSEE's responsibility to maintain the meter and keep it in working order at
all times. If metering unit is on a pole or outside premises and display unit is inside: the
licensee is responsible for safe custody of the metering unit.

**Clause 6.16 — Check Meter:**
Consumer MAY install a check meter (conforming to CEA Regulations 2006) at own cost.
Licensee may calibrate it on payment of prescribed fee.
IMPORTANT: Check meter readings are NOT used for billing — EXCEPT when the main
meter fails.

**KEYWORDS:** locked weatherproof enclosure, meter rent, no rent consumer meter, meter space
accessibility, multi-storeyed ground floor, 7 days replacement notice, Meter Particulars Sheet,
meter sealing, consumer safe custody, licensee maintain meter, check meter not for billing,
check meter main meter failure, CTs PTs enclosure

## CHUNK 68
**TITLE:** Section 6 – Reading of Meters: Schedule, Smart Meters, Inaccessible Meters & Notices
**SOURCE:** Pages 49–51, Section 6
**CLAUSE:** 6.17, 6.18, 6.19, 6.20, 6.21, 6.22, 6.23, 6.24, 6.25

**Clause 6.17 — Meter Reading Schedule:**
- Regular meters: Read on fixed date ± 3 working days (monthly) or ± 5 working days
  (bi-monthly).
- Smart meters: Read REMOTELY at least ONCE EVERY MONTH.
- Other pre-payment meters: Read at least ONCE EVERY THREE MONTHS.
- Energy data made available to consumer via website, mobile app, or SMS.
- Smart pre-payment meter consumers may access consumption data in REAL TIME.
- Consumer with smart pre-payment meter may request a recorded reading — must be allowed.

**Clause 6.18 — Who Reads the Meter:**
Only an authorised representative of the licensee. All meter readers must carry and show
their PHOTO IDENTITY CARD during meter reading.

**Clause 6.19 — MRI / Remote Reading:**
Licensee may use handheld instruments, MRI (Meter Reading Instrument), or wireless
equipment. If bills are generated from MRI downloads or remote reading, consumer may
request a record of the reading — must be allowed.

**Clause 6.20 — Earth Leakage LED Check:**
Meter reader must check LED condition on electronic meters. If Earth Leakage (E/L) LED
is ON: inform consumer of leakage in premises, advise wiring check, and report to licensee officials.

**Clause 6.21 — Provisional Billing When Meter Not Read:**
If meter cannot be read in a billing cycle:
- Provisional bill based on AVERAGE of last 3 billing cycles when readings were taken.
- Provisional billing must NOT continue for more than 2 BILLING CYCLES at a stretch.
- More than 2 provisional bills NOT generated in one financial year (except force majeure).
- Consumer option: Send photo of meter (showing reading + date) via registered mobile/email
  within ±3 days (monthly) or ±5 days (bi-monthly) of bill due date.
- If meter remains inaccessible even after 2 billing cycles: Bill for MINIMUM CHARGE
  and/or fixed charge only.

**Clause 6.22 — Notice for Inaccessible Meter (After 2 Consecutive Missed Readings):**
If meter inaccessible on 2 consecutive reading dates AND consumer has not sent meter photo:
Licensee issues notice specifying date and time (at least 7 days from notice date) for
consumer to make meter accessible.

**Clause 6.23 — Supply Disconnection for Continued Inaccessibility:**
If meter not made accessible on the specified date: A final notice served on consumer or
affixed at main entrance — consumer must provide access within next 7 DAYS.
Failing this: SUPPLY DISCONNECTED.

**Clause 6.24 — Exemption for Domestic Consumer Out of Station:**
Clauses 6.22 and 6.23 do NOT apply to a domestic consumer who has:
- Given advance intimation of inaccessibility due to being out of station, AND
- Deposited advance amount per Clause 6.67.

**Clause 6.25 — Extended Absence Facility for Domestic Consumers:**
When a domestic consumer informs licensee in writing of continued absence:
- No notice or provisional bill sent — provided consumer pays FIXED CHARGES in advance.
- On return, entire consumption treated as if for the period excluding the absence period.
- Consumer must make meter available for reading once every 6 MONTHS (with prior notice)
  OR send meter photo via registered mobile/email. Failing this: supply may be disconnected.
- This facility is available only if consumer has paid up-to-date dues.

**KEYWORDS:** meter reading schedule, monthly bi-monthly, smart meter remote reading monthly,
pre-payment meter 3 months, MRI handheld, earth leakage LED, provisional bill 3 cycles,
2 billing cycles max, meter photo registered mobile, inaccessible meter notice 7 days,
disconnection inaccessible, out of station advance payment, 6 months meter reading, force
majeure, minimum charge inaccessible

## CHUNK 69
**TITLE:** Section 6 – Testing of Meters: Licensee, Consumer Request & Third-Party Testing
**SOURCE:** Pages 51–52, Section 6
**CLAUSE:** 6.26, 6.27, 6.28, 6.29, 6.30, 6.31, 6.32, 6.33, 6.34

**Clause 6.26 — Licensee's Pre-Installation Testing Responsibility:**
Licensee must satisfy itself about meter accuracy BEFORE installation. Licensee must conduct
periodic inspection, testing, and calibration per CEA (Installation & Operation of Meters)
Regulations 2006. Licensee must provide the accuracy report and periodic calibration reports
to the consumer.

**Clause 6.27:**
All test results must be maintained (recorded and kept).

**Clause 6.28 — Licensee-Initiated Testing:**
Licensee has the right to test any meter and related apparatus if there is reasonable doubt
about its accuracy. Consumer must provide necessary assistance for the test.

**Clause 6.29 — Consumer-Requested Testing (On Premises):**
Consumer may request meter testing on their premises if they doubt accuracy — by applying
to the licensee with the requisite TESTING FEE. Licensee then follows procedure in
Clauses 6.31–6.33.

**Clause 6.30 — Third-Party Testing:**
Consumer may request testing at a THIRD-PARTY facility approved by the Commission.
List of approved third-party agencies available at licensee's offices and on website.
Consumer pays requisite testing fee.

Refund of test fee (adjustment in subsequent bill) if:
- Meter found defective/burnt due to technical reasons attributable to the LICENSEE
  (voltage fluctuations, transients, etc.), OR
- Third-party test results are CONTRARY to the licensee's own test results.

Third-party test results are FINAL AND BINDING on both consumer and licensee.

**Clause 6.31 — Testing Timeline:**
Licensee must inspect and check correctness of meter within time period specified in GERC
(Standard of Performance) Regulations 2005.

**Clause 6.32 — Signing the Test Report:**
Consumer/authorised representative present during testing must sign the test report as witness.
If consumer/rep is absent: licensee's representative and testing lab official sign the report.

**Clause 6.33 — Dispatch of Test Report & Billing Adjustment:**
- Test report dispatched to consumer within 2 WORKING DAYS of testing date (under acknowledgment).
- If meter found faulty: Billing rectification for a MAXIMUM PERIOD OF 6 MONTHS or from
  the date of last testing (whichever is SHORTER) — adjusted in subsequent bill.

**Clause 6.34 — Dispute on Licensee's Test Results:**
If consumer disputes results of testing carried out at the Licensee's Testing Facility, they
may appeal to the CGRF (Consumer Grievance Redressal Forum) for adjudication.

**KEYWORDS:** meter testing, licensee testing, consumer request testing fee, third party testing,
approved third party agency, test report 2 working days, 6 months billing rectification, CGRF
dispute, third party final binding, refund test fee, voltage fluctuation, accuracy report, periodic
calibration, Standard of Performance timeline

**SOURCE:** Pages 52–55, Section 6

**Clause 6.35 — Meter Defective/Burnt Due to LICENSEE's Fault:**
- No testing fee charged from consumer when reporting defective/burnt meter.
- If defect/burning is due to technical reasons attributable to the LICENSEE
  (voltage fluctuations, transients, etc.):
  → Licensee bears COST OF METER and testing fee.

**Clause 6.36 — Meter Defective/Burnt Due to CONSUMER's Fault:**
(Causes: defect in consumer's installation, unauthorized load connection, etc.)

Sub-clause (1) — If meter was OWNED BY CONSUMER:
- Licensee informs consumer to replace meter and equipment.
- Licensee immediately replaces meter (temporarily) and charges METER RENT until
  consumer arranges their own meter.
- Testing fee recovered from consumer through subsequent bills.

Sub-clause (2) — If meter was OWNED BY LICENSEE:
- Licensee installs new meter at its own cost initially and recovers the cost from consumer.
- Testing fee also charged to consumer through subsequent bills.

Special case — TAMPERING or DELIBERATE INTERFERENCE:
If meter was rendered defective/burnt due to tampering or deliberate act by consumer:
- Consumer assessed per Clauses 7.7–7.9 (theft assessment formula).
- Legal action for pilferage and tampering also initiated.

**Clause 6.37 — Consumer Disputes Defect Test Results:**
Consumer may have meter tested at a third-party facility (approved by Commission per
Clause 6.30). If meter found defective/burnt due to licensee's fault: test fee refunded to
consumer by adjustment in subsequent bill.

**Clause 6.38 — Lost Meter — Who Bears Cost:**
Cost of new meter and apparatus borne by the CONSUMER — unless the meter was installed
at the licensee's office or sub-station (in which case licensee bears cost).

## CHUNK 71
**TITLE:** Section 6 – Replacement of Non-Recording, Burnt & Lost Meters: Procedures
**SOURCE:** Pages 54–55, Section 6
**CLAUSE:** 6.39, 6.40, 6.41, 6.42, 6.43, 6.44, 6.45, 6.46, 6.47

**Clause 6.39 — Consumer's Duty for Non-Recording Meter:**
Consumer must intimate the licensee AS SOON AS they notice the meter has stopped or
is not recording.

**Clause 6.40 — Licensee's Action on Non-Recording Meter:**
If meter is found not recording (during inspection or on consumer complaint): licensee
follows procedure in Clauses 6.31–6.33 (testing and test report within 2 working days).

**Clause 6.41 — Replacement of Non-Recording Meter:**
If meter is actually found not recording (stuck, slow, fast, or creeping): licensee replaces
it within the time period specified in GERC (Standard of Performance) Regulations 2005.

**Clause 6.42 — Burnt Meter: Immediate Restoration:**
If meter is found burnt (on consumer complaint or licensee inspection): Licensee must
RESTORE SUPPLY IMMEDIATELY by providing another meter — after taking necessary
preventive action at site to avoid future damage.
NON-AVAILABILITY OF METER WITH LICENSEE is NOT a valid reason to delay
restoration of supply.

**Clause 6.43 — Burnt Meter Due to Licensee's Fault:**
If burnt due to causes attributable to the licensee: Replace burnt meter within the time
period specified in GERC (Standard of Performance) Regulations 2005.

**Clause 6.44 — Burnt Meter Due to Consumer's Fault:**
(Causes: tampering, defect in consumer's installation, meter getting wet, unauthorized load)
- Procedure per Clause 6.36 applies for cost of meter.
- Licensee immediately replaces the meter.
- Corrective action taken to prevent further damage.
- If cost not paid by consumer: included in subsequent energy bill.

**Clause 6.45 — Lost Meter: FIR Mandatory:**
Complaints about lost meters entertained ONLY if accompanied by a copy of the FIR
(First Information Report) lodged by the consumer with the POLICE.

**Clause 6.46 — Restoring Supply for Lost Meter:**
Supply restored after:
- Installation of new meter, AND
- Payment of electricity charges for the period meter was not available (assessed per Clause 6.60), AND
- Any other prescribed charges.
NON-AVAILABILITY OF METER WITH LICENSEE is NOT a valid reason to delay
restoration of supply.

**Clause 6.47 — Consumer Must Not Interfere with Licensee's Equipment:**
Meter, cut-outs, MCB, service mains, and other licensee equipment installed at consumer's
premises must NOT be handled or removed by anyone not authorised by the licensee.
Seals on meter and metering equipment must NOT be tampered with, damaged, destroyed,
or broken.

no excuse, lost meter FIR mandatory, lost meter electricity charges, replacement timeline
Standard of Performance, consumer fault burnt, tampering burnt, meter interference prohibited,
seal tampering, stuck slow fast meter replacement

## CHUNK 72
**TITLE:** Section 6 – Billing: General Rules, Tariff Display & Bill Preparation
**SOURCE:** Pages 55–56, Section 6
**CLAUSE:** 6.48, 6.49, 6.50, 6.51, 6.52, 6.53

**Clause 6.48 — Tariff Determination & Advance Notification:**
Tariffs and charges determined by GERC from time to time. Tariff for each consumer category
must be displayed on licensee's website. Consumers must be notified of any tariff change
(including FPPPA changes) at least A FULL BILLING CYCLE AHEAD of the effective date —
through both the website and energy bills.

**Clause 6.49 — Bill Preparation:**
- Post-payment meters: Bill prepared every billing cycle based on ACTUAL meter reading.
- Pre-payment meters: Bill issued to consumer ON REQUEST only.
- Licensee must intimate consumer of bill dispatch through SMS and/or email immediately
  (if consumer has furnished contact details). Intimation must include bill amount and due date.
- Bill uploaded on licensee's website on the DAY OF BILL GENERATION.
- Billing details of last ONE YEAR (including latest bill) available on website with payment
  receipt details.

**Clause 6.50 — Bill Distributor Identification:**
Licensee must have the bill distributor's name rubber-stamped on the bill. Bill distributor
must write down the delivery date on the bill before handing it to the consumer.

**Clause 6.51 — First Bill for New Connection:**
For post-payment meters: Licensee must issue the FIRST BILL within 2 BILLING CYCLES
of releasing the connection.
If consumer does not receive first bill within 2 billing cycles: Consumer MAY complain in
writing; licensee must issue the bill within the next 7 DAYS.

**Clause 6.52 — Bill Delivery Timeline:**
- Spot billing: Delivered immediately.
- All other cases: Delivered by hand/post/electronic mode at least 10 DAYS before the
  due date of payment.

**Clause 6.53 — Duplicate Bill:**
If consumer does not receive bill within 7 DAYS of normal bill issue date:
- Consumer may obtain a duplicate bill from the billing office.
- Licensee issues duplicate bill IMMEDIATELY (in person or by phone) or on the date of
  acknowledgment (if by post).
- Non-receipt of bill does NOT entitle consumer to delay payment beyond the due date.
- If any bill is served with a delay of 60 DAYS: Consumer entitled to a REBATE OF 2%.

## CHUNK 74
**TITLE:** Section 6 – Billing Under Special Circumstances: Inaccessible Meter & Defective Meter
**SOURCE:** Pages 58–59, Section 6
**CLAUSE:** 6.57, 6.58, 6.59

**Clause 6.57 — Provisional Billing When Meter Not Accessible:**
If licensee cannot read the meter AND consumer has not sent meter photo (per Clause 6.21):
- Provisional bill issued on basis of AVERAGE CONSUMPTION of previous 3 BILLING CYCLES.
- Maximum: 2 BILLING CYCLES at a stretch (provisional billing must not continue longer).
- Maximum: 2 PROVISIONAL BILLS per financial year (except force majeure).
- Provisional bills adjusted against subsequent actual meter reading.

**Clause 6.58 — Billing When Meter is Defective / Stuck / Stopped / Burnt:**
Consumer billed on basis of AVERAGE CONSUMPTION of PAST 3 BILLING CYCLES
immediately preceding the date the meter was found/reported defective.
- If insufficient data available: Use average of 2–3 billing cycles of the SUCCEEDING
  period (next available data).
- Maximum assessable period: 3 BILLING CYCLES only.
- Consumer may submit evidence of working/occupancy conditions during the relevant period;
  licensee may consider this in billing.

**Clause 6.59 — Billing When MDI is Faulty:**
If Maximum Demand Indicator (MDI) of consumer's meter is faulty or not recording
(and not tampered):
- Demand charges calculated based on maximum demand during CORRESPONDING
  MONTHS/BILLING CYCLE of the PREVIOUS YEAR (when meter was functional).
- If previous year's MDI data is also unavailable: Use average maximum demand for
  whatever period is available.

## CHUNK 75
**TITLE:** Section 6 – Billing for Lost Meters, Vacancy / Change of Occupancy & Self-Assessment
**SOURCE:** Pages 59–60, Section 6
**CLAUSE:** 6.60, 6.61, 6.62, 6.63, 6.64, 6.65

**Clause 6.60 — Billing When Meter is Lost:**
Electricity charges for the period meter was not available assessed as:
1. Per Clauses 7.7–7.9 (THEFT assessment formula) — if licensee's enquiry establishes the
   loss was a DELIBERATE ACT of the consumer or with their connivance.
2. Per Clauses 6.58 and 6.59 (average of past cycles / previous year MDI) — in ALL OTHER
   cases.

**Clause 6.61 — Special Reading on Vacancy / Change of Occupancy (Domestic):**
Consumer's responsibility to get a special meter reading done by the licensee at the time
of change of occupancy or when premises become vacant. Consumer must also obtain a
No-Dues Certificate from the licensee.

**Clause 6.62 — Request Timeline for Special Reading:**
Consumer must request in writing at least 15 DAYS before the vacancy or change of
occupancy. Licensee may accept a notice of SHORTER PERIOD at its discretion.

**Clause 6.63 — Licensee's Actions on Special Reading Request:**
- Licensee arranges special reading within 7 WORKING DAYS of receiving written request.
- Issues FINAL BILL (including all arrears till date of billing) at least 7 WORKING DAYS
  BEFORE the vacancy/change of occupancy date.
- Final bill must state: "No other dues pending on the premises; this bill is final."
- Final bill includes pro-rata charges for the period between special reading date and
  vacancy date.

**Clause 6.64 — After Final Bill is Raised:**
- Licensee has NO RIGHT to recover any charges for any period prior to the date of the final bill.
- Licensee disconnects supply on vacancy of premises.
- Consumer must make final payment on vacating; licensee issues No-Dues Certificate.
- In case of CHANGE OF OCCUPANCY (not vacancy): Connection is NOT disconnected;
  change of name formalities completed and effected.

**Clause 6.65 — Payment on Self-Assessment:**
If consumer does not receive a bill: Consumer may deposit a SELF-ASSESSED amount,
provided it is not LESS THAN the AVERAGE CONSUMPTION over the last 6 MONTHS.
Any excess or deficient payment adjusted in the next bill.

## CHUNK 76
**TITLE:** Section 6 – Advance Payment for Absent Domestic Consumer (Clause 6.66 & 6.67)
**SOURCE:** Page 60, Section 6
**CLAUSE:** 6.66, 6.67

**Clause 6.66 — Protection for Domestic Consumer Who Is Absent (Post-Payment Meters):**
When a domestic consumer gives prior information IN WRITING about continued absence
from residence:
- Licensee shall NOT send any notice or provisional bill.
- Supply line shall NOT be disconnected.
- Condition: Consumer must pay the FIXED CHARGES for the absence period IN ADVANCE.

**Clause 6.67 — How Much to Deposit:**
Consumer must deposit an amount covering the FIXED CHARGES for the ENTIRE DURATION
of the proposed absence. This provisional payment is adjusted when the subsequent bill is
issued based on actual meter reading after return.

**Key Points:**
- This provision applies ONLY to DOMESTIC consumers.
- Applies to POST-PAYMENT meters only.
- Consumer must be up-to-date on all dues to avail this facility (per Clause 6.25).
- Meter must be made accessible for reading at least ONCE EVERY 6 MONTHS (per Clause 6.25).

## CHUNK 81 — CRITICAL COMPARISONS (Pages 46–60)
**TITLE:** Key Distinctions – Easily Confused Rules in Section 6 (Pages 46–60)
**SOURCE:** Pages 46–60, Section 6 (Synthesised Reference Chunk)
**CLAUSE:** Multiple

**1. Smart Pre-Payment Meter vs Other Pre-Payment Meter (Reading Frequency):**
- Smart pre-payment meter: Read remotely at least ONCE A MONTH
- Other pre-payment meters: Read at least ONCE EVERY THREE MONTHS
- Regular meters: Read on fixed date ±3 days (monthly) or ±5 days (bi-monthly)

**2. Provisional Bill (Inaccessible Meter) vs Assessed Bill (Defective Meter):**
- Inaccessible meter: Provisional bill based on avg of 3 cycles; max 2 cycles in a stretch
- Defective/stuck/burnt meter: Bill based on avg of 3 cycles PRECEDING defect date; max 3 BILLING CYCLES total
- Key difference: Inaccessible = 2 cycles max; Defective = 3 cycles max

**3. Consumer Procures Own Meter vs Licensee Supplies Meter:**
- Consumer procures: No meter rent; consumer buys from approved vendor; licensee installs/seals
- Licensee supplies: Meter rent charged; included in estimate; licensee installs/seals

**4. Check Meter vs Main Meter:**
- Main meter: Used for ALL billing; sealed by licensee
- Check meter: Consumer's own; used for billing ONLY when main meter FAILS
- Check meter calibrated by licensee on fee payment; readings not used for routine billing

**5. Vacant Premises vs Change of Occupancy (After Special Reading):**
- Vacant premises: Supply DISCONNECTED after final bill; no-dues certificate issued
- Change of occupancy: Supply NOT disconnected; change of name formalities completed

**6. Lost Meter — FIR vs No FIR:**
- Without FIR: Complaint not entertained
- With FIR: Complaint accepted; supply restored after new meter + payment of charges
- Non-availability of meter with licensee is NOT a reason to delay restoration

**7. Burnt Meter — Immediate Supply vs Scheduled Replacement:**
- Burnt meter: Licensee IMMEDIATELY restores supply with another meter (not scheduled)
- Non-recording meter: Replaced within Standard of Performance timeline (scheduled)
- Non-availability of replacement meter is NOT an excuse for delay in burnt meter cases

**8. First Bill for New Connection vs Pre-Payment:**
- Post-payment: First bill within 2 BILLING CYCLES of connection release
- Pre-payment: Bill issued ON CONSUMER'S REQUEST only (no automatic billing cycle)

**KEYWORDS:** smart meter vs pre-payment reading frequency, provisional vs assessed billing,
2 cycles vs 3 cycles, consumer meter vs licensee meter rent, check meter vs main meter, vacant
vs change of occupancy disconnection, lost meter FIR, burnt meter immediate vs non-recording
scheduled, post-payment first bill vs pre-payment on request
