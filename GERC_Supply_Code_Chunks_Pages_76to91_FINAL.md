## CHUNK 102
**TITLE:** Section 9 – Failure & Interruption of Supply: 24×7 Obligation, Force Majeure & Maintenance
**SOURCE:** Pages 76–77, Section 9
**CLAUSE:** 9.2, 9.3, 9.4

**Clause 9.2 — 24×7 Supply Obligation:**
The Distribution Licensee SHALL supply 24×7 power to ALL consumers.
Exception: Commission may specify LOWER HOURS for certain consumer categories
(e.g., agriculture).

**Licensee NOT Responsible for Loss Due to Interruptions/Fluctuations Caused By:**
(unless there is a specific contract to the contrary)
- War, mutiny, riot, earthquake, cyclone, tempest, strike, civil commotion, lock-out
- Lightning, fire, flood, accident, or breakdown of plant and machinery
- Load shedding as directed by SLDC
- Any other cause beyond the Distribution Licensee's reasonable control

**Communication Obligation:**
Licensee must communicate interruption details through SMS or other electronic mode
along with the ESTIMATED TIME FOR RESTORATION. This information must also be
available at the licensee's call centre.

**Clause 9.3 — Emergency Curtailment:**
The Distribution Licensee may curtail, stagger, or cut off supply for such period as
necessary if the power position or any other technical emergency warrants such action.

**Clause 9.4 — Planned Outages for Maintenance:**
The Distribution Licensee is always entitled to temporarily discontinue supply for testing,
outages, maintenance, or any other cause for efficient working of the undertaking.
Licensee must inform the consumer through SMS or other electronic mode along with
the ESTIMATED TIME FOR RESUMPTION OF SUPPLY. This information must also be
available at the call centre.

**KEYWORDS:** 24x7 supply obligation, force majeure interruption, SLDC load shedding,
estimated time restoration, SMS electronic notification, call centre outage, emergency
curtailment, planned maintenance outage, no liability interruption fluctuation

## CHUNK 103
**TITLE:** Section 9 – Load Shedding, Harmonic Distortion, Parallel Generation & Phase Splitters
**SOURCE:** Pages 76–77, Section 9
**CLAUSE:** 9.5, 9.6, 9.7, 9.8, 9.9

**Clause 9.5 — Load Shedding Compliance:**
Regardless of any agreement or tariff, consumers must restrict electricity use in terms of
maximum demand and/or stagger consumption as directed by:
- State Load Dispatch Centre (SLDC)
- State Government
- The licensee
...for maintaining orderly grid operation. Licensee must inform bulk/HT consumers about
such restrictions as early as possible.

**Clause 9.6 — Harmonic Distortion (THD) Limits:**
Voltage and current Harmonic Distortion (THD) at any installation must NOT exceed limits
specified in CEA (Technical Standards for Connectivity to the Grid) Regulations 2007.
Consumer must take appropriate measures to restrict THD to the stipulated limit.

**Clause 9.7 — Specific THD Limits:**
- Voltage THD at connection point: Must NOT exceed 5%
- Individual harmonic: Must NOT exceed 3%
- Current THD drawn from transmission system at connection point: Must NOT exceed 8%

**Clause 9.8 — Parallel Operation with Grid:**
Users operating a power generating plant in parallel with the grid must:
- Comply with the terms and conditions of the connectivity agreement with the Distribution
  Licensee.
- Promptly comply with any direction from SLDC or the Distribution Licensee for orderly
  grid operation.

**Clause 9.9 — Phase Splitters (Tetas) Prohibited:**
Consumer must NOT keep connected to the licensee's supply system any apparatus
including PHASE SPLITTERS (known as "tetas" in Gujarati) that may — in the
Distribution Licensee's opinion — interfere with or injuriously affect supply to other
consumers or disturb the distribution system in the short or long term.

**KEYWORDS:** load shedding SLDC, maximum demand restriction, harmonic distortion THD,
voltage THD 5%, current THD 8%, individual harmonic 3%, parallel generation grid, SLDC
directions, phase splitter teta prohibited, CEA Technical Standards, connectivity agreement

## CHUNK 104
**TITLE:** Section 9 – Demand Side Management, Consumer Grievances & Standards of Performance
**SOURCE:** Pages 77, Section 9
**CLAUSE:** 9.10, 9.11, 9.12, 9.13

**Clause 9.10 — Demand Side Management (DSM):**
Every consumer has a duty to stop wastage and inefficient use of electricity and to extend
necessary cooperation to the licensee in implementing Demand Side Management (DSM)
programs launched by the licensee.

**Clause 9.11 — Consumer Grievance Redressal:**
Consumers with complaints about implementation of this Code may:
1. First approach the LOCAL OFFICE of the Distribution Licensee.
2. If not satisfied: Approach the CONSUMER GRIEVANCES REDRESSAL FORUM (CGRF)
   or ELECTRICITY OMBUDSMAN as per GERC (Consumer Grievance Redressal Forum
   and Ombudsman) Regulations 2011 and amendments.

**Clause 9.12 — Force Majeure Exemption:**
Standards of performance specified in these Regulations remain SUSPENDED during Force
Majeure conditions including: war, mutiny, civil commotion, riot, flood, cyclone, lightning,
earthquake, lockout, fire, etc. — that affect the licensee's installations and activities.

**Clause 9.13 — Transmission System Faults — No Licensee Liability:**
Non-compliance of any standard in these Regulations is NOT treated as a violation and the
licensee is NOT required to pay compensation to consumers if the violation is caused by:
- State Transmission Utility (STU) or Central Transmission Utility (CTU)
- Grid failure
- Fault on the Transmission Licensee's network
- Instructions given by SLDC
...over which the Distribution Licensee has NO REASONABLE CONTROL.

**KEYWORDS:** demand side management, consumer duty energy efficiency, consumer grievances
local office, CGRF Ombudsman, GERC Consumer Grievance Redressal Regulations 2011,
force majeure standards suspended, STU CTU grid failure no liability, transmission fault no
compensation, SLDC instructions no violation

## CHUNK 105
**TITLE:** Section 9 – Service of Notice: Licensee to Consumer & Consumer to Licensee
**SOURCE:** Pages 77–78, Section 9
**CLAUSE:** 9.14, 9.15, 9.16

**Clause 9.14 — How Licensee Serves Notice on Consumer:**
Any order or notice (including Section 56 disconnection notice) from the licensee is deemed
DULY SERVED if:
- Sent by REGISTERED POST to the consumer's correct postal address, OR
- Delivered BY HAND to the person at the address notified by the consumer to the licensee.

**Sufficient service also includes:**
- For an INDIVIDUAL: Notice served on the consumer's SPOUSE or authorised representative.
- For a FIRM / COMPANY / CORPORATION: Notice served on the Managing Director,
  Director, Principal Officer, or an authorised person of the concern.

**Clause 9.15 — How Consumer Gives Notice to Licensee:**
Any communication or notice from the consumer to the Distribution Licensee is deemed duly
given if:
- Served IN WRITING addressed to the Distribution Licensee, AND
- Hand delivered OR sent by post or any other mode permitted under law, TO THE OFFICE
  FROM WHERE THE BILL IS SERVED to the consumer.

**Clause 9.16 — When Consumer Refuses to Receive Notice:**
If a consumer refuses or avoids receiving a notice, service may be effected by EITHER:
1. Affixing the notice at a conspicuous place on the consumer's premises in the presence of
   TWO WITNESSES and photographing the notice; OR
2. Publication of the notice in daily newspaper(s) commonly read in the concerned locality
   (kept on record by the licensee).

Either of the above alternatives is deemed as SUFFICIENT service of notice.

**KEYWORDS:** service of notice, registered post, hand delivery, Section 56 notice service,
spouse authorised representative, Managing Director Director, consumer notice to licensee,
billing office notice, consumer refuses notice, affixing notice two witnesses, newspaper notice,
deemed served

## CHUNK 106
**TITLE:** Section 9 – Multiple Violations, Repeal & Savings, Interpretation, Relaxation & Commission Powers
**SOURCE:** Pages 78–79, Section 9
**CLAUSE:** 9.17, 9.18, 9.19, 9.20, 9.21, 9.22, 9.23, 9.24, 9.25

**Clause 9.17 — Multiple Violations:**
If a consumer commits MORE THAN ONE VIOLATION on the same service connection:
- Each violation is dealt with SEPARATELY.
- Penalty is levied SEPARATELY for each violation.

**Clause 9.18 — Repeal:**
The earlier GERC (Electricity Supply Code and Related Matters) Regulations 2005 and
subsequent amendments are REPEALED by this Code, except as provided in Clause 9.19.

**Clause 9.19 — Savings:**
1. Anything done or proceedings initiated under the repealed Regulations is deemed to have
   been done under this Code (to the extent not inconsistent with the Act).
2. The Commission may at any time amend, alter, modify any provision of this Code or
   remove any error or defect.

**Clause 9.20 — Interpretation:**
All provisions of this Code must be read and construed subject to the Electricity Act 2003
and rules thereunder, plus any restrictions and control orders in force. Nothing in these
Regulations shall abridge or prejudice the rights or remedies of the Distribution Licensee
or Consumer under any Act or law in force.

**Clause 9.21 — Power of Relaxation:**
The Commission may, IN PUBLIC INTEREST and for REASONS RECORDED IN WRITING,
relax any provision of this Code.

**Clause 9.22 — Power to Remove Difficulties:**
If any difficulty arises in giving effect to any provision, or if there is a dispute regarding
interpretation, the matter may be referred expeditiously to the Commission. The Commission
passes necessary orders after consulting the parties concerned.
IMPORTANT: Commission may REFUSE to entertain a reference filed with MORE THAN
3 MONTHS' DELAY without sufficient cause.

**Clauses 9.23, 9.24, 9.25 — Inherent Powers of the Commission:**
- Nothing in this Code limits GERC's inherent power to make orders necessary for ends of
  justice or to prevent abuse of process.
- GERC may adopt a procedure at variance with this Code in special circumstances (with
  written reasons).
- GERC may deal with matters for which no Code has been framed, in any manner it thinks fit.

**KEYWORDS:** multiple violations separate penalty, repeal Supply Code 2005, savings clause,
Electricity Act 2003 construed, Commission power relaxation public interest, difficulty removal

## CHUNK 112
**TITLE:** Annexure IV – Theft/UUE Assessment Formula: Complete Reference
**SOURCE:** Pages 90–91, Annexure IV
**CLAUSE:** Annexure IV

## Theft / UUE Energy Assessment Formula

**Formula:**
```
Units Assessed = L × H × F
```

**L = Load (in kW):**
- For NON-DEMAND BASED consumers: Connected load found at the premises during inspection (in kW).
- For DEMAND BASED consumers: L = 75% of total connected load at time of inspection OR
  the contracted demand — WHICHEVER IS HIGHER.

**H = Number of Hours:**
Number of hours of the days in the period of assessment as per Clause 7.7 of the Supply Code.

**F = Load Factor:**
Load factor varies by consumer category and usage pattern:

| Sr. No. | Consumer Category | Usage | Load Factor |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | HT Industries, Railway Traction, HT Agriculture | Continuous process | 100% |
| 1 | HT Industries, Railway Traction, HT Agriculture | I Shift (8 hrs) | 30% |
| 1 | HT Industries, Railway Traction, HT Agriculture | II Shift (16 hrs) | 60% |
| 1 | HT Industries, Railway Traction, HT Agriculture | III Shift (24 hrs) | 90% |
| 2 | HT Non-residential | — | 50% |
| 3 | HT Residential | — | 13% |
| 4 | LT Industrial | Continuous process | 100% |
| 4 | LT Industrial | I Shift (8 hrs) | 25% |
| 4 | LT Industrial | II Shift (16 hrs) | 38% |
| 4 | LT Industrial | III Shift (24 hrs) | 75% |
| 5 | LT Non-residential | — | 45% |
| 6 | LT Residential | Appliances other than AC | 13% |
| 6 | LT Residential | AC (Feb–Oct) | 50% |
| 6 | LT Residential | AC (Nov–Jan) | 0% |
| 7 | Agriculture Metered — Agriculture dominant feeder | — | 25% |
| 7 | Agriculture Metered — Other feeder | — | 50% |
| 8 | Water Supply and Sewage Pumping | — | 50% |
| 9 | Street Light / Public Lighting | — | 50% |
| 10 | Temporary Power Supply | — | 50% |
| 11 | **Direct Theft — ALL categories** | — | **1.25 × Load Factor of relevant category (max 100%)** |

**Direct Theft Multiplier:**
For DIRECT THEFT (all categories): Load factor = 1.25 times the applicable load factor from
Sr. Nos. 1–10, subject to a MAXIMUM OF 100%.

**Example Calculation:**
LT Industrial consumer (2 shifts = 16 hrs) — theft detected, period = 6 months (~4380 hrs):
- L = Connected load found (say 50 kW)
- H = 4380 hours
- F = 38% (LT Industrial, II Shift)
- Units Assessed = 50 × 4380 × 0.38 = 83,220 kWh
- Assessment at 2× tariff applies (per Clause 7.12)

**KEYWORDS:** Annexure IV, assessment formula, theft formula, L × H × F, load factor table,
HT industrial 30% 60% 90%, LT industrial 25% 38% 75%, LT residential 13%, agriculture
25% 50%, direct theft 1.25 times, demand based 75% connected load contracted demand,
water supply 50%, street light 50%, temporary 50%, AC residential Feb Oct Nov Jan

**Load Shedding (9.5):**
- Consumer must comply with SLDC/State Govt/licensee directions on restricting demand.

**Harmonic Distortion (9.6–9.7):**
- Consumer must keep THD within CEA limits.
- Voltage THD ≤ 5% (no individual harmonic > 3%); Current THD ≤ 8%.

**Parallel Generation (9.8):** Must comply with connectivity agreement and SLDC directions.

**Phase Splitters (9.9):** Prohibited if they interfere with supply to other consumers.

**DSM (9.10):** Consumer duty to stop waste and cooperate with DSM programs.

**Grievance Redressal (9.11):** Local office → CGRF → Electricity Ombudsman.

**Force Majeure Exemption (9.12):** Standards suspended during war, flood, cyclone, etc.

**Transmission Fault Exemption (9.13):** No licensee liability for STU/CTU/grid/SLDC faults.

**Service of Notice (9.14–9.16):**
- Licensee to consumer: Registered post or hand delivery; spouse/MD/Director also valid.
- Consumer to licensee: Written to the billing office, hand delivery or post.
- If consumer refuses: Affix at premises (2 witnesses + photo) OR newspaper publication.

**Multiple Violations (9.17):** Each violation on same connection dealt with and penalised separately.

**Repeal & Savings (9.18–9.19):** 2005 Code repealed; proceedings under old code deemed valid.

**Interpretation (9.20):** Subject to Electricity Act 2003; licensee/consumer rights preserved.

**Relaxation (9.21):** Commission may relax any provision in public interest.

**Difficulty Removal (9.22):** Refer to Commission; 3-month delay may be refused.

**Inherent Powers (9.23–9.25):** GERC's inherent powers unaffected; may act in special circumstances; may deal with un-coded matters.

## CHUNK 119 — CRITICAL COMPARISONS (Pages 76–91)
**TITLE:** Key Distinctions – Easily Confused Rules in Section 9 & Annexures (Pages 76–91)
**SOURCE:** Pages 76–91 (Synthesised Reference Chunk)
**CLAUSE:** Multiple

**1. Force Majeure (Section 9.12) vs Transmission Fault Exemption (Section 9.13):**
- Force Majeure (9.12): Standards SUSPENDED due to natural/civil events (war, flood, earthquake,
  cyclone, fire, riot, lockout) affecting the licensee's own installations.
- Transmission fault (9.13): No compensation payable if violation caused by STU/CTU/grid/SLDC —
  this is not force majeure; it is a specific external-cause exemption.

**2. 24×7 Supply Obligation vs No Liability for Interruptions:**
- Obligation (9.2): Licensee SHALL supply 24×7.
- Limitation: Licensee NOT responsible for losses due to force majeure or SLDC load shedding.
- Both coexist: Obligation exists, but liability for damage does not arise from force-majeure causes.

**3. Form A-1 vs Form A-2 — Who Uses What:**
- Form A-1 (LT): 9 service types; suitable for residential, commercial, industrial, agriculture ≤ LT
- Form A-2 (HT/EHT): 4 service types; suitable for HT/EHT industrial/commercial consumers
- Key difference: Form A-2 requires a MAP, project report, Industries Dept. registration, Tariff Order
  copy — none of these are required in Form A-1.

**4. Annexure III vs Annexure IV:**
- Annexure III: Tariff Schedule — filled by licensee annually; different per DISCOM; dynamic.
- Annexure IV: Theft/UUE formula — fixed formula; same across all DISCOMs; used by Assessing Officer.

**5. THD Voltage Limit vs THD Current Limit:**
- Voltage THD at connection point: ≤ 5% overall; no individual harmonic > 3%.
- Current THD from transmission system: ≤ 8%.
- These apply simultaneously and independently.

**6. Consumer Duty (DSM/THD) vs Licensee Duty (24×7 Supply):**
- Consumer duty: DSM cooperation, THD restriction, load shedding compliance.
- Licensee duty: 24×7 supply, SMS notification of outages, estimated restoration time, call centre info.

**7. Service of Notice — Licensee to Consumer vs Consumer to Licensee:**
- Licensee to consumer: Registered post or hand delivery to consumer's address; spouse/MD also valid.
- Consumer to licensee: Written notice to the BILLING OFFICE (where bill is served from); hand delivery or post.

**8. Deemed Termination vs Permanent Disconnection:**
- Deemed termination (8.6): After 180 continuous days of disconnection (not consumer-requested),
  agreement is deemed terminated after 15-day written notice.
- Permanent disconnection (8.6): Initiated when cause of temporary disconnection not removed within
  180 days — this is a deliberate licensee action, not automatic deemed termination.

**KEYWORDS:** force majeure vs transmission fault, 24x7 obligation vs no liability, Form A-1 vs
A-2, Annexure III vs IV, THD voltage vs current, consumer duty vs licensee duty, notice
licensee to consumer vs consumer to licensee, deemed termination vs permanent disconnection
