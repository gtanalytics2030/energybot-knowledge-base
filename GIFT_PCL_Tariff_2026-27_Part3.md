## CHUNK 18 — Rate Railway Traction (132 kV/66 kV)

**DOCUMENT:** Tariff Schedule for FY 2026-27, GIFT PCL
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Tariff Schedule
**HIERARCHY:** Part II — High Tension and Extra High Tension Tariffs
**PROVISION:** Rate Railway Traction, Clauses 16–16.8.2
**TITLE:** Tariff for Railway Traction at 132 kV / 66 kV (Rate Railway Traction)
**EFFECTIVE DATE:** 1st April 2026

**SUMMARY:**
The Railway Traction tariff applies specifically to power supply for railway traction at 132 kV/66 kV. Demand charge is Rs. 180/kVA/month (within contract demand) or Rs. 425/kVA/month (excess). Energy charge is 500 paise/unit. A special note provides that excess demand charges may be waived when demand exceeds contract demand due to load transfer from an adjacent supply point (DISCOM non-availability or maintenance), or due to train bunching, subject to satisfactory documentary proof by Railways. Billing demand, minimum bill, maximum demand, contract demand, and HV/EHV rebates follow HTP-I. Power factor penalty and rebate provisions are the same structure as other HT tariffs.

**KEY TOPICS:** Railway traction tariff, 132 kV, 66 kV, demand charges, energy charges, excess demand, load transfer, train bunching, power factor adjustment, HV rebate

**TEXT:**

**16. RATE: RAILWAY TRACTION**

This tariff is applicable for power supply to Railway Traction at 132 kV/66 kV.

**16.1. DEMAND CHARGES:**

| Demand Category | Rate |
|---|---|
| (a) For billing demand up to the contract demand | Rs. 180 per kVA per month |
| (b) For billing demand in excess of contract demand | Rs. 425 per kVA per month |

**NOTE:** In case of the load transfer for traction supply due to non-availability of power supply at preceding or succeeding point of supply or maintenance at DISCOM's level, excess demand over the contract demand shall be charged at normal rate at appropriate point of supply.

Normal Demand Charges will also apply in case of bunching of trains. However, DISCOMs shall charge excess demand charges while raising the bills and Railways have to give convincing details and documentary proof of bunching of trains if they want to be charged at the normal demand charges. If satisfactory proof of bunching of trains is provided, DISCOM shall consider that occasion for normal demand charges, otherwise excess demand charges will be applicable specified as above at 16.1(b).

**PLUS**

**16.2. ENERGY CHARGES:**
For all the units consumed during the month: **500 Paise per Unit**

**16.3. Billing Demand:**
**16.4. Minimum Bill**
**16.5. Maximum demand and its measurement** — Same as HTP-I Tariff (see Clause 11.4, 11.5, 11.7)
**16.6. Contract Demand** — Same as HTP-I (see Clause 11.8)
**16.7. Rebate for supply at HV and EHV** — Same as HTP-I (see Clause 11.9)

**16.8. POWER FACTOR ADJUSTMENT CHARGES**

16.8.1. Penalty for poor Power Factor:
a) The power factor adjustment charges shall be levied at the rate of 1% on the total amount of electricity bills for the month under the head "Energy Charges", arrived at using tariff as per para 16.2 of this schedule, for every 1% drop or part thereof in the average power factor during the month below 90% up to 85%.
b) In addition to the above clause, for every 1% drop or part thereof in average power factor during the month below 85% at the rate of 2% on the total amount of electricity bill for that month under the head "Energy Charges", arrived at using tariff as per para 16.2 of this schedule, will be charged.

16.8.2. Power Factor Rebate:
If the power factor of the consumer's installation in any month is above 95%, the consumer will be entitled to a rebate at the rate of 0.5% (half percent) in excess of 95% power factor on the total amount of electricity bill for that month under the head "Energy Charges", arrived at using tariff as per para 16.2 of this schedule, for every 1% rise or part thereof in the average power factor during the month above 95%.

**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** HTP-I (Clauses 11.4–11.9) — incorporated by reference; Clause 16.1(b) — excess demand charges for bunching of trains

## CHUNK 19 — Rate HT EV Charging Stations

**DOCUMENT:** Tariff Schedule for FY 2026-27, GIFT PCL
**DOCUMENT TYPE:** Tariff Schedule
**HIERARCHY:** Part II — High Tension and Extra High Tension Tariffs
**PROVISION:** Rate HT Electric Vehicle (EV) Charging Stations, Clauses 17–17.9
**TITLE:** HT Tariff for Electric Vehicle Charging Stations (Rate HT EV Charging)
**EFFECTIVE DATE:** 1st April 2026

**SUMMARY:**
Rate HT EV Charging applies exclusively to HT consumers using electricity only for EV charging. Other HT consumers may use their regular category (HTP-I through HTP-V, Railway Traction). Demand charge is Rs. 25/kVA/month (within contract demand) or Rs. 50/kVA/month (excess). Energy charge is 400 paise/unit. A peak ToU surcharge of 45 paise/unit applies during 0700–1100 and 1800–2200 hrs. Off-peak discount of 60 paise/unit applies during 1100–1700 hrs. Billing demand, minimum bill, maximum demand measurement, contract demand, and HV/EHV rebates follow HTP-I provisions.

**KEY TOPICS:** HT EV charging, electric vehicle, HT demand charges, energy charges, ToU charges, ToU discount, contract demand, billing demand, HV rebate

**TEXT:**

**17. RATE: HT ELECTRIC VEHICLE (EV) CHARGING STATIONS**

This tariff is applicable to consumers who use electricity exclusively for Electric Vehicle Charging installations.

Other consumers can use their regular electricity supply for charging electric vehicle under same regular category i.e. HTP-I, HTP-II, HTP-III, HTP-IV, HTP-V, RAILWAY TRACTION as the case may be.

**17.1. DEMAND CHARGES:**

| Demand Category | Rate |
|---|---|
| (a) For billing demand up to the contract demand | Rs. 25/- per kVA per month |
| (b) For billing demand in excess of contract demand | Rs. 50/- per kVA per month |

**PLUS**

**17.2. ENERGY CHARGES: FOR THE TOTAL MONTHLY CONSUMPTION**
Energy Charge: **400 Paise per Unit**

**PLUS**

**17.3. TIME OF USE CHARGES:**
Additional Charge for energy consumption during two peak periods, viz. 0700 Hrs to 1100 Hrs and 1800 Hrs to 2200 Hrs: **45 Paise per Unit**

**17.4. TIME OF USE DISCOUNT**
Concession of 60 Paise per Unit shall be applicable for the consumption of energy during 1100 Hrs to 1700 Hrs.

**17.5. Billing Demand:**
**17.6. Minimum Bill**
**17.7. Maximum demand and its measurement** — Same as HTP-I Tariff (see Clause 11.4, 11.5, 11.7)
**17.8. Contract Demand** — Same as HTP-I (see Clause 11.8)
**17.9. Rebate for supply at HV and EHV** — Same as HTP-I (see Clause 11.9)

**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:** HTP-I (Clauses 11.4–11.9) — incorporated by reference; HTP-I through HTP-V and Railway Traction — applicable for regular consumers charging EVs under existing HT categories

**TEXT:**

### Smart Pre-paid Meter Billing — Clause 17

Energy charges for smart pre-paid meter consumers are applicable only when a positive balance is maintained.

Any deviation from this (i.e., negative balance) leads the consumer to follow the **post-paid meter tariff** for the respective billing month.

*Effect: Pre-paid consumers with insufficient balance are automatically billed at the higher post-paid rates for that month.*

### Green Power Tariff — Clause 18

| Parameter | Details |
|---|---|
| Surcharge | Rs. 0.75/kWh (75 paise per unit), **over and above** normal category tariff |
| Eligible Consumers | All consumers — Extra High Voltage, High Voltage, and Low Voltage |
| Purpose | Meeting consumer's demand through green/renewable energy |
| Opt-in Process | Consumer gives one month's notice in writing to Distribution Licensee before commencement of billing period |

**Example:** An RGP consumer consuming 200 units/month opting for Green Power would pay normal RGP energy charges plus an additional Rs. 0.75/unit (Rs. 150/month extra for 200 units).
