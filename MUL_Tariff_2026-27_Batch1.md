## CHUNK 01 — General Conditions (Clauses 1–11)

**DOCUMENT:**
Tariff Schedule for License Area of MPSEZ Utilities Ltd. (MUL) — FY 2026-27

**DOCUMENT TYPE:**
Tariff Schedule / Tariff Order (Annexure)

**HIERARCHY:**
Tariff Schedule → General Conditions → Clauses 1–11

**PROVISION:**
General Conditions, Clauses 1–11

**TITLE:**
General Conditions of Supply — Applicability, Billing Period, Contract Demand, and Maximum Demand

**SUMMARY:**
This chunk covers the foundational conditions governing MUL's tariff schedule effective 1 April 2026 for the Mundra SEZ license area. It sets out: applicability to all MUL consumers; single-point supply rule; 10-day payment window with 15% p.a. delayed payment charge; purpose-of-use restriction; applicability of GERC Miscellaneous Charges Regulations 2005 (except Meter Charges); monthly billing basis; kW–BHP conversion rate (0.746 kW = 1 BHP); billing in 0.5 HP/kW multiples; connected load definition; and Contract Demand definition as the maximum KVA MUL undertakes to supply.

**KEY TOPICS:**
Tariff applicability, Mundra SEZ, single-point supply, delayed payment charges, purpose of use, GERC Miscellaneous Charges Regulations 2005, billing period, kW to BHP conversion, Contract Demand, Connected Load, billing multiples

**TEXT:**
General Conditions

1. This tariff schedule is applicable to all the consumers of MUL in License area of Mundra SEZ.
2. All these tariffs for power supply are applicable to only one point of supply.
3. The energy bills shall be paid by the consumer within 10 days from the date of billing, failing which the consumer shall be liable to pay the delayed payment charges @15% p.a. for the number of days from the due date of bill to the date of payment of bill.
4. The power supplied to any consumer shall be utilized only for the purpose for which supply is taken and as provided for in the tariff.
5. The various provisions of the GERC (Licensee's power to recover expenditure incurred in providing supply and other miscellaneous charges) Regulations, 2005 except Meter Charges, will continue to apply.
6. The charges specified in the tariff are on monthly basis, MUL shall adjust the rates according to billing period applicable to consumer.
7. Conversion of Ratings of electrical appliances and equipment from kilowatt to B.H.P. or vice versa will be done, when necessary, at the rate of 0.746 kilowatt equal to 1 B.H.P.
8. The billing of fixed charges based on contracted load or maximum demand shall be done in multiples of 0.5 (one half) Horse Power or kilo-Watt (HP or kW) as the case may be.
9. The fraction of less than 0.5 shall be rounded to next 0.5. The billing of energy charges will be done on complete one kilo-watt-hour (kWh).
10. The Connected Load for the purpose of billing will be taken as the maximum load connected during the billing period.
11. Contract Demand shall mean the maximum KVA for the supply which MUL undertakes to provide facilities to the consumer from time to time.

**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:**
- GERC (Licensee's power to recover expenditure incurred in providing supply and other miscellaneous charges) Regulations, 2005
- Electricity Act, 2003 (implied — MUL operates under it)

**TITLE:**
General Conditions — Fixed Charges Computation, Excess Demand, Broken Period, FPPAS, Duties, and Power Factor

**SUMMARY:**
This chunk covers billing mechanics and penalty provisions for MUL consumers. Fixed charges are computed on 85% of Contract Demand or actual demand, whichever is higher. Payment of excess-demand penal charges does not confer a right to draw beyond contract demand. Fixed, minimum, and demand charges are not adjusted for broken billing periods. The Fuel and Power Purchase Adjustment Surcharge (FPPAS) applies per GERC-approved formula. Tariff rates exclude electricity duty, tax on sale of electricity, customs duty, and other government levies. Payment of power factor penalty does not exempt the consumer from improving power factor.

**KEY TOPICS:**
Fixed charges computation, Contract Demand, 85% billing, excess demand penalty, broken period billing, Fuel and Power Purchase Adjustment Surcharge (FPPAS), electricity duty, tax on sale of electricity, power factor penalty, Electricity Act 2003

**TEXT:**
12. For computation of Fixed Charges, they will be computed on 85% of Contract Demand at Unity Power Factor or Actual whichever is higher on monthly basis.
13. Maximum Demand in a month means the highest value of average KVA delivered at the point of supply of the consumer during any consecutive 15/30 minutes in the said month.
14. Payment of penal charges for usage in excess of contract demand/load for any billing period does not entitle the consumer to draw in excess of contract demand/load as a matter of right. **PROVISION:**
Part-I, Rate 1 — Residential

**TITLE:**
LT Residential Tariff — Fixed Charge, Energy Charge, BPL Concession, Minimum Bill

**SUMMARY:**
This tariff applies to residential consumers at low/medium voltage for lights, fans, domestic appliances, and general household load. Fixed charges are Rs. 30/month (single phase) or Rs. 45/month (three phase). Energy charges are slab-based: 420 paise/unit for first 250 units and 470 paise/unit for remaining units. BPL consumers pay Rs. 5/month fixed charge and 250 paise/unit for first 50 units (balance at normal residential rate), subject to producing a BPL card at the zonal office. Minimum bill equals the applicable fixed charge.

**KEY TOPICS:**
Residential tariff, LT supply, fixed charge, energy charge, slab tariff, BPL consumers, Below Poverty Line, concessional tariff, single phase, three phase, minimum bill

**TEXT:**
PART-I — SUPPLY DELIVERED AT LOW OR MEDIUM VOLTAGE

The following tariffs are available for supply at low and medium voltage for contract demand up to 150 kVA.

1. RATE: Residential
This tariff is applicable to services for lights, fans and domestic appliances for heating, cooling, cooking, cleaning and refrigeration purposes, general load and motive power in residential premises.

1.1. FIXED CHARGE
(a) Single Phase Supply — Rs. 30 per month per installation
(b) Three Phase Supply — Rs. 45 per month per installation
For BPL household consumers*
Fixed Charges — Rs. 5 per month per installation

(i) First 250 units consumed per month — 420 Paisa per Unit
(ii) Remaining units consumed per month — 470 Paisa per Unit

(a) First 50 units — 250 Paise per Unit
(b) For remaining units — Rates as per Residential

Payment of fixed charges as specified in 1.1 above.

## CHUNK 06 — Rate 3: Commercial (Demand) (LT)

**DOCUMENT:**
Tariff Schedule for License Area of MPSEZ Utilities Ltd. (MUL) — FY 2026-27

**DOCUMENT TYPE:**
Tariff Schedule / Tariff Order (Annexure)

**HIERARCHY:**
Tariff Schedule → Part-I (LT/MV Supply) → Rate 3

**PROVISION:**
Part-I, Rate 3 — Commercial (Demand)

**TITLE:**
LT Commercial (Demand) Tariff — Fixed Charge, Energy Charge, Power Factor Adjustment, Minimum Bill

**SUMMARY:**
This tariff applies to LT commercial premises (not covered under other LT categories) with connected load of 6 kVA and above. Billing demand is the highest of: actual maximum demand, 85% of contract demand, or 6 kVA. Fixed charge is 75 paise/unit (within contract demand) and 125 paise/unit (excess demand), both computed at 100% load factor. Energy charge is a flat 370 paise/unit. Power factor rebates apply above 90% PF (0.15 paise/unit per 1% from 90–95%; 0.27 paise/unit per 1% above 95%). A penalty of 3.00 paise/unit per 1% applies below 90% PF. Minimum bill equals fixed charges.

**KEY TOPICS:**
Commercial Demand tariff, LT supply, connected load above 6 kVA, billing demand, contract demand, 85% demand, fixed charge, flat energy charge, power factor rebate, power factor penalty, minimum bill

**TEXT:**
3. RATE: Commercial (Demand)
This tariff is applicable to lights, fans and appliances for heating, cooling, cooking, cleaning and refrigeration purposes, general load and motive power in premises other than those requiring the power supply for the purposes not specified in any other LT categories, having connected load of 6 kVA and above.

3.1. FIXED CHARGE
A) For Billing Demand up to and including the Contract Demand
Computed on 85% of Contract Demand at u.p.f. or Actual maximum demand at monthly average power factor or six KVA at u.p.f. whichever is higher on monthly basis at 100% Load Factor — 75 Paisa per Unit

B) For Billing Demand in excess of the Contract Demand
Computed on billing demand in excess of Contract Demand on Monthly basis at 100% Load Factor — 125 Paisa per Unit

NOTE: The Billing Demand shall be highest of the following:
i. Actual Maximum Demand established during the month OR
ii. Eighty-five percent of the Contract Demand OR
iii. Six kVA

3.2 ENERGY CHARGE
A flat rate of 370 Paisa per Unit

3.3. POWER FACTOR ADJUSTMENT CHARGE
A) Where the average Power Factor during the Billing period exceeds 90%
- For each 1% improvement in the Power Factor from 90% to 95%: Rebate of 0.15 Paisa per Unit
- For each 1% improvement in the Power Factor above 95%: Rebate of 0.27 Paisa per Unit

B) Where the average Power Factor during the Billing period is below 90%
- For each 1% decrease in the Power Factor below 90%: Penalty of 3.00 Paisa per Unit

3.4. MINIMUM BILL
Payment of fixed charges as specified in 3.1 above.

**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:**
- General Condition Clause 11 (Contract Demand definition)
- General Condition Clause 12 (85% billing rule)
- General Condition Clause 13 (Maximum Demand definition)
- General Condition Clause 14 (penal charges for excess demand)
- General Condition Clause 18 (power factor penalty)
- Regulations under Electricity Act, 2003
