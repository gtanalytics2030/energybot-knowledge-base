## CHUNK 11

**DOCUMENT:**
Gujarat Electricity Regulatory Commission (Standards of Performance of Distribution Licensees) Regulations, 2023

**DOCUMENT TYPE:**

Chapter IV — Guaranteed Standards of Performance / Regulation 4.3

**PROVISION:**
Regulation 4.3(i) and 4.3(ii)

**TITLE:**
Guaranteed Standard 3 — Energisation of Temporary Connection

**SUMMARY:**
After receipt of full payment of the demand note and the applicant's test report, a temporary connection must be energised within 2 days (48 hours) in both urban and rural areas if it can be provided from the existing distribution network. If network modification is required, the standard time is 7 days for both urban and rural areas. Failure to meet these timelines triggers automatic compensation of Rs. 50 per day of default under Regulation 6.2.

**KEY TOPICS:**
Temporary Connection, Energisation, 48 Hours, 7 Days, Network Modification, Urban, Rural, Standard Time, Compensation

**TEXT:**
3. Energisation of Temporary Connection

4.3 After receipt of full payment of the demand note, Standard time for energizing the Temporary Connection is as under, provided that the necessary test report is received from the applicant;

(i) to be provided from existing distribution network is 2 (Two) days i.e. 48 Hours in Urban and Rural areas.

(ii) to be provided within 7 (Seven) days, in Urban as well as Rural area, in the cases where modification in network is required.

**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:**
- Regulation 4.1 (Demand Note)
- Regulation 6.2, Sl. No. 4(i) (Compensation: Rs. 50/day)

Regulations 4.5, 4.6

**TITLE:**
Guaranteed Standards 5 & 6 — Transfer of Service Connection (7 Days) and Change of Tariff (7 Days)

**SUMMARY:**
Regulation 4.5 requires the licensee to effect and intimate a transfer of service connection (name change or ownership change) to the consumer within 7 days of receipt of the complete application with all necessary documents and fee payment. Regulation 4.6 requires the licensee to dispose of an application for change of tariff class within 7 days. If the reclassification request is found invalid, the licensee must inform the consumer in writing with reasons within 10 days from the date of inspection. Compensation of Rs. 50/day applies for delays beyond the 7-day standard.

**KEY TOPICS:**
Transfer of Service Connection, Name Change, Ownership Change, Change of Tariff, Tariff Reclassification, 7-Day Standard, Inspection, Written Notice

**TEXT:**
5. Standard time for Transfer of Service Connection

4.5 After receipt of complete application along with necessary documents and payment of necessary fees, the transfer of service connection with respect to name change or ownership change shall be effected and intimated to the consumer within 7 (Seven) days.

6. Change of Tariff

4.6 After receipt of the application of change of tariff class by a Consumer, the licensee shall dispose of such application within maximum period of 7 (Seven) days. If the licensee does not find the request for reclassification valid, shall inform the consumer in writing, giving reason(s) for the same, within 10 (Ten) days from the date of inspection.

**TITLE:**
Guaranteed Standard 7 — Redressal of Billing Complaints

**SUMMARY:**
Regulation 4.7 prescribes standard times for resolving billing complaints: resolution on the same day if no additional information is required; within 10 days if additional information is needed; within 15 days if a site visit is required. These standards also apply to requests from disconnected consumers regarding due amounts, reconnection, and other charges. Regulation 4.8 provides that if bill revision becomes necessary, the due date for payment is reckoned from the revised bill date, which also governs additional charges for delayed payment and disconnection.

**KEY TOPICS:**

**TEXT:**

4.7 Standard time, from receipt of complaint, for redressal of a complaint about electricity bills regarding arithmetical errors or non-receipt or inadequate time for payment and addressing the request of disconnected consumer for details about due amount, reconnection and other charges, is as under;

| Sr. No. | Nature of complaint | Time limit |
|---------|---------------------|------------|
| 1 | Resolution of Billing mistake if no additional information is required | On the Same day |
| 2 | Resolution of Billing mistake if additional information is required | Within 10 (Ten) days |
| 3 | Resolution of Billing mistake if site visit is required | Within 15 (Fifteen) days |

4.8 On redressal of complaint if revision of bill becomes necessary, the due date for payment of bill would be reckoned from the date of the revised bill. For purpose of calculating additional charges for delayed payment or for disconnection of supply, the date of revised bill shall be considered.

## CHUNK 15

**DOCUMENT:**
Gujarat Electricity Regulatory Commission (Standards of Performance of Distribution Licensees) Regulations, 2023

**DOCUMENT TYPE:**
Regulation

**HIERARCHY:**
Chapter IV — Guaranteed Standards of Performance / Regulations 4.9, 4.10

**PROVISION:**
Regulations 4.9(a), 4.9(b), 4.10

**TITLE:**
Guaranteed Standard 8 — Redressal of Meter/Metering System Complaints

**SUMMARY:**
The licensee must inspect/check/test a meter within 7 working days in urban areas and 15 working days in rural areas of receiving a complaint. If a meter is found defective (burnt, stolen, stuck, running fast/slow, etc.), it must be replaced within 24 hours in urban areas and 72 hours in rural areas from the time of inspection. Non-availability of meter is not a valid reason to delay supply restoration if the meter is burnt. The test-checking report must be provided to the consumer within 7 days of meter removal/replacement; consumers may opt to seal the meter before removal and be present during laboratory testing.

**KEY TOPICS:**
Meter Complaint, Defective Meter, Burnt Meter, Stolen Meter, Meter Replacement, 7 Working Days, 24 Hours Urban, 72 Hours Rural, Test Report, Consumer Rights, Meter Sealing

**TEXT:**
8. Redressal of Complaints related to Meter/Metering System

4.9 The licensee shall regularly inspect, check and test the meters. However, the periodicity of such inspection shall not be less than that provided in the Central Electricity Authority (Installation and Operation of Meters) Regulations, 2006, as amended from time to time.

a) Licensee shall inspect, check or test the meter within 7 (Seven) working days of receiving a complaint in urban area and within 15 (Fifteen) working days of receiving a complaint in rural area.

b) If during inspection, checking and testing, or on a complaint made by the consumer any meter or metering system, is found to be defective (e.g. burnt, stolen, stuck up, running slow, fast, creeping or improperly recording or not functioning according to the specifications etc.), the licensee shall inform the consumer and replace the meter within 24 hours in urban area and 72 hours in rural area from the time of inspection, checking/testing and restore the supply.

Further, non-availability of meter shall not be reason for delay in restoration of supply in case of meter found burnt.

4.10 In all such cases of the meter being removed/replaced, the test checking report shall be supplied to the consumer, if the consumer is present or otherwise within 7 (Seven) days of removal/replacement of meter. In case meter is to be further tested in licensee's laboratory, its test report shall also be supplied to the consumer. Consumer can opt to seal the meter before its removal from site and also opt to be present during testing on the date to be notified by the licensee.

**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:**
- CEA (Installation and Operation of Meters) Regulations, 2006
- Regulation 6.2, Sl. No. 9 (Compensation: Rs. 25/day for LT max Rs. 1500, Rs. 250/day for HT max Rs. 2500; from 9th working day urban, 19th working day rural)

**TITLE:**
Guaranteed Standard 9 — Standard Time for Reconnection of Power Supply

**SUMMARY:**
After receipt of all dues (including reconnection charges and security deposit), reconnection must be effected within specified periods. For disconnection periods not exceeding 6 months: 6 working hours if no service line/meter installation is required; 1 working day if service line/meter installation is required. For disconnection periods exceeding 6 months: 48 working hours if no work is required; 7 working days if work is required. Where disconnection exceeds 6 months, the consumer must also submit a test report from a Licensed Electrical Contractor. Where the agreement has been terminated, the consumer must apply afresh under the GERC Electricity Supply Code 2015.

|---------|--------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|

**TITLE:**
Guaranteed Standard 10 — Restoration of Power Supply: Maximum Interruptions and Fault-wise Standard Times

**SUMMARY:**
The licensee must not allow more than 2 interruptions per day to a consumer. For fault restoration, standard times from receipt of complaint are: LT fuse/MCB/loose connection — 2 hours urban, 6 hours rural; HT fuse — 3 hours urban, 6 hours rural; HT and LT line fault — 4 hours urban, 12 hours rural; distribution transformer failure — 6 hours urban, 24 hours rural; underground cable fault — temporary restoration in 6 hours urban/12 hours rural, permanent rectification in 12 hours urban/24 hours rural. Essential services (water supply, hospitals, government services) must be attended immediately. Complaint data must be maintained at the Complaint Centre per Appendix-A and B.

**TITLE:**
Guaranteed Standard — Period of Scheduled Outages

**SUMMARY:**
The anticipated period of interruption due to a scheduled outage must be notified at least 48 hours in advance through public notice (local newspapers, radio/TV, or website). Scheduled outages must not exceed 10 hours in a day and supply must normally be restored by 6 pm. Supply to essential services must be maintained through prior consultation with those service providers. The licensee must display feeder-wise outage data and efforts to minimise outages on its website. Failure triggers compensation of Rs. 50/hour per consumer for first 2 hours and Rs. 100/hour thereafter.

**KEY TOPICS:**
Scheduled Outage, 48 Hours Advance Notice, 10 Hours Maximum, Restoration by 6 PM, Essential Services, Feeder-wise Outage, Website Disclosure, Compensation

**TEXT:**
Period of Schedule Outages

4.16 The anticipated period of interruption due to scheduled outage shall be notified well in advance (a minimum of 48 (Forty eight) hours in advance) by public notice through local newspapers or local radio/TV stations, etc. and placing the same on its website. Such outages shall not exceed 10 (Ten) hours in a day. The licensee shall ensure that supply is normally restored by 6 pm. The supply to essential services shall be maintained through prior consultation with providers of such services. The Licensee shall arrange to display feeder wise outage data and efforts made for minimising outages on its website.

**DOCUMENT:**
Gujarat Electricity Regulatory Commission (Standards of Performance of Distribution Licensees) Regulations, 2023

**DOCUMENT TYPE:**
Regulation

**HIERARCHY:**
Chapter V — Overall Standards of Performance / Regulations 5.1–5.2

**PROVISION:**
Regulations 5.1, 5.2 (including full formulae for SAIFI, SAIDI, CAIDI, MAIFI)

**TITLE:**
Overall Standards — Reliability Indices: SAIFI, SAIDI, CAIDI, MAIFI — Definitions, Formulae, and Exclusions

**SUMMARY:**
The distribution licensee must calculate the reliability of its distribution system using four IEEE Standard 1366-2003 indices: SAIFI, SAIDI, CAIDI, and MAIFI. Indices are computed for the Discom as a whole by aggregating all 11KV/33KV feeders (excluding agricultural feeders) monthly. Outages of 5 minutes or less are "momentary"; more than 5 minutes are "sustained." Excluded from calculations: scheduled outages, grid failures, and force majeure events (Clause 8.4). CAIDI must also be computed separately for HT Consumers. Full formulae with all variables are prescribed.

**KEY TOPICS:**
Reliability Indices, SAIFI, SAIDI, CAIDI, MAIFI, IEEE 1366-2003, Sustained Interruption, Momentary Interruption, 5-Minute Threshold, Agricultural Feeder, HT Consumer, Scheduled Outage Exclusion, Force Majeure Exclusion

**TEXT:**
CHAPTER V — OVERALL STANDARDS OF PERFORMANCES

1. Reliability of the System

5.1 The Distribution Licensee shall calculate the reliability of its distribution system on the basis of number and duration of interruptions in a reporting period, using following indices as prescribed by the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) Standard 1366-2003 as per the formulae described below in Clause 5.2;
i. System Average Interruption Frequency Index (SAIFI);
ii. System Average Interruption Duration Index (SAIDI);
iii. Customer Average Interruption Duration Index (CAIDI); and
iv. Momentary Average Interruption Frequency Index (MAIFI);

Provided that while calculating the above indices, the following interruptions shall not be taken into account;
i. Scheduled outages;
ii. Outages due to failure of the grid;
iii. Outages due to the reasons described in Clause 8.4 below.

5.2 The Indices shall be computed for the Discom as a whole by stacking, for each month all the 11KV/33KV feeders in the supply area, excluding those serving predominantly agricultural loads, and then aggregating the number and duration of all interruptions in that month for each feeder. The outage time less than or equal to 5 (Five) minutes shall be considered as momentary interruption and outage time higher than 5 (Five) minutes shall be considered as sustained interruption.

**I. System Average Interruption Frequency Index (SAIFI)**
SAIFI indicates how often the average customer experiences a sustained interruption over a period of one month.
SAIFI = (Total Number of Interrupted Customers for Sustained Interruption events during the Reporting Period) ÷ (Total Number of Customers served)
= ∑(Ai × Ni) / Nt

**II. System Average Interruption Duration Index (SAIDI)**
SAIDI indicates total duration of interruption for the average customer during a period of one month.
SAIDI = Cumulative Customer Interruption Duration / Total Number of Customers served
= ∑(Ri × Ni) / Nt

Where:
- i = an interruption event
- Ai = Number of Sustained Interruptions during the Reporting Period
- Ri = Restoration time for each Interruption Event
- Ni = Number of Interrupted Customers for Sustained Interruption event during the Reporting Period
- Nt = Total number of Customers Served for the Areas
- CMI = Cumulative Customer Interruption Duration = ∑ Ri Ni

Provided that while calculating the above indices, the following types of interruptions shall not be taken into account:
a. Momentary outages of a duration not more than Five (5) minutes;
b. Outages due to the failure of the grid;
c. Outages due to the reasons described in Clause 8.4 below.

**III. Customer Average Interruption Duration Index (CAIDI)**
CAIDI indicates the average time required to restore service.
CAIDI = (Cumulative Customer Interruption Durations) ÷ (Total Number of Interrupted Customers for Sustained Interruption events during the Reporting Period)
= ∑(Ri × Ni) / ∑(Ai × Ni) = SAIDI / SAIFI

Provided that CAIDI should also be computed separately only for HT Consumers based on the metering data of such Consumers.

**IV. Momentary Average Interruption Frequency Index (MAIFI)**
MAIFI indicates total number of momentary interruptions for the average customer during a period of one month.
MAIFI = Cumulative Momentary Customer Interruptions / Total Number of Customers served for the Areas
= ∑(Imi × Nmi) / Nt

Where:
- mi = a momentary interruption event
- Imi = Total Number of Momentary Interruption Events
- Nmi = Total Number of Consumers affected due to Momentary Interruption Events during the Reporting Period
- Nt = Total Number of Customers Served for the Areas

The Distribution Licensee shall maintain data on the reliability indices specified above for each zone/circle/division/sub-division on a monthly basis as well as for Urban and Rural area separately.

The Licensee shall compute the value of these indices separately for feeders serving predominantly agricultural loads. The methodology for computation of indices shall remain the same as in the case of other feeders.

**REFERENCED PROVISIONS:**
- IEEE Standard 1366-2003
- Regulation 5.3 (data capture automation)
- Regulation 5.4 (target setting by Commission)
- Clause 8.4 (force majeure exemptions)
- Appendix D, SoP 010–013 (reporting formats for SAIFI, SAIDI, CAIDI, MAIFI)
