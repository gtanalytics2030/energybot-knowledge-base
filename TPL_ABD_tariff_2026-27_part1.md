## CHUNK 2 — General Conditions (Clauses 1–10)

DOCUMENT:
Tariff Schedule for Ahmedabad–Gandhinagar License Area of Torrent Power Limited – Ahmedabad

DOCUMENT TYPE:

General Conditions — Clauses 1 to 10

PROVISION:
General Conditions, Clauses 1–10

TITLE:
General Conditions — Applicability, Billing Basis, Definitions of Contract Demand and Maximum Demand

SUMMARY:
These general conditions govern all tariff categories in the TPL-D (Ahmedabad–Gandhinagar) schedule. Key provisions include: tariffs apply to one point of supply; charges are on a monthly basis (adjusted for billing period); contracted load must match the purpose of supply; billing of fixed charges is in multiples of 0.5 HP/kW; "Connected Load" means maximum load during billing period; "Contract Demand" means maximum kW TPL-D undertakes to supply; "Maximum Demand" is highest average kW in any consecutive 15/30 minutes in the month. GERC (Licensee's power to recover expenditure) Regulations, 2005 (except Meter Charges) continue to apply.

KEY TOPICS:
General Conditions, Applicability, One Point of Supply, Monthly Charges, Billing Period, Connected Load, Contract Demand, Maximum Demand, kW, BHP, Conversion Factor, GERC Regulations 2005

TEXT:
GENERAL CONDITIONS

1. This tariff schedule is applicable to all the consumers of TPL-D in Ahmedabad and Gandhinagar area.

2. All these tariffs for power supply are applicable to only one point of supply.

3. Except in cases where the supply is used for purposes for which a lower tariff is provided in the tariff schedule, the power supplied to any consumer shall be utilized only for the purpose for which supply is taken and as provided for in the tariff.

4. The charges specified in the tariff are on monthly basis. TPL-D shall adjust the rates according to billing period applicable to consumer.

5. The various provisions of the GERC (Licensee's power to recover expenditure incurred in providing supply and other miscellaneous charges) Regulations, 2005, except Meter Charges, will continue to apply.

6. Conversion of Ratings of electrical appliances and equipments from kilowatt to B.H.P. or vice versa will be done, when necessary, at the rate of 0.746 kilowatt equal to 1 B.H.P.

7. The billing of fixed charges based on contracted load or maximum demand shall be done in multiples of 0.5 (one half) Horse Power or kilowatt (HP or kW) as the case may be. The fraction of less than 0.5 shall be rounded to next 0.5. The billing of energy charges will be done on complete one kilo-watt-hour (kWh).

8. The Connected Load for the purpose of billing will be taken as the maximum load connected during the billing period.

9. Contract Demand shall mean the maximum kW for the supply of which TPL-D undertakes to provide facilities to the consumer from time to time.

10. Maximum Demand in a month means the highest value of average kW as the case may be, delivered at the point of supply of the consumer during any consecutive 15/30 minutes in the said month.

REFERENCED PROVISIONS:
GERC (Licensee's power to recover expenditure incurred in providing supply and other miscellaneous charges) Regulations, 2005

## CHUNK 4 — Rate: RGP (Residential / Common Services LT Tariff)

DOCUMENT TYPE:
Tariff Schedule

PROVISION:
Rate: RGP — Clauses 1, 1.1, 1.2

TITLE:
Rate: RGP — Residential and Common Services (Low/Medium Voltage)

SUMMARY:
Rate RGP is a Low/Medium Voltage tariff (Part-I) applicable to residential consumers and common service installations (elevators, water pumps, passage lighting in residential premises, local authority pumping stations) with connected load up to 15 kW. It provides a concessional tariff for Below Poverty Line (BPL) households. Non-BPL fixed charges are Rs. 25/month (single phase) and Rs. 65/month (three phase). Energy charges are slab-based: 320 paise/unit (first 50 units), 395 paise/unit (next 150 units), 500 paise/unit (remaining). BPL consumers pay Rs. 5/month fixed charge and 150 paise/unit for first 50 units; beyond 50 units, RGP rates apply. BPL benefit requires production of the BPL card at the zonal office; the concessional rate applies only to first 50 units per month.

KEY TOPICS:
RGP, Residential Tariff, BPL Consumer, Below Poverty Line, Common Services, Elevators, Water Pumping, Local Authorities, Fixed Charge, Energy Charge, Slab Rate, LT Consumer, Single Phase, Three Phase, Concessional Tariff

TEXT:
PART-I
SUPPLY DELIVERED AT LOW OR MEDIUM VOLTAGE
(230 VOLTS- SINGLE PHASE, 400 VOLTS- THREE PHASE, 50 HERTZ)

The following tariffs are available for supply at Low and Medium voltage for Contract demand up to 150 kW.

1. RATE: RGP

This tariff is applicable to supply of electricity for:
i. residential purpose, and
ii. Installations having connected load up to and including 15 kW for common services like elevators, water pumping systems, passage lighting in residential premises and pumping stations run by local authorities.

1.1. FIXED CHARGE

For Other than BPL consumers
(a) Single Phase Supply — Rs. 25 per month per installation
(b) Three Phase Supply — Rs. 65 per month per installation

For BPL household consumers*
(a) Fixed Charges — Rs. 5 per month per installation

1.2. ENERGY CHARGE

For Other than BPL consumers
(a) First 50 units consumed per month — 320 Paise per Unit
(b) For the next 150 units consumed per month — 395 Paise per Unit
(c) Remaining units consumed per month — 500 Paise per Unit

For BPL household consumers*
(a) First 50 units consumed per month — 150 Paise per Unit
(b) For remaining units consumed per month — Rate as per RGP

* The consumer who wants to avail the benefit of the above tariff has to produce a copy of the Card issued by the authority concerned at the zonal office of the Distribution Licensee. The concessional tariff is only for 50 units per month.

## CHUNK 8 — Rate: LTMD-1 (Common Services above 15 kW — Residential)

DOCUMENT:
Tariff Schedule for Ahmedabad–Gandhinagar License Area of Torrent Power Limited – Ahmedabad

DOCUMENT TYPE:
Tariff Schedule

HIERARCHY:
Part-I (LT/MV Supply) > Rate: LTMD-1

PROVISION:
Rate: LTMD-1 — Clauses 5, 5.1, 5.2, 5.3

TITLE:
Rate: LTMD-1 — Common Services above 15 kW for Residential Purpose (LT Maximum Demand Tariff)

SUMMARY:
Rate LTMD-1 applies to LT installations above 15 kW of connected load used for common services (elevators, water pumping, passage lighting) in residential premises and pumping stations run by local authorities. Billing demand is the highest of: actual maximum demand, 85% of contract demand, or 6 kW. Fixed charges are slab-based: Rs. 150/kW (first 50 kW), Rs. 185/kW (next 30 kW), Rs. 245/kW (rest) for demand within contract demand; Rs. 350/kW for excess demand. Energy charges: 465 paise/unit (billing demand up to 50 kW); 480 paise/unit (above 50 kW). Power factor rebate and penalty apply: rebate of 0.15 paise/unit per 1% improvement from 90–95%, 0.27 paise/unit above 95%; penalty of 3 paise/unit per 1% drop below 90%.

KEY TOPICS:
LTMD-1, LT Maximum Demand, Common Services, Residential, Elevators, Water Pumping, Billing Demand, Contract Demand, Fixed Charge, Energy Charge, Power Factor Adjustment, Rebate, Penalty, Local Authorities

TEXT:
5. Rate: LTMD-1

Applicable for supply of electricity to installations above 15 kW of connected load used for common services like elevators, water pumping systems and passage lighting for residential purpose and pumping stations run by local authorities.

5.1. FIXED CHARGE

1. For Billing Demand up to and including Contract Demand
(a) First 50 kW of Billing Demand per month — Rs. 150 per kW
(b) Next 30 kW of Billing Demand per month — Rs. 185 per kW
(c) Rest of Billing Demand per month — Rs. 245 per kW

2. For Billing Demand in excess of the Contract Demand
Fixed Charge per kW of Billing Demand per month — Rs. 350 Per kW

Note: The Billing Demand will be taken as under:
i. The Maximum Demand recorded during the month OR
ii. 85% of the Contract Demand OR
iii. 6 kW
Whichever is the highest.

5.2. ENERGY CHARGE
(a) For Billing Demand up to and including 50 kW — 465 Paise per unit
(b) For Billing Demand above 50 kW — 480 Paise per unit

5.3. POWER FACTOR ADJUSTMENT CHARGE

A. Where the average Power Factor during the Billing period exceeds 90%
For each 1% improvement in the Power Factor from 90% to 95% — Rebate of 0.15 Paise per Unit
For each 1% improvement in the Power Factor above 95% — Rebate of 0.27 Paise per Unit

B. Where the average Power Factor during the Billing period is below 90%
For each 1% decrease in the Power Factor below 90% — Penalty of 3.00 Paise per Unit

REFERENCED PROVISIONS:
Part-I General Conditions; Rate: RGP (for residential common services up to 15 kW, see Clause 1)
