TITLE:
Rate: HTMD-Metro Traction — Metro Rail Traction Supply (HT/EHT, 100 kW and above)

SUMMARY:
Rate HTMD-Metro Traction applies to supply of energy for metro rail traction purposes, contracting for 100 kW and above. Billing demand: highest of actual maximum demand, 85% of contract demand, or 100 kW. Fixed charges within contract demand: Rs. 335/kW per month; excess: Rs. 385/kW. Energy charge: flat rate 355 paise/unit — among the lowest energy charges in the HT schedule, reflecting the public transport nature of metro services. TOU surcharge: 60 paise/unit during 0600–0800 hrs and 1700–2300 hrs. Solar Hour Rebate: 30 paise/kWh during 1100–1500 hrs (time-segment meter required). Standard power factor rebate/penalty applies.

## CHUNK 17 — Rate: NTCT (Night Time Concessional Tariff)

PROVISION:
Rate: NTCT — Clauses 14, 14.1, 14.2, 14.3 and Notes 1–7

TITLE:
Rate: NTCT — Night Time Concessional Tariff (Exclusive Night-Hour HT Consumers)

SUMMARY:
Rate NTCT is an optional HT concessional tariff for consumers who use electricity EXCLUSIVELY during night hours (2200 hrs to 0600 hrs next day). Fixed charges are 30% of the demand charges applicable under the consumer's relevant HT tariff category. Energy charge: flat 350 paise/unit. Standard power factor rebate/penalty applies. Conditions: up to 15% of contracted demand and up to 10% of total units consumed may be drawn outside night hours without penalty. Breaching the 15% demand condition triggers HTMD-1 demand charges for that month; breaching the 10% energy condition triggers HTMD-1 energy charges for the entire month's consumption. Both conditions violated simultaneously means both HTMD-1 demand and energy charges apply. Consumers may switch between NTCT and HTMD-1 up to four times per calendar year on 15 days' advance written notice before billing period commencement.

KEY TOPICS:
NTCT, Night Time Concessional Tariff, Night Hours, Exclusive Night Use, Off-Peak, Demand Charge, Energy Charge, HTMD-1 Penalty Trigger, Option to Switch, Advance Notice, Calendar Year, Penal Billing, HT Consumer

⭐ Billing Demand Calculation Rules — All Demand-Based Tariff Categories

SUMMARY:
This anchor chunk consolidates the billing demand computation rules across all demand-based tariff categories in the TPL-D schedule. The formula is consistent: billing demand = highest of (i) actual recorded maximum demand, (ii) a percentage of contract demand, or (iii) a minimum floor demand. The following applies uniformly across all demand-based tariff categories in this schedule:

Billing Demand = HIGHEST of:
  (i)   Actual Maximum Demand recorded during the month
  (ii)  Specified percentage × Contract Demand
  (iii) Minimum floor demand
