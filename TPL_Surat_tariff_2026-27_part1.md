## CHUNK GC-01

DOCUMENT:
Tariff Schedule for Surat License Area of Torrent Power Limited – Surat (Effective 1st April 2026)

DOCUMENT TYPE:
Tariff Order / Tariff Schedule (Annexure)

HIERARCHY:
Annexure: Tariff Schedule > General Conditions > Items 1–5

PROVISION:
General Conditions, Items 1–5

TITLE:
General Applicability, Single Point of Supply, Purpose of Use, Monthly Billing Basis, GERC Miscellaneous Charges Regulations

SUMMARY:
This chunk establishes the foundational conditions governing the TPL-Surat tariff schedule. The tariff applies to all TPL consumers in the Surat area, is limited to a single point of supply, and power must be used only for the purpose for which it was sanctioned. Charges are on a monthly basis with adjustments for actual billing periods. The GERC (Licensee's power to recover expenditure incurred in providing supply and other miscellaneous charges) Regulations continue to apply, except Meter Charges.

KEY TOPICS:
- Tariff applicability (Surat area, all consumers)
- Single point of supply restriction
- Purpose of use restriction
- Monthly billing basis
- GERC Miscellaneous Charges Regulations
- Meter Charges exclusion

TEXT:
GENERAL CONDITIONS

1. The tariff schedule is applicable to all the consumers of TPL in Surat area.

2. All these tariffs for power supply are applicable to only one point of supply.

3. Except in cases where the supply is used for purposes for which a lower tariff is provided in the tariff schedule, the power supplied to any consumer shall be utilized only for the purpose for which supply is taken and as provided for in the tariff.

4. The charges specified in the tariff are on monthly basis, TPL shall adjust the rates according to billing period applicable to consumer.

5. The various provisions of the GERC (Licensee's power to recover expenditure incurred in providing supply and other miscellaneous charges) Regulations, except Meter Charges, will continue to apply.

REFERENCED PROVISIONS:
- GERC (Licensee's power to recover expenditure incurred in providing supply and other miscellaneous charges) Regulations

## CHUNK GC-02

DOCUMENT:
Tariff Schedule for Surat License Area of Torrent Power Limited – Surat (Effective 1st April 2026)

HIERARCHY:

PROVISION:
General Conditions, Items 6–9

TITLE:
Unit Conversion, Billing of Fixed and Energy Charges, Contract Demand Definition, Maximum Demand Definition

SUMMARY:
These conditions specify the technical and billing rules for TPL-Surat consumers. kW-to-BHP conversion is at 0.746 kW = 1 BHP. Fixed charges are billed in multiples of 0.5 HP/kW/kVA (fractions rounded up), while energy charges are billed in whole kWh/kVAh/kVARh units. Contract Demand is defined as the maximum kW or kVA TPL agrees to supply. Maximum Demand in a month is the highest average kW recorded in any consecutive 15 or 30 minutes in that month.

KEY TOPICS:
- kW to BHP conversion rate (0.746 kW = 1 BHP)
- Fixed charges billing rounding rule (multiples of 0.5)
- Energy charges billing unit (complete 1 kWh/kVAh/kVARh)
- Contract Demand definition
- Maximum Demand definition
- 15/30 minute demand interval

TEXT:
6. Conversion of Ratings of electrical appliances and equipment from kilowatt to B.H.P. or vice versa will be done, when necessary, at the rate of 0.746 kilowatt equal to 1 B.H.P.

7. The billing of fixed charges based on contracted load or maximum demand shall be done in multiples of 0.5 (one half) Horsepower or kilo watt (HP, kW, kVA) as the case may be. The fraction of less than 0.5 shall be rounded to next 0.5. The billing of energy charges will be done on complete 1.0 (one) kilo-watt-hour (kWh) or kilo-volt-ampere-hour (kVAh) or kilo-volt-ampere-reactive-hour (kVARh), as the case may be.

8. Contract Demand shall mean the maximum kW or kVA for the supply of which TPL undertakes to provide facilities to the consumer from time to time.

9. Maximum Demand in a month means the highest value of average kW as the case may be, delivered at the point of supply of the consumer during any consecutive 15/30 minutes in the said month.

REFERENCED PROVISIONS:
- General Condition Item 4 (monthly billing basis)

PROVISION:
General Conditions, Items 10–13

TITLE:
Metering, Excess Demand Penalty, Broken Period Billing, Time of Use (ToU) and Solar Hours Definition

SUMMARY:
TPL may install kWh and kVARh meters to measure power factor and reactive energy. Paying excess demand penalty does not grant the right to habitually draw beyond the contract demand/load. Fixed, minimum, and demand charges are not adjusted for broken billing periods due to connection or disconnection. Time of Use (ToU) peak hours are defined as 0600–0800 hrs and 1700–2300 hrs; Solar Hours rebate applies from 1100–1500 hrs.

KEY TOPICS:
- kWh and kVARh metering
- Excess contract demand — no right to draw
- Broken period billing — no charge adjustment
- Time of Use (ToU) charges
- Peak Hours: 0600–0800 hrs and 1700–2300 hrs
- Solar Hours: 1100–1500 hrs
- Solar Hours rebate

TEXT:
10. TPL may install kWh and kVARh meter for ascertaining power factor, reactive units and kWh units.

11. Payment of penal charges for usage in excess of contract demand/load for any billing period does not entitle the consumer to draw in excess of contract demand/load as a matter of right.

12. The fixed charges, minimum charges, demand charges and the slabs of consumption of energy for energy charges mentioned shall not be subject to any adjustment on account of existence of any broken period within billing period arising from consumer supply being connected or disconnected any time within the duration of billing period for any reason.

13. ToU charges wherever applicable unless otherwise notified shall be levied for the energy consumption during the period between 0600 hours and 0800 hours; and between 1700 hours and 2300 hours, termed as PEAK HOURS. Solar hours rebate wherever applicable will be given for the energy consumption during the period between 1100 hours and 1500 hours, termed as 'SOLAR HOURS'.

REFERENCED PROVISIONS:
- Rate HTMD-I (Para 8.3), Rate HTMD-II (Para 9.3), Rate HTMD-III (Para 10.3), Rate HTMD-Metro (Para 11.3) — ToU charges applicable

PROVISION:
General Conditions, Items 14–15

TITLE:
Fuel and Power Purchase Adjustment Surcharge (FPPAS), Delayed Payment Charges

SUMMARY:
FPPAS charges are applicable as per the formula approved by GERC from time to time. No delayed payment charges are levied if the bill is paid by the due date. If paid after the due date, delayed payment charges are levied at 15% per annum, computed on a daily basis on the outstanding bill amount from the due date until the date of actual payment.

KEY TOPICS:
- Fuel and Power Purchase Adjustment Surcharge (FPPAS)
- GERC-approved FPPAS formula
- Delayed payment charges
- 15% per annum (daily basis)
- Due date payment
- Outstanding bill

TEXT:
14. Fuel and Power Purchase Adjustment Surcharge (FPPAS) charges shall be applicable in accordance with the formula approved by the Gujarat Electricity Regulatory Commission from time to time.

15. Delayed Payment Charges:
    a. No delayed payment charges will be levied if the bill is paid on or before due date indicated in the bill.
    b. Delayed payment charges, if the bill is paid after due date, will be levied at the rate of 15% per annum (computed on daily basis) on the outstanding bill from the due date till the date of payment.

REFERENCED PROVISIONS:
- GERC (approved formula for FPPAS)

PROVISION:
General Condition, Item 18

TITLE:
Green Power Tariff — Eligibility, Rate, and Opt-In Procedure

SUMMARY:
A Green Power Tariff of Rs. 0.75/kWh is levied over and above the normal tariff of the respective consumer category for consumers opting to meet their energy demand from Renewable Energy (RE). All consumers — Extra High Voltage, High Voltage, and Low Voltage — are eligible. The option must be exercised by giving a written billing cycle notice to the Distribution Licensee before the commencement of the billing period.

KEY TOPICS:
- Green Power Tariff
- Renewable Energy (RE) power
- Rate: Rs. 0.75/kWh (over and above normal tariff)
- Eligible consumers: EHV, HV, LV (all categories)
- Opt-in procedure — written billing cycle notice
- Distribution Licensee

TEXT:
18. Green Power Tariff
    o Green Power Tariff of Rs 0.75/kWh, which is over and above the normal tariff of the respective category as per Tariff Order, be levied to the consumers opting for meeting their demand of green energy.
    o All consumers (Extra High Voltage, High Voltage and Low Voltage) shall be eligible for opting RE power on payment of Green Power Tariff.
    o This option can be exercised by consumer giving Billing Cycle notice to the Distribution Licensee in writing before commencement of billing period.

REFERENCED PROVISIONS:
- Respective category tariff rates (Part-I LT tariffs, Part-II HT tariffs, Part-III EHT tariffs)
- Distribution Licensee obligations (GERC Supply Code / Electricity Act, 2003)
