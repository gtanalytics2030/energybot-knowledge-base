TITLE:
Rate HTMD-I — HT General Industrial/Commercial Tariff (3.3 kV and above, 100 kVA and above): Demand Charges, Energy Charges, ToU Charges, Power Factor, Solar Hour Rebate, EHV Rebate

SUMMARY:
HTMD-I applies to consumers supplied at 3.3 kV and above with contract demand of 100 kVA and above, for purposes other than local authority pumping stations. Demand charges are Rs. 170/kVA (first 500 kVA) and Rs. 285/kVA (next 500 kVA) within contract demand; Rs. 395/kVA for excess. Billing demand is highest of actual MD, 85% of CD, or 100 kVA. Energy charges are 480 Paise/unit (first 400 units per kVA billing demand) and 470 Paise/unit for remaining units. ToU surcharge is 65 Paise/unit (billing demand up to 500 kVA) or 100 Paise/unit (above 500 kVA) during peak hours (0600–0800 hrs and 1700–2300 hrs). Power factor adjustment charges apply at 1%/1% drop below 90% down to 85%, and 2%/1% drop below 85%. Rebate of 1%/1% rise above 95% PF. Solar hour rebate is 30 Paise/kWh (1100–1500 hrs). EHV supply rebate: 0.5% at 33/66 kV; 1.0% at 132 kV and above — on energy charges.

- Solar Hour Rebate: 30 Paise/kWh (1100–1500 hrs)
- EHV Rebate: 0.5% (33/66 kV), 1.0% (132 kV and above)

Part-II, Rate 12.0 through 12.3 (including Notes 1–7) — Rate: NTCT (Night Time Concessional Tariff)

TITLE:
Rate NTCT — Night Time Concessional Tariff (HT): Conditions, Fixed Charges, Energy Charges, Power Factor, Compliance Rules, and Tariff Switching

SUMMARY:
NTCT is an optional HT concessional tariff for consumers who use electricity EXCLUSIVELY during night hours (2200–0600 hrs), subject to acceptable switching arrangements. Fixed charges are 30% of the demand charges under the relevant HTMD tariff category. Energy charges are a flat 340 Paise/unit. Power factor provisions are identical in structure to HTMD categories (penalty 1%/drop below 90%–85%; 2%/drop below 85%; rebate 1%/rise above 95%). Compliance conditions are strict: 15% of contracted demand may be drawn beyond night hours; 10% of total units may be consumed beyond night hours. Breach of demand condition triggers billing at HTMD demand charge rates (para 8.1); breach of energy condition triggers HTMD energy charge rates (para 8.2); breach of both triggers both. Switching between HTMD and NTCT is allowed up to four times per calendar year with 15 days' advance written notice before the billing period.

KEY TOPICS:
- NTCT tariff (Night Time Concessional Tariff)
- Exclusive night-time use (2200–0600 hrs)
- Switching arrangement requirement
- Fixed charges: 30% of HTMD demand charges
- Energy charges: 340 Paise/unit (flat)
- Power Factor Adjustment (penalty and rebate)
- 15% demand tolerance beyond night hours
- 10% energy tolerance beyond night hours
- Penalty for non-compliance: HTMD rates (para 8.1/8.2)
- Tariff switch: 4 times/year, 15 days' advance notice
- Optional tariff (in place of HTMD)
