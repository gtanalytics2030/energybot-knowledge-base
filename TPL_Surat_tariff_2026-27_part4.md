SUMMARY:
Billing demand is the basis for demand charge computation in all demand-based tariff categories (LTMD, HTMD-I, HTMD-II, HTMD-III, HTMD-Metro). The definition is consistent across HT categories but differs slightly for LTMD. This chunk consolidates all billing demand definitions for easy retrieval.

TEXT:
BILLING DEMAND DEFINITIONS — CONSOLIDATED REFERENCE

FOR RATE LTMD (LT Maximum Demand, Para 4.1):
Billing demand during the month shall be the HIGHEST of the following:
  (i) Maximum demand recorded during the month
  (ii) 85% of the contract demand
  (iii) 6 kVA
[Minimum floor: 6 kVA]

FOR RATE HTMD-I (Para 8.1), HTMD-II (Para 9.1), HTMD-III (Para 10.1), HTMD-Metro (Para 11.1):
Billing demand shall be the HIGHEST of the following:
  (i) Actual maximum demand established during the month
  (ii) 85 percent of the Contract Demand
  (iii) 100 kVA
[Minimum floor: 100 kVA for all HT categories]

KEY POINT — 85% Ratchet:
Even if actual consumption is low, billing demand cannot fall below 85% of the contracted demand. This ensures TPL recovers minimum costs for maintaining capacity. Consumers must size their contract demand carefully to avoid over-contracting.

KEY POINT — Excess Demand:
Demand in excess of contract demand is billed at penal rates. However, per General Condition Item 11, paying the penal charge does not entitle the consumer to draw excess demand as a matter of right.
